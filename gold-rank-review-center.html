28. Gold Au crystallizes in a cube closed packed structure (face centered cube) and has a density of 19.3 g/cm3. Calculate the atomic radius of the gold.​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the volume of the unit cell by using dimensional analysis

In a face-centered cubic crystal structure, there are 4 atoms in a unit cell. The molar mass of gold is 196.97 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} V & = \text{4 atoms} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{6.0221 \times 10^{23} \: \text{atoms}} \times \frac{\text{196.97 g}}{\text{1 mol}} \times \frac{\text{1 cm}^3}{\text{19.3 g}} \\ & = 6.7788 \times 10^{-23} \: \text{cm}^3 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the edge length of the unit cell.

[tex]\begin{aligned} a & = \sqrt[3]{V} \\ & = \sqrt[3]{6.7788 \times 10^{-23} \: \text{cm}^3} \\ & = 4.0774 \times 10^{-8} \: \text{cm} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the atomic radius of gold.

[tex]\begin{aligned} r & = \frac{a}{\sqrt{8}} \\ & = \frac{4.0774 \times 10^{-8} \: \text{cm}}{\sqrt{8}} \\ & = 1.44 \times 10^{-8} \: \text{cm} \\ & = 1.44 \times 10^{-8} \: \text{cm} \times \frac{\text{1 m}}{\text{100 cm}} \times \frac{\text{1 pm}}{1 \times 10^{-12} \: \text{m}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{144 pm}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the atomic radius of gold is 144 pm.
