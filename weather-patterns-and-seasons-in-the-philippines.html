Answer:

The Philippines enjoys a tropical climate that is for the most part hot and humid year-round, but can be roughly divided into a dry season between November and May, and a wet season between June and October. Answer:

Impacts of climate change in the Philippines are immense, including: annual losses in GDP, changes in rainfall patterns and distribution, droughts, threats to biodiversity and food security, sea level rise, public health risks, and endangerment of vulnerable groups such as women and indigenous people.

11. 1. What are the activitys of people during dry season? 2. What are the activitys of people during wet season?3. Describe the weather patterns in the Philippines..​


Answer:

1. The activities of people during the dry season may include farming, fishing, hiking, camping, and engaging in outdoor activities. People may also take advantage of the warm and dry weather to do some recreational activities like swimming, playing outdoor sports, and having picnics.

2. During the wet season, people tend to stay indoors and engage in indoor activities like reading, watching movies, playing board games, and spending more time on their hobbies. Farmers may also use this time to prepare their fields for the next planting season, while fishermen may reduce their fishing activities due to the rough seas and strong winds.

3. The weather patterns in the Philippines vary depending on the region. Generally, the Philippines has a tropical climate characterized by a rainy season from June to November and a dry season from December to May. However, some regions experience different weather patterns. In the northern parts of the Philippines, there are only two seasons- the dry season from November to April and the wet season from May to October. In the southern parts, the climate is characterized by year-round rainfall with no distinct dry season. Additionally, the Philippines is also prone to typhoons, which usually occur during the rainy season.
