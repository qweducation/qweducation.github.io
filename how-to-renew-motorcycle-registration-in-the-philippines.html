5. What are the issues on pre-registration for Philippine Identification System (PhilSys) or National ID?​


Answer:

N/A (Free of charge) The Philippine Identification System ID (PhilSys ID) or the Philippine Identification Card (PhilID) (Filipino: Pambansang Pagkakakilanlan) is the official national identity card for Filipino citizens worldwide and foreign permanent residents in the Philippines.

Current concerns on the pre-registration process for PhilSys ID:

1. Privacy

While the intentions of the centralized ID are good, the government has not yet proven its capability in handling and protecting data privacy. Previous instances of hacking of government websites, social media accounts and other digital assets over the years also contributed to the general public’s skepticism and fears over the implementation of a centralized ID system.

2. House-to-house Visit

Some Filipinos raised concerns on the health and security risks of the manual visitation of PSA staff amidst the pandemic.

3. Voluntary Basis

Other Filipinos questioned the purpose for the pre-registration to be on a voluntary basis, citing the government’s goal of convenience to Filipinos, businesses and other organizations.

24. PHILIPPINE SENATES REJECTS US BASE RENEWAL


This headline is referring to the rejection of a proposal to renew the agreement allowing the United States military to use Philippine military bases. The agreement, known as the Visiting Forces Agreement (VFA), was set to expire in 2021 but the Philippine Senate voted in February 2020 to end it earlier. The VFA had been in effect since 1998 and provided a legal framework for US military personnel to visit and operate in the Philippines. The decision to end the VFA was a result of various factors, including political tensions between the US and the Philippines, concerns over Philippine sovereignty, and opposition from various Philippine groups who viewed the presence of US military personnel as a threat to national security. The rejection of the US base renewal was seen as a significant move by the Philippine government to assert its independence and pursue a more independent foreign policy.
