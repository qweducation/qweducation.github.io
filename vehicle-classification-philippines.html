1. What is the first vehicle in the philippines ? History of the first vehicle in philippines


Answer:

In 1900, La Estrella del Norte shipped from France to the Philippines a "Georges Richard", the first-ever automobile to have landed in the Philippines. Its owner was Dr. Miciano, a rich doctor.

Answer: The first vehicle in the Philippines was a steam-powered locomotive in 1887, which was used for the Manila-Dagupan railway system. In 1907, the first car arrived, a 4-horsepower Benz automobile, but it was slow to become popular. Today, the Philippines has a thriving automotive industry with many international car manufacturers.

20. In what way philippine literature becomes a vehicle in promoting the filipino cultural heritage


Answer:

Philippine Literature portrays the lives, cultures, traditions and everyday lives of our early Filipinos. As the readers read these, they are introduced to the cultural heritage of the pre-colonial times. While reading, the readers can imagine what is life like before the conquerors came into the country. After reading, the readers are now familiar with the life and culture of early Filipinos. Philippine Literature becomes a simple and fun vehicle in promoting the cultural heritage of Filipinos since they can be familiarized with different traditions and cultures without reading and studying tons of books and articles.
