23. Philippine individual income tax is progressive. In this context, what does progressive means?​


Answer: A progressive tax is one where the average tax burden increases with income. High-income families pay a disproportionate share of the tax burden, while low- and middle-income taxpayers shoulder a relatively small tax burden. Expand Definition.

Explanation: The individual income tax is progressive, thanks to the impact of refundable credits for lower-income households (average tax rates are negative for the two lowest income quintiles), the standard deduction (which exempts a minimum level of income from the tax), and a graduated rate structure

30. What are the various modes of collection and recovery of income tax in every country? How about in the Philippines? ​


Answer: The Corporate Recovery and Tax Incentives for Enterprises Act (CREATE) is the most significant fiscal stimulus for businesses in recent history.

Explanation: Over the next ten years, it is expected to save private businesses more than a trillion pesos in taxes. MSMEs will be the biggest beneficiaries of CREATE, as the country's corporate income tax rate will be reduced from 30% to 20% for the first time. Large corporations will also benefit from an immediate reduction in the corporate income tax rate from 30% to 25%.

CREATE also provides additional types of tax relief as part of the government's economic recovery package, which addresses the various demands and concerns of the business sector as a result of the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic. CREATE also includes a performance-based, time-bound, targeted, and transparent tax incentive system. During hearings and conversations, parties universally agreed on these principles.

The CREATE tax incentive scheme balances the interests of all stakeholders while adhering to core values and keeping the country's fiscal concerns in mind. CREATE has implemented a tier-based incentive system based on industry and location. This allows our industrial policy to be reflected in the fiscal incentive system.

Activities in more sophisticated sectors and less developed areas will receive longer incentives. To summarize, all businesses seeking tax breaks will continue to work with investment promotion agencies, or IPAs. The IPAs will approve incentives unless the proposed project or activity exceeds a one billion peso investment capital threshold.

Above this threshold, applications for incentives will be decided on by the Fiscal Incentives Review Board, an interagency Cabinet-level body charged with overseeing the granting of fiscal incentives. The Philippines' Strategic Investment Priority Plan will cover the industrial tiers (SIPP). The SIPP includes programs or activities that encourage long-term development and progress. Because the first SIPP has yet to be produced, the Board of Investments' 2020 Investment Priorities Plan serves as a transitional SIPP until the first SIPP is released.

Activities under the 2020 IPP may be eligible for incentives under the Tier I classification, as suggested by the BOI and authorized by the FIRB, without prejudice to upgrading to Tiers II or III if qualified under the new SIPP. The powers and functions of the Fiscal Incentives Review Board (FIRB) are expanded under the CREATE Act as part of its oversight mandate. The FIRB structure under CREATE ensures that, in addition to tax subsidies, tax incentives are granted and properly monitored by relevant agencies. The FIRB is an interagency government body authorized by Philippine law to grant tax breaks to registered businesses.
