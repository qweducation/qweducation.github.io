[tex]\sf f(x)= \begin{cases}\sf 300, &\sf if \: x \leq 15 \\\sf 400, &\sf if \: 15<x \leq 40 \\\sf 20x-400, &\sf if \: x > 40 \end{cases}[/tex]

B. What would the groomer charge if your bulldog weighs 60 pounds?

x=60, 60>40

[tex]\begin{aligned} &\sf f(x)=20x-400 \\&\sf f(60)=20(60)-400 \\&\sf f(60)=1200-400 \\&\sf f(60)=800 \end{aligned} [/tex]

The groomer would charge 800 pesos if your bulldog weighs 60 pounds.

30. Solve the problems below. Show your complete solutions. There are 250 dogs at a dog show that weigh an average of 12 pounds, with a standard deviation of 8 pounds. If 4 dogs are chosen at random, what is the probability that the average weight is greater than 8 pounds?​


We can use the Central Limit Theorem to solve this problem. The theorem says that if we have a large sample size (n > 30 in most cases) and a population with a known mean and standard deviation, then the distribution of the sample means will be approximately normal, regardless of the shape of the population distribution. The mean of the sample means will be equal to the population mean, and the standard deviation of the sample means (also called the standard error) will be equal to the population standard deviation divided by the square root of the sample size.

In this case, we have a population of 250 dogs with a mean weight of 12 pounds and a standard deviation of 8 pounds. We are interested in the average weight of a sample of 4 dogs, and we want to find the probability that this average weight is greater than 8 pounds.

First, we need to calculate the standard error of the sample means:

standard error = standard deviation / square root of sample size

standard error = 8 / sqrt(4)

standard error = 4

Next, we can convert the sample mean to a z-score using the formula:

z = (x - μ) / SE

where x is the sample mean, μ is the population mean, and SE is the standard error.

For a sample mean of 8 pounds:

z = (8 - 12) / 4

z = -1

The probability of getting a z-score less than -1 (i.e., a sample mean less than 8 pounds) is 0.1587, according to a standard normal distribution table. Therefore, the probability of getting a z-score greater than -1 (i.e., a sample mean greater than 8 pounds) is:

P(z > -1) = 1 - P(z < -1)

P(z > -1) = 1 - 0.1587

P(z > -1) = 0.8413

So the probability that the average weight of a sample of 4 dogs is greater than 8 pounds is approximately 0.8413 or 84.13%.
