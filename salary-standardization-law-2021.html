9. why development wage and salary structure affects the workers to thier standard of living​


Answer:

The wage and salary structure is an essential aspect of any organization, and it has a significant impact on the standard of living of workers. The development of wage and salary structure is crucial because it determines how much employees are paid for their work. This, in turn, affects their standard of living in several ways.

Firstly, the development of wage and salary structure affects the purchasing power of workers. When employees are paid a fair wage, they can afford to purchase goods and services that improve their quality of life. On the other hand, if workers are paid low wages, they may struggle to meet their basic needs such as food, shelter, and healthcare. This can result in a decline in their standard of living.

Secondly, the development of wage and salary structure affects the motivation and productivity of workers. When employees are paid a fair wage, they are motivated to work harder and be more productive. This is because they feel valued and appreciated by their employer. On the other hand, if workers are paid low wages, they may feel demotivated and unappreciated, which can lead to a decline in their productivity.

Finally, the development of wage and salary structure affects the retention rate of workers. When employees are paid a fair wage, they are more likely to stay with their employer for a longer period. This is because they feel satisfied with their job and compensation package. On the other hand, if workers are paid low wages, they may leave their employer in search of better-paying jobs. This can result in high turnover rates, which can be costly for employers.

In conclusion, the development of wage and salary structures plays a crucial role in determining the standard of living of workers. It affects their purchasing power, motivation and productivity, and retention rate. Therefore, it is essential for organizations to develop a fair and competitive wage and salary structure that takes into account the needs of their employees.

13. The salaries of employees of a certain company in Baguio City have a mean of P6,500 and a standard deviation of P1,500. What is the probability that an employee selected at random at random will have a salary of less than P7,000. *​


NORMAL DISTRIBUTION

Given

Mean (µ) = ₱ 6,500Standard Deviation (σ) = ₱ 1,500Observed Value (x) = x < ₱ 7,000Z-Score (Z) = ?Probability of the sample = ?

First, we have to find the z-score of the observed value in order to determine the probability.

Z = x - µ / σ

Z = 7000 - 6500 / 1500

Z = 1500 / 1500

Z = 1

Using a normal distribution table, find the area of the z-score which is 1. The area of Z = 1 is 0.3413.

But what we are finding is the probability for the salary of less than ₱ 7,000, so we have to subtract the area to 1.

1 - 0.3413  

= 0.6587

The probability is 65.87 %

If the average starting salary for this year's graduates at a large university (LU) is $20,000 with a standard deviation of $8,000, and the starting salaries are normally distributed, it means that the majority of the graduates from LU will have starting salaries that are within one standard deviation of the average starting salary. This means that most of the graduates will have starting salaries between $20,000 - $8,000 = $12,000 and $20,000 + $8,000 = $28,000.

16. Mga halimbawa ng salary standardization


Answer:

Salary standardization is a law of 2019. The proposed Salary Standardization Law of 2019 is set to benefit more than 1.4 million government employees especially those under Salary Grade 11 to 19. This comprises 79 percent of the total government employees, including teachers and nurses.

According to the Salary Standardization:

"The proposed Salary Standardization Law (SSL) of 2016 will result in a weighted average increase of forty five percent (45%) in the compensation of all salary grades, and raise compensation of government personnel to at least seventy percent (70%) of the private sector rate."

Example of salary standardization is the following:

The minimum salary for public school teachers increased from P19,077 in 2016 to P20,754 in 2019, under the Salary Standardization Law.

1. To calculate the expected mean and standard deviation of the sampling distribution of sample mean, we can use the following formulas:

Expected Mean = Population Mean = 88

Standard Deviation of Sampling Distribution of Sample Mean = Population Standard Deviation / sqrt(sample size)

= 20 / sqrt(10) = 6.32

Therefore, the expected mean of the sampling distribution of the sample mean is 88, and the standard deviation of the sampling distribution of the sample mean is 6.32.

Average Salary = Population Mean = Php25,000

Standard Deviation of Sample = Population Standard Deviation / sqrt(sample size)

= 12,054.05 / sqrt(150) = 988.74

Therefore, the average salary of the 150 teachers is Php25,000, and the standard deviation of their salary is Php988.74.
