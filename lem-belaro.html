Find the LCM by listing multiples. (method)

List the first several multiples of each number.

Look for multiples common to both lists.

If there are no common multiples in the lists, write out additional multiples for each number.

We can find out the common multiples of two or more numbers by listing their multiples. Out of these common multiples, the least common multiple is considered and the LCM of two given numbers can thus be calculated.

9. What are the importance of lemming,lichen,artic fox,snowy owl and caribou?


Answer:

Lemmings are vital to the functioning of many High Arctic ecosystems. They occur in large numbers, reproduce rapidly, and consume large quantities of vegetation.

Lichens enable algae to live all over the world in many different climates, they also provide a means to convert carbon dioxide in the atmosphere through photosynthesis into oxygen, which we all need to survive.

Arctic helps circulate the world's ocean current, moving cold and warm water around the globe.

Foxes help to control populations of their prey animals, such as rodents and rabbits. They also may disperse seeds by eating fruit.

Snowy owls play an essential role in controlling the numbers of rodents in the Arctic tundra environment. One snowy owl can eat up to 1600 rodents in a year.

Caribou are an important resource for indigenous peoples, a prey species for carnivores and omnivores, such as bears and wolves, and a critical source of nutrients for the soil in areas where they forage in large numbers.

17. lems John took 750s to bicycle to his grandmother's house, a displacement of 400m. What is his speed?​


Answer:

John's speed is 0.533 m/s.

Explanation:

To find John's speed, we can use the formula:

speed = distance / time

Given that John took 750 seconds to travel a distance of 400 meters, we can substitute these values into the formula:

speed = 400 m / 750 s

Simplifying this expression, we get:

speed = 0.533 meters per second (m/s)

Therefore, John's speed is 0.533 m/s.
