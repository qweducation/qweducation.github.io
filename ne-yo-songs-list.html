Explanation:

The yo sen scale is a musical scale used in traditional Japanese music. It consists of the following notes:

yo

ro

ha

ni

ho

to

The song "Sakura" is a traditional Japanese folk song that is often associated with springtime and the cherry blossom season. Here is the melody of "Sakura" using the yo sen scale:

yo-re-ha-ni

yo-re-ha-ni

ha-ni-ho-he-to

ha-ni-ho-he-to

In terms of musical elements, "Sakura" uses a pentatonic scale, which is a musical scale that consists of five notes per octave. This is evident in the melody of the song, which uses the five notes of the yo sen scale: yo, ro, ha, ni, and ho. The song also has a simple, repetitive structure, with each verse consisting of the same four measures.

Another musical element present in "Sakura" is the use of vibrato, which is a fluctuation in pitch that is created by rapid, slight variations in the tension of the vocal cords or the strings of a musical instrument. Vibrato is often used in traditional Japanese music to add expressiveness and emotional depth to the performance.

Overall, "Sakura" is a simple yet beautiful song that showcases the unique musical elements of traditional Japanese music.
