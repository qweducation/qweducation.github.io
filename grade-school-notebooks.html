Greatest Common Factor

Factors are numbers that when multiplied will result to the given number. It divides that number without leaving any remainder.Greatest Common Factor is the highest among the factors.  While factoring uses division, multiples involve the multiplication process. The common method of finding the GCF is by listing method. Factor tree and continuous division can also be used.

Answers:

12 students

8 notebooks, 7 pencils, and 10 pad papers

Solutions:

Asked: The number of students that will receive the items and the number of each items that they will receive.

Given: 96 notebook

           84 pencils

           120 pad papers

Operation: Find the GCF of 96, 84, and 120

Number Sentence: 96 - 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 96

                                84 - 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 7, 12, 14, 21, 28, 42, 84

                               120 - 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 10, 12, 15, 20, 24, 30, 40, 60, 120

                                GCF:

Solution: 96 - 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 16, 24, 32, 48, 96

              84 - 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 7, 12, 14, 21, 28, 42, 84

            120 - 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8, 10, 12, 15, 20, 24, 30, 40, 60, 120

            GCF: 12

Answer: 12 students

96 ÷ 12 = 8 notebooks

84 ÷ 12 = 7 pencils

120 ÷ 12 = 10 pad papers
