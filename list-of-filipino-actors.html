17. list down the qualities should a good actor /actress must have. I​


Answer:

Creativity. Actors interpret their characters’ feelings and motives in order to portray the characters in the most compelling way.

Memorization skills. Actors memorize many lines before filming begins or a show opens. Television actors often appear on camera with little time to memorize scripts, and scripts frequently may be revised or written moments before filming.

Persistence. Actors may audition for many roles before getting a job. They must be able to accept rejection and keep going.

Physical stamina. Actors should be in good enough physical condition to endure heat from stage or studio lights and the weight of heavy costumes. They may work long hours, including acting in more than one performance a day, and they must do so without getting overly tired.

Reading skills. When looking for a new role, actors read many scripts and must be able to interpret how a writer has described their character.

Speaking skills. Actors—particularly stage actors—must be able to say their lines clearly, project their voice, and pronounce words so that audiences understand them.

Movie: "Heneral Luna"

Director: Jerrold Tarog

Actors: John Arcilla, Mon Confiado, Epi Quizon, Joem Bascon, and Archie Alemania

Film Genre: Historical Drama

Summary:

"Heneral Luna" is a historical drama film that tells the story of General Antonio Luna, a celebrated Filipino military leader during the Philippine-American War. The film portrays Luna's struggles to unite the Filipino forces against the American colonial forces while also dealing with internal political and personal conflicts.

Introduction:

The film begins with a scene showing the American troops invading the Philippines and the Filipino leaders discussing their next course of action. General Luna, played by John Arcilla, is introduced as a brilliant strategist who is passionate about leading the Filipino army to victory.

Body:

The film explores Luna's efforts to inspire the Filipino troops to fight for their country and take on the American forces. It also depicts his confrontations with rival factions in the Philippine government who are opposed to his leadership style and vision for the country. Luna's character is portrayed as a complex and flawed individual who is struggling to balance his personal ambitions with his duty to his country.

Climax:

The climax of the film is the Battle of Santo Tomas, where the Filipino forces led by Luna engage in a fierce battle against the American troops. Despite Luna's efforts to rally his troops and lead them to victory, he is ultimately betrayed by his own men and assassinated.
