2. What are the requirements for motor vehicle registration?


The requirement of motor vehicle registration is regulated in The Land Transportation Office (Filipino: Tanggapan ng Transportasyong-Lupa; LTO).

LTO is a Philippine government body under the Department of Transportation in charge of all land transportation in the country. Therefore, here are some required documents that you should fulfill:

1. Original Sales Receipt

2. Original LTO copy or applicable insurance Certificate of Coverage or COC electronically transmitted (Third Party Liability)

3. Original copy of the Philippine National Police - Highway Patrol Group (PNPHPG) Motor Vehicle (MV) Clearance Certificate and the Special Bank Receipt (SBR)

4. Reported original CSR or Certificate of Stock

5. Bring your Payment Reference Number if you paid by e-PAT.

Before going to LTO to register your car, make sure you have all of the essential paperworks so you don't have to go through the trouble of having to come back because you forgot some of the documentation you needed.

6. A cylinder tin can measure 18 cm high and 20 cm in diameter. How many square centimeters of tin will be required for 2.5 dozen cans? Use π = 22/7.


Answer:

To find the surface area of the tin can, we need to calculate the area of the top and bottom circles and the area of the cylinder's curved surface.

The area of one circle is given by:

A = πr^2, where r is the radius of the circle.

Since the diameter is given as 20 cm, the radius is half of that or 10 cm.

So, the area of one circle is A = (22/7) x 10^2 = 314 sq. cm

The area of the curved surface of the cylinder is given by:

A = 2πrh, where r is the radius and h is the height.

So, the area of the curved surface for one can is A = 2 x (22/7) x 10 x 18 = 1131.43 sq. cm (approx.)

The total surface area of one can is the sum of the areas of the top and bottom circles and the area of the curved surface:

Total surface area = 2A(circle) + A(curved surface)

Total surface area = 2 x 314 + 1131.43

Total surface area = 1759.43 sq. cm (approx.)

Since we are asked for the surface area of 2.5 dozen cans, we need to multiply the surface area of one can by 2.5 x 12 = 30:

Total surface area for 2.5 dozen cans = 30 x 1759.43

Total surface area for 2.5 dozen cans = 52783.04 sq. cm (approx.)

Therefore, approximately 52,783 square centimeters of tin will be required for 2.5 dozen cans.

Knowing the general requirements and procedures for registering a business or organization is incredibly important as it ensures that everything has been completed correctly and that your entity is legally registered.
