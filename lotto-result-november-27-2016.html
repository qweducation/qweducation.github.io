15. ACCOUNTING: I HAVE THE ANSWER, I JUST NEED SOLUTION & SHORT EXPLANATION. On November 1, 2016, GLOBE CORPORATION, a Philippine company said merchandise to SMART COMPANY, a foreign firm. GLOBE measured and recorded the account receivable from the sale at P78,000. SMART paid for this account on November 30, 2016. Spot rates for the Foreign Currency (FC) on November 1 and November 30, respectively, were P.080 and P0.78 If the sale of the merchandise was denominated in the foreign currency (FC), the November 30 entry to record the receipt of payment from SMART included a A. credit to Accounts Receivable for P76,050 B. credit to Exchange Gain for P1,950 C. debit to Cash for P78,000 D. debit to Exchange Loss for P1,950 ANSWER: D SOLUTION: ? EXPLANATION: ?


Answer:

SOLUTION:

The sale of merchandise was denominated in foreign currency, which means that the account receivable of P78,000 was recorded in FC. On November 30, when SMART paid for the account, GLOBE received FC at a spot rate of P0.78. However, the spot rate on November 1 was P0.80, which means that the FC appreciated against the Philippine peso (PHP).

To record the receipt of payment, GLOBE needs to convert the FC into PHP using the spot rate on November 30. The formula for the conversion is:

FC x Spot Rate = PHP

Using this formula, the conversion of the account receivable of P78,000 on November 30 is:

P78,000 x P0.78 = P60,840

Therefore, the entry to record the receipt of payment from SMART on November 30 is:

Debit Cash P60,840 Debit Exchange Loss P1,950 Credit Accounts Receivable P78,000

The debit to Exchange Loss is necessary because the FC appreciated against the PHP, which means that GLOBE received less PHP than what it recorded as account receivable. The difference between the amount recorded and the amount received is the exchange loss of P1,950.

EXPLANATION:

The entry to record the receipt of payment from SMART on November 30 involves converting the FC into PHP using the spot rate on that day. The difference between the amount recorded as account receivable and the amount received is either an exchange gain or an exchange loss, depending on whether the FC appreciated or depreciated against the PHP. In this case, the FC appreciated, which means that GLOBE incurred an exchange loss of P1,950.

30. Mr. Sixto is a minimum wage earner in Region IX. Last November 7, 2016, he worked for 7 hours only. How much is his compensation for this said date?​


Answer:

Mr. Sixto is a minimum wage earner in Region IX. Last November 7, 2016, he worked for 7 hours only. How much is his compensation for this said date?

His compensation for November 7 is his daily rate divided by the total number of hours he should render times the number of hours he worked.

(undertime pay = daily rate ÷ 8 × 7)

Step-by-step explanation:

Working time that is less than full time or less than the required minimum working hours is known as undertime.

Working Hours in the Philippines

Normal Working Hours

The normal hours of work an employee has to render must not exceed eight (8) hours a day and should be exclusive of the one (1) hour daily lunch break. Philippine laws, however, do not prohibit work done for less than eight hours.

Undertime Not Offset by Overtime

According to Article 88 of the Labor Code, undertime work on a business day shall not be offset by overtime work on any other day because the hourly rate of overtime is higher than the hours missed when an employee works for less than eight hours.

Going back to the problem, Mr. Sixto worked for just 7 hours last November 7, 2016, which clearly shows that he didn't complete the 8-hour work and that fell as undertime.

The problem didn't mention his daily rate, and I don't know exactly the daily rate in Region IX, so let's just assume that his daily rate is 400 pesos to see how undertime is calculated.

As mentioned, undertime pay is equal to daily rate divided by the total number of hours he should render (8) times the number of hours he worked (7).

undertime pay = 400 ÷ 8 × 7

= 350 pesos

So on the said date, he would be paid 350 pesos.
