13. In a college entrance test, 450 students took the exam and the results yielded u= 125 and σ = 30.50. How many students passed the entrance exam if only those whose score is at least above Z = -0.5 will pass?​


Answer:

=> 311 students passed the entrance exam.

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

n = 450

u = 125

σ = 30.5

z = -0.5

Step 1: Calculate the Z-score for the passing mark of the entrance exam:

z_p = ( 125 - (-0.5) - 125 ) / 30.5

z_p = 0.50

Step 2: Calculate the proportion of students who have scored above the passing mark:

p = (1 - NormalCDF(-0.5, 0.50))

p = 0.6915

Step 3: Calculate the number of students who passed the entrance exam:

Number of students who passed the entrance exam = p * n

Number of students who passed the entrance exam = 310.73

=> 311 students passed the entrance exam.

16. Suppose that a set of exam scores has a mean of 75 and a variance of 25 Assume that the exam scores are normally distributed. What is the probability that an exam score is between 75 and 77?


PROBLEM:

Suppose that a set of exam scores has a mean of 75 and a variance of 25. Assume that the exam scores are normally distributed. What is the probability that an exam score is between 75 and 77?

SOLUTION:

Step 1: Identify the parts of the word problem. The word problem will identify:

The mean (average or μ) — 75

Standard deviation (σ) — 5

Standard deviation is the square root of variance. [tex]\rm \sqrt{25} = 5 [/tex]

X: the numbers associated with “between” — 75 and 77

Step 2: Figure out the z-scores and Find the area in the z-table. Plug the X values into the z value formula and solve. The μ (the mean), is 75.

[tex]\small\underline{\boxed{z=\frac{X-μ}{σ}}}[/tex]

[tex]z=\frac{75-75}{5}[/tex]

[tex]z=\frac{0}{5}[/tex]

[tex]z=0=0.00000[/tex]

NOTE: If a Z-score is 0, it indicates that the data point's score is identical to the mean score.

[tex]\small\underline{\boxed{z=\frac{X-μ}{σ}}}[/tex]

[tex]z=\frac{77-75}{6}[/tex]

[tex]z=\frac{2}{5}[/tex]

[tex]z=0.4=0.15542[/tex]

See the attached pictures for the z-table and the graph (illustration).

Step 3: Add the computed z-scores to get the probability or area between 0 and 0.4. You can also convert them in percent.

[tex]\boxed{0.00000+0.15542=0.15542=15.54\%}[/tex]

ANSWER:The probability that an exam score is between 75 and 77 is 0.15542.

26. On a standardized exam, the scores are normally distributed with a mean of 200 and a standard deviation of 50. Find the z-score of a person who scored 280 on the exam.​


On a standardized exam, the scores are normally distributed with a mean of 200 and a standard deviation of 50. Find the z-score of a person who scored 280 on the exam.

To find the z-score of a person who scored 280 on the exam, we use the formula:

z = (x - μ) / σ

where:

x is the exam score (280 in this case)

μ is the mean score (200)

σ is the standard deviation (50)

Substituting the values into the formula, we get:

z = (280 - 200) / 50

z = 1.6

Therefore, the z-score of a person who scored 280 on the exam is 1.6.
