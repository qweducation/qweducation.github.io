11. Accountingwhat is the journal entry of this?The bank issued a debit memo, P1,000 for interest charges.​


Answer:

The journal entry for this would be:

Debit: Interest Expense (or Interest Charges) 1,000

Credit: Bank 1,000

This entry records the expense of the interest charges that the bank has issued. The debit to the Interest Expense account increases the expense and decreases the retained earnings while the credit to the Bank account decreases the assets of the company.

Answer:

The unadjusted book disbursements for December are P1,080,275. This is the final amount after adjusting for various errors, including an error of P1,800, an NFS check that was returned by the bank in December but recorded in January for P4,300, an NFS check that was returned by the bank and recorded in December for P5,200, a debit memo for this month for P2,100, and a debit memo for last month for P3,750. By subtracting the total adjustments from the adjusted book disbursements of P1,089,925, we arrive at the unadjusted book disbursements of P1,080,275.

28. Accountingwhat is the journal entry of this?The bank issued a debit memo, P1,000 for interest charges.​​


Answer:

The journal entry for this transaction would be:

Debit: Interest Expense (or Interest Payable) 1,000

Credit: Bank 1,000

This entry records the increase in the Interest Expense account, which represents the cost of borrowing money from the bank, and the decrease in the Bank account, which represents the reduction in cash due to the payment of interest charges. The debit memo from the bank serves as the source document for this transaction.
