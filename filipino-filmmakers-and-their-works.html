QUESTION:

What is the connection of filmmaking in the lives of Filipino?

ANSWER:

The cinema of the Philippines (Filipino: Pelikulang Pilipino or Sine Pilipino) began with the introduction of the first moving pictures to the country on August 31, 1897 at the Salón de Pertierra in Manila. The following year, local scenes were shot on film for the first time by a Spaniard, Antonio Ramos, using the Lumiere Cinematograph.[5] While most early filmmakers and producers in the country were mostly wealthy enterprising foreigners and expatriates, on September 12, 1919, Dalagang Bukid (Country Maiden), a movie based on a popular musical play, was the first movie made and shown by Filipino filmmaker José Nepomuceno.[6] Dubbed as the "Father of Philippine Cinema," his work marked the start of cinema as an art form in the Philippines.[7

EXPLANATION:

Even with the problems currently facing motion pictures around the world, films are still considered as one of the popular forms of entertainment among the Filipino people, directly employing some 260,000 Filipinos and generating around ₱2 billion revenues annually.[8]

The Film Development Council of the Philippines established a national film archive in October 2011.[9] Furthermore, their annually held Luna Awards honor the outstanding Filipino films as voted by their own peers. Meanwhile, the Manunuri ng Pelikulang Pilipino hands out the Gawad Urian Awards, which is well known due to its credible choices of winners. Currently, Box Office Mojo compiles box office performance for local and foreign films in the country.

25. what is traditional filmmaking?


Answer

Traditional Filmmaking refers to the use of non-digital cameras which uses Rolls of Celluloid film strips (https://brainly.ph/question/2495311) to capture motion. Technology today made the use of Celluloid film strips obsolete, and cameras were replaced with digital filmmaking which was more efficient and much easier to handle in film production.

History

The turning point of the Art of Film making started in the 1880s when artists started experimenting with pictures and cameras available during that period. One of these experiments involves setting up multiple cameras in capturing the motion of an equestrian riding a horse. (This experiment was to see if all four hooves of a horse are off the ground during a gallop).

The artist then assembled the photos in a sequence to create an illusion of a moving picture. This came to be known as motion photography titled "The Horse in Motion" (1878) and is considered as the first film to be made.

28. Allied arts in filmmaking


Answer

Allied arts or Combined arts is the term used when referring to different Art forms joined together to create another Art form. For FIlm making, these are the field of Arts involved:

Visual Art - This refers to Cinematography in which the Director artistically envisions how the film should be shown. It involves compositing of visual effects, costumes and makeup, arrangements of visual elements to be shown on the camera, and other artistic elements that are visible to the camera.

Performance Art - This refers to acting for the camera, which is very different from acting for an audience in a theatre. Theatre acting involves exaggerated facial expressions and gestures because the performance was on stage. There was no support of cameras to see acting and what emotion was being portrayed. While in movie acting, the composition of the film affects the actor's gesture and facial expression.

Literature Art - This refers to written works of art like the story, scripts, storyboard, and screenplay.

Audio Art - This refers to Musical Art that involves Background music, Ambient Music, Sound Effects, Dialogue, and Composed Music.
