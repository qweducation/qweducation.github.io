9. If quadrilateral PQRS is a kite, which statements must be true? Check all that apply. QP ≅ QR PM ≅ MR QR ≅ RS ∠PQR ≅ ∠PSR ∠QPS ≅ ∠QRS


Draw the shape of a typical kite and label the vertices consecutively with PQRS in alphabetical order. A kite is a quadrilateral that has two sets of congruent sides. If you draw the kite, the two lines that formed the vertix pointing upward, and the twolines that formed the vertix pointing downward, are congruent to each other. If they are congruent, then it also follows that the opposite angles found inside the kite are congruent to each other. Following this logic, then the applicable statements are correct:

QP ≅ QR  

∠PQR ≅ ∠PSR  

∠QPS ≅ ∠QRS

16. Solve:∆XYZ is congruent to ∆QRS. Find the length of the side of ∆QRS​


Answer:

We cannot find the length of the side of ∆QRS with the given information alone.

The fact that ∆XYZ is congruent to ∆QRS only tells us that the corresponding sides of the two triangles have the same length. However, we do not know the length of any side of either triangle to begin with.

We need additional information, such as the length of a side or an angle measure, to find the length of a side of ∆QRS.

24. if R is between Q and S, QR= 20x + 2, RS= 30x + 3 and QS =105. what is the distance of QR?​


Answer:

QR = 42

Step-by-step explanation:

QS = 105

QR = 20x + 2

RS = 30x + 3

Equation:

20x + 2 + 30x + 3 = 105

50x + 5 = 105

50x = 105-5

50x/50 = 100/50

x = 2

Substitute x=2:

QR = 20x + 2  ⇒ 20(2)+2 = 42

RS = 30x + 3  ⇒  30(2)+3 = 63

Check:

42 + 63 = 105

105 = 105  (True)
