What is Social Placement?

Theories Connecting to Social Placement.

Social placement as defined by the text is the use of education to improve one's social standing.

In other words it is placing people in higher or lower social classes based on the education level they have completed.

Question to the class.

Conflict Theory

Education promotes social inequality through the use of standardized testing and the fact that all schools differ in the way that they teach and present the curriculum to the students making education unequal to different students.

Symbolic Interactionism

This theory explores the interaction of students in the classroom, on the playground and around the school in general and how that interaction affects the students such as how they may view gender roles. It also looks at how the different expectations and how much the teachers challenge their students may affect the success of the students or how much they may learn.
