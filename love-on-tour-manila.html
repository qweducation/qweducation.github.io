How to Get to Boracay

Located off the northwestern coast of Aklan, Boracay Island is very accessible to the beach-going public. If you’re coming from Manila, flying is the best option. All major airlines fly to either of them.

Two airports serve Boracay-bound passengers. Each airport has pros and cons.

Caticlan Airport and Kalibo Airport

Caticlan Airport and Kalibo Airport

Kalibo is farther, requiring around 2 hours of travel time, but fares are much cheaper.

Caticlan is closer, 15 minutes away, but fares are way more expensive.

Whatever you choose, you need to make your way to the Caticlan Jetty Port. Here’s a brief step-by-step guide:

From Kalibo Airport, take the bus or van to Caticlan Jetty Port. Fare: P200.

If you’re a big group, you may also consider arranging a private van transfer that will wait for you at the airport. I have tried this pre-arranged setup before when I was part of a group of 10. Cost was P3800 roundtrip (P1900 one-way, or P180 per person per way).

Upon arrival at the jetty port, proceed to the booths near the entrance to the building and settle the Terminal Fee (P100), Environmental Fee (P75), and Ferry Fare (P25).

Inside the port building, register, proceed to the Passenger Waiting Area, and wait for boarding announcement.

Board the boat or ferry to the island.

You will dock at either Cagban or Tambisaan Port. Either way, there are tricycles waiting here that can take you to your hotel. If your hotel is in the White Beach Area, cost is P100 per ride.

Welcome to the enchanting archipelago of the Philippines, a tropical paradise that offers a plethora of breathtaking destinations and cultural experiences. Manila, a vibrant metropolis, blends modernity with rich history. Explore the walled city of Intramuros, where you can stroll along cobblestone streets and discover Spanish colonial architecture. Visit Fort Santiago, a citadel that holds significant historical value, or immerse yourself in the past at the National Museum of the Philippines. For shopaholics, indulge in retail therapy at the sprawling malls of Makati or experience the bustling markets of Divisoria.

As we venture outside Manila, let's head to Cebu, known as the Queen City of the South. Discover the fascinating blend of heritage and urbanization in this bustling city. For nature enthusiasts, a visit to Oslob is a must, where you can swim alongside whale sharks or take a dip in the refreshing Kawasan Falls.

Moving on, let's explore the pristine islands of Palawan. Palawan boasts unparalleled natural beauty, with its turquoise waters, limestone cliffs, and hidden lagoons. Explore the enchanting Underground River in Puerto Princesa, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, or embark on an island-hopping adventure in El Nido, where you can discover secret beaches and stunning coral reefs. 10. what is the difference between a package tour and independent tour


Answer:

A package tourist goes on a package tour, where they pay for all flights, transfers, accommodation, etc., together and in advance, usually through a travel agency. An independent traveler books different aspects of their trip separately (for example, they might book their flight on the internet, then get a taxi from the airport to their hotel, pay for their hotel when they arrive at their destination, etc.) and does not usually rely on an agency.

21. C. Answer the following questions: 1. What is a tour? 2. What is tour guiding? 3. What are the roles that tour guides play during a tour? 4. Cite 5 (five) characteristics of a tour guide 5. What are the limitations of a tour guide? 6. How to become a professional tour guide? 7. What is a tour guide's client? 8. What are the operational problems of a tour guide during a tour? 9. What are the elements of a tour guide? 10. What is the meaning of the Greek word tornos?​


Answer:

1. A tour is a planned and organized trip, usually for leisure or educational purposes, that involves visiting different places of interest.

2. Tour guiding is the profession of providing assistance, information, and interpretation to people who are visiting places of interest.

3. The roles that tour guides play during a tour include:

- Providing information about the destination, its history, culture, and attractions

- Ensuring the safety and comfort of the tourists

- Managing the itinerary and logistics of the tour

- Facilitating communication and interaction between the tourists and locals

- Creating a fun and engaging experience for the tourists

4. Characteristics of a tour guide include:

- Knowledgeable about the destination

- Good communication skills

- Flexibility and adaptability

- Personable and friendly

- Organized and detail-oriented

5. Limitations of a tour guide include:

- Language barriers

- Limited time at each attraction

- Limited flexibility in the itinerary

- Unpredictable weather or other unforeseen circumstances

6. To become a professional tour guide, one usually needs to complete a training program or obtain a certification from a recognized organization, such as a tourism board or association.

7. A tour guide's client is typically a tourist or group of tourists who are visiting a destination and have hired a tour guide to assist them.

8. Operational problems of a tour guide during a tour can include issues with transportation, accommodations, weather, and unexpected events such as strikes or closures of attractions.

9. The elements of a tour guide include:

- Knowledge about the destination and its attractions

- Good communication skills

- Management of logistics and itinerary

- Interpersonal skills and ability to connect with tourists

- Flexibility and problem-solving skills

10. The Greek word tornos means "to turn around," which may refer to the idea of a guide leading a group of tourists around a destination.
