10. 2. You are at war. Deciphering an intercepted code, you learn two things about your enemy. A single spot in their defense will be at its weakest in ten days, and they will attack one of your cities in five days. What will you do with this information? What is the most efficient course of action? ​


Answer:

Do I have the ability to win the war by exploiting this brief weakness? If so, then my attention will be on making sure that happens. If not, then I have to keep the fact that I have broken the enemy code a secret, so that I can monitor enemy movements over the long term and (hopefully) come up with a winning strategy.

Assuming the first option, in which I can potentially win in 10 days:

Does anything need to be done in response to the city threat? Your question does not state whether or not the city is defended or has been the target of attacks previously. For now, let’s assume the city is vulnerable to this attack.

This presents a difficult choice. People will die no matter what is done, and I have to decide who. I can’t evacuate the city, since that would give away that we knew the attack was coming. Nor can I completely fortify it, since that would also give it away. Either would jeopardize the chance of ending the war afterward, since the enemy would respond by shoring up their defenses.

However, it is wartime, so some degree of defense is expected. Probably the best course of action would be to slightly (and subtly) shore up certain defenses to protect against a total loss. If it is possible to increase defenses generally (say, in several cities), without compromising resources for the ultimate goal, then I’d do so. That would look like an overall strategy, rather than a response to specific information.

An alternative is a preliminary strike to potentially weaken the upcoming attack. This is difficult to pull off, though. The question implies that this is a formidable foe and so attacking openly is very dangerous. I can’t risk losing forces for the main assault in 10 days. The ultimate point in this scenario is to not let the enemy know that I have broken the code while organizing a winning stroke. If I can reduce damage in the meantime without sacrificing the strategic advantage, then that is the best play, since it will ensure a quick end to the war and save the most lives.
