D.51

Let's analyze the question.

There are 76 students in the canteen. 24 buys both banana cue and ice candy while 49 students buy ice candy. The question is how many students bought banana cue?

Okay, so we know that there are already 24 students that bought the banana cue. So we have to add the remaining students.

We have to subtract the ones who bought the ice candies from the total students.

76-49= 27

So we have 27 students who bought banana cues only. We have to add this to the ones that bought both banana cue and ice candy because they are still counted.

24+27= 51

We have 51 students that bought banana cues.

23. 4. How these candy/snack packages may cause environmental pollution?​


Answer:

Almost all food that we buy comes packaged. Most food packaging is made of plastic and unfortunately, most packaging is designed as single-use and is typically thrown away rather than reused or recycled. After it is used, most packaging is discarded and is either buried in a landfill or becomes litter that is carried along by wind and water currents into the environment. Packaging sent to landfills, especially when made from plastics, does not degrade quickly or, in some cases, at all, and chemicals from the packaging materials, including inks and dyes from labeling, can leach into groundwater and soil. Food packaging waste that isn’t recycled or composted is typically landfilled or incinerated. While both options have benefits for waste management, They produce air emissions, including greenhouse gases. Landfills emit ammonia and hydrogen sulfide and incinerators can emit mercury, lead, hydrogen chloride, sulfur dioxides, nitrous oxides and particulates.
