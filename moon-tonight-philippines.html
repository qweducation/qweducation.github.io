Answer:

These were led by Garcia Jofre Loaisa (1525), Sebastian Cabot (1526), Alvaro de Saavedra (1527), Rudy Lopez de Villalobos (1542) and Miguel Lopez de Legazpi (1564). Only the last two actually reached the Philippines; and only Legazpi succeeded in colonizing the Islands.

The expedition of Garcia Jofre de Loaisa left Spain for the Moluccas on 24 July 1525, to open up the spice trade initiated by the Magellan expedition.

Thus, Legazpi was able to reconquer the Philippines and establish the first ever the Manila-Mexico trade route by way of Barra de Navidad and later through the port of Acapulco which later became known as the Manila-Acapulco trade route of the Manila Galleon.
