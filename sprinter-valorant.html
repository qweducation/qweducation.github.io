4. a sprinter runs 9 km/hr faster than the other sprinter. When the faster sprinter travelled a distance of 93 km, the other one has gone 66 km. what is the speed of both runners?​


Answer:

22 km/hr and 31 km/hr

Step-by-step explanation:

The time for the faster sprinter to travel a distance of 93 km is equal to the time for the slower one to cover 66 km.

Let x be the speed of the slower sprinter in km/hr.

Note: Time = Distance/Speed

93/(x + 9) = 66/x

93x = 66(x + 9)

93x = 66x + 594

27x = 594

➠x = 22 km/hr

➠x + 9 = 31 km/hr

17. a sprinter goes from a speed of 10 m/s to a 15 m/s in 2 seconds find the solution of sprinter ​


Answer:

To find the solution of the sprinter, we need to use the formula:

acceleration (a) = (final velocity (v) - initial velocity (u)) / time (t)

Here, the initial velocity is 10 m/s, the final velocity is 15 m/s, and the time taken is 2 seconds.

So, the acceleration of the sprinter can be calculated as:

a = (15 m/s - 10 m/s) / 2 s

a = 2.5 m/s^2

This means that the sprinter has an acceleration of 2.5 m/s^2.

We can also use this acceleration to find the distance that the sprinter covers during these 2 seconds using the equation:

distance (s) = u*t + 1/2*a*t^2

Here, u is the initial velocity (10 m/s), t is the time taken (2 seconds), and a is the acceleration (2.5 m/s^2).

s = 10 m/s * 2 s + 1/2 * 2.5 m/s^2 * (2 s)^2

s = 20 m + 2.5 m

s = 22.5 m

So, the sprinter covers a distance of 22.5 meters during these 2 seconds.

Assuming that the acceleration of the sprinter is constant, we can use the following kinematic equation to find the distance covered by the sprinter:

v_f = v_i + a*t

where:

v_i is the initial velocity of the sprinter (10 m/s)

v_f is the final velocity of the sprinter (15 m/s)

a is the acceleration of the sprinter

t is the time elapsed (2 seconds)

To solve for a, we can rearrange the equation as follows:

a = (v_f - v_i) / t

Substituting the given values, we get:

a = (15 m/s - 10 m/s) / 2 s

a = 2.5 m/s^2

Now, we can use another kinematic equation to find the distance covered by the sprinter:

d = v_i*t + (1/2)at^2

Substituting the given values and the calculated value of a, we get:

d = 10 m/s * 2 s + (1/2) * 2.5 m/s^2 * (2 s)^2

d = 20 m + 5 m

d = 25 meters

Therefore, the sprinter covers a distance of 25 meters during the 2-second interval, assuming that the acceleration is constant.

Answer: a = 0.00167 m/h²

Explanation:

Step 1: Using the formula: a = (Sf - Si) x t

where a is the acceleration, Sf is the final speed, Si is the initial speed and t is the time.

Step 2: Substitute the given data to the equation to get the acceleration.

a = (27 m/h - 25 m/h) x 3 seconds

a = (2 m/h) x (3 seconds)

Step 3: We need to make sure that the units are the same. So we need to convert seconds to hour. 1 hr = 3600 secs

a = (2 m/h) x (3 seconds x 1 hr/3600 secs)

a = 6/3600 m/h²

Then we need to simplify our answers.

a = 1/600 m/h² (Since the GCF of 6 and 3600 is 6, we just divide it to the numerator and denominator of the final answer. We can also convert this to decimal)

a = 0.00167 m/h²
