Step-by-step explanation:

Age Word Problem

The problem is asking for Joshua and Mckay's ages. To find the answer, you have to identify the known and unknown in the problem and express them in an algebraic equation. The algebraic equation refers to the statement of equality of two algebraic expressions. The algebraic expression is a combination of operations, variables and constants.

Given:

Joshua's age - a year older than his sister Mckay

product of their ages - 33 more than 7 times of Joshua's age

Equation:

Let "x" be Mckay's age and "x + 1" be Joushua's age.

x (x + 1) = 33 + 7 (x + 1)

Solution:

x (x + 1) = 33 + 7 (x + 1)

x² + x = 33 + 7x + 7

x² + x = 40 + 7x

(Equate to 0)

x² + x - 7x - 40 = 0

x² - 6x - 40 = 0

(Factor out)

(x - 10) (x + 4)

x = 10 ; x = -4

There are two possible values of x, 10 and -4. Since the problem is asking for their ages, use the positive value of x which is 10. Therefore, Mckay is 10 years old. You can now find Joshua's age.

Joshua = x + 1

= 10 + 1

= 11

Final Answer:

Joshua is 11 years old and Mckay is 10 years old.
