4. LRT-2 train left santolan station at 8:0 a. m.and arrived at the recto station at 8:30 a.m if the distance between the two station is 13 kilometers what was the average speed of the train


To find the average speed of the train, we can use the formula:

average speed = total distance ÷ total time

In this case, the total distance is 13 kilometers (the distance between Santolan Station and Recto Station), and the total time is 30 minutes (the time it took for the train to travel from Santolan Station to Recto Station).

However, the formula for average speed requires that the distance and time are expressed in the same units. Since the distance is in kilometers and the time is in minutes, we need to convert the time to hours:

30 minutes = 30 ÷ 60 hours = 0.5 hours

Now we can substitute the values into the formula:

average speed = 13 km ÷ 0.5 hours = 26 km/h

Therefore, the average speed of the train was 26 kilometers per hour.

Note: It's important to keep in mind that this is the average speed over the entire journey from Santolan Station to Recto Station. The actual speed of the train may have varied at different points along the way depending on factors such as traffic and station stops.

7. A police station receives an average of 9 calls per hour. What is the probability that exactly 5 calls will be received by the police station in a randomly selected hour?​


[tex] \huge \mathbb{SOLUTION:} [/tex]

[tex] \!\begin{array}{l} \textsf{This problem can be solved by Poisson Distri-} \\ \textsf{bution. Note that Poisson distribution focuses} \\ \textsf{only on the number of discrete occurrences over} \\ \textsf{some interval, unlike binomial distribution which} \\ \textsf{describes a distribution of two possible outcomes} \\ \textsf{designated as successes and failures from a given} \\ \textsf{number of trials.} \end{array} [/tex]

[tex] \:\:\:\boxed{\begin{array}{l} \quad \underline{\bold{Poisson\ Distribution\ Formula}} \\ \\ \sf \quad \quad \quad \quad \large P(x) = \dfrac{{\lambda}^x e^{-\lambda}}{x!} \\ \\ \sf where: \\ \footnotesize \sf x = \sf number\ of\ successes\ over\ a\ given\ time\ or\ space \\ \footnotesize \begin{aligned} \sf \lambda =&\:\sf mean\ number\ of\ successes\ per\ time\ or\ space \\ &\sf\: unit, \lambda > 0 \end{aligned} \\ \footnotesize e = \sf Euler's\ number \approx 2.71828 \end{array}} [/tex]

[tex] \begin{array}{l} \bold{Given:}\: \begin{cases} \: \sf \lambda = 9 \\ \: \sf x = 5 \end{cases} \\ \\ \bold{Required:}\: \sf P(x = 5) \\ \\ \textsf{Substituting the given, we get} \\ \\ \sf P(x = 5) = \dfrac{9^5 \cdot e^{-9}}{5!} \approx \boxed{\sf 0.06072\ or\ 6.07\%} \end{array} [/tex]
