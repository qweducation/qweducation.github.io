4. what is the Introduction and disadvantages of The fare Hike-from ₱12 to ₱15 on jeepneys​


Introduction:

The fare hike, from ₱12 to ₱15, on jeepneys has been a topic of discussion and debate among commuters and stakeholders. It refers to the increase in the fare price that passengers have to pay for their rides. This decision is typically made by transportation regulatory bodies or local government units in response to various factors such as rising fuel costs, maintenance expenses, and the need for drivers' income augmentation.

Disadvantages:

1. Increased financial burden on commuters: The fare hike puts an additional financial burden on commuters, especially for those who heavily rely on jeepneys as their primary mode of transportation. The increased fare may strain their daily budget and affect their overall cost of living.

2. Affected livelihood of low-income individuals: The fare hike may have a significant impact on the livelihood of low-income individuals, particularly those who rely on jeepney driving as their source of income. It could lead to a decline in passengers and earnings, potentially affecting their ability to sustain their families and meet their daily needs.

3. Limited access to public transportation: With the fare increase, some commuters, particularly those with limited financial resources, may have reduced access to public transportation. This can result in difficulties in commuting to work, school, or other essential destinations, especially for marginalized communities who heavily rely on affordable transportation options.

4. Potential decrease in passenger demand: A significant fare hike may discourage passengers from using jeepneys as a mode of transportation, leading to a decrease in passenger demand. This could potentially impact the livelihoods of jeepney drivers and the sustainability of the public transportation system.

5. Environmental impact: The fare hike may inadvertently contribute to an increase in private vehicle usage as commuters seek alternative means of transportation. This could lead to higher carbon emissions, air pollution, and traffic congestion, which are detrimental to the environment and public health.
Explanation:It is important to note that the impact of fare hikes on jeepneys varies depending on the specific context and the overall transportation ecosystem of a particular area. Understanding the concerns and considerations of both commuters and jeepney operators is crucial in finding a balance between affordability, sustainability, and the welfare of all stakeholders involved.

For example number 1:

Suppose junk food is off-limits in your household and the only way of getting them is thru doing a household chore. Doing a household chore will give you points and doing more of it will give you more.

You chose washing dishes.

For a minimum of 3 things washed, whether it is a plate, a glass, a spoon or a fork, you will be given 5 points. Every additional thing that you wash will be another additional 2 points. The table below will show you the pattern.

Number of Dishes/Things Washed         Amount of Points Rewarded

                       3                                                                  5

                       7                                                                  13

Note that every increase in dishes washed corresponds to a two-point increase. Thus, this is an example of an arithmetic sequence.

For example number 2:

You may notice that everytime the minute hands of the clock rotates to the next number, the minutes increase by 5. Suppose it is currently 1:05 PM, meaning the hour hand is at 1 and the minute hand is at 1, and the next time you checked it the hour hand is still at 1 and the minute hand is now at digit 2, the time is now 1:10 PM. This means that for every rotation of the minute hand of the clock to the next digit, the minute in the clock increases by 5. Thus it is a arithmetic sequence.

For example number 3:

Suppose you want to fill a 10-Liter Jug with water using a cup which can hold 200 mL of water. Every additional cup of water you add will increase the water in the jug by 200 mL. Sarting from scratch, if you put 1 cup og water in the jug, the water level of the jug will at 200 mL. If you add another cup, you get 400 mL. Add a third cup and the total water in the jug will come up to 600 mL.  A fourth cup will make it 800 mL. This pattern continues. Thus, it is an arithmetic sequence.
