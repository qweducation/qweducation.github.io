The implementation of the Public Utility Vehicle (PUV) Modernization Program by the Land Transportation Franchising and Regulatory Board (LTFRB) has been a topic of controversy in recent years. The program aims to replace old and dilapidated jeepneys with modern, eco-friendly, and safer vehicles. However, there are concerns about the cost of the new vehicles and the impact of the program on the livelihoods of jeepney drivers and operators.

While the PUV Modernization Program is a step towards improving the quality and safety of public transportation in the country, the timing of its implementation should be carefully considered. The COVID-19 pandemic has greatly affected the economy, and many jeepney drivers and operators are still struggling to make ends meet. The high cost of the new vehicles may be too much of a burden for them to bear, especially since they are still recovering from the pandemic's impact.

In conclusion, the implementation of the PUV Modernization Program by the LTFRB should be approached with caution. While the program's goals are noble, the timing of its implementation should be reconsidered, given the current economic situation. The government should work together with the affected stakeholders to find a solution that benefits everyone, including the riding public, jeepney drivers and operators, and the environment.

30. What is difference between temporary appointment and permanent appointment?​


Answer:

A permanent position is one where there is no defined employment end date and the employee receives a benefits package. A temporary position is one that has a defined duration of employment with a contract end date.
