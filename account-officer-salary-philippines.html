7. Eric is the accountant for a trading firm. While posting entries in the books of accounts, he entered $2,500 into the salary account. However, the total salaries for the firm amount to $3,700. What type of error did Eric make?


The type of error that Eric made in the given situation was the error of commission.

When Eric incorrectly entered $2,500 into the salary account instead of entering the correct amount of $3,700 into the salary account, he made an accounting error type which is called as error of commission.

The error of commission is an accounting error wherein the transaction was recorded in the correct account but was entered as a wrong value. An example of this was the one that Eric made, and another example is subtracting a value that should have been added into the account.

There are other different common types of accounting errors that may impact the correctness of accounting books. These common accounting errors include the error of omission (example is failing to record the item), error of principle (example is recording in the incorrect account), transposition errors (example is recording with reversed digits), rounding error (example is mathematical miscalculation due to rounding), and reversal of entries (example is wrong debiting or crediting).
