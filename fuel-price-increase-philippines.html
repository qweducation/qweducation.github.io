The text presents a clear justification for the concerns of PUV operators over the increasing prices of fuel. The fluctuation in fuel prices has a significant impact on the financial viability of these operators, as they heavily rely on fuel to run their vehicles. The high cost of fuel leads to increased operating expenses, which can result in reduced profits for operators and even the cessation of operations for some PUVs.

Furthermore, the challenges of fuel prices on PUV operators are multifaceted. The direct impact of fuel prices on their operating costs leads to decreased profits, making it difficult for operators to maintain their vehicles, pay their employees, and cover other expenses. The increase in fares due to increased operating costs can also make transportation less affordable for passengers, particularly those who are already struggling to make ends meet.

The unpredictability of fuel prices also makes it difficult for PUV operators to manage their finances effectively and make long-term investments such as purchasing new vehicles or expanding their business. This text provides a strong justification for why the government should consider providing support to PUV operators to help them cope with the challenges of fuel price increases.
