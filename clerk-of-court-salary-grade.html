12. Clara Ramos works as a sales clerk. She is paid a salary of P3000.00 a week plus 2% commission an sales over P4000.00. Find her gross pay for a week in which her sales amounts to P9500.00.


The gross salary Clara received was P3.190,00.

Step-by-step explanation:

Be discovered:

Clara Ramos works as a sales clerk. She is paid a salary of P3000,00 a week plus 2% commission an sales over P4000,00.

Question:

Find her gross pay for a week in which her sales amounts to P9500,00.

Asked:

Because the number of sales exceeds P4000, Clara gets a 2% bonus from the total sales.

The first step, we look for the big bonus that Clara received.

Bonus received by Clara = Bonus percentage times the number of sales

Bonus received by Clara = 2% x P9500,00

Bonus received by Clara = P190,00

The second step is to find the amount of gross salary that Clara receives.

Clara's gross salary = base salary + bonus

Clara's gross salary = P3000,00 + P190,00

Clara's gross salary = P3.190,00

The gross salary Clara received was P3.190,00.

28. Answer by yes or no and why?In reparation, damage are paid by the convict according to the court decision, the actual damage covers the life expectancy of the victim computed by monthly salary or compensation.​


Yes, in some cases, damages can be paid by the convict according to the court's decision, and the actual damage may be computed based on the life expectancy of the victim and their monthly salary or compensation.

Here are the reasons why damages can be paid by the convict:

Reparation
It is an important aspect of the criminal justice system, as it helps to provide some level of compensation to the victim for the harm they have suffered. In some cases, this may involve paying damages to cover the cost of medical bills, lost wages, and other expenses related to the injury or harm caused by the crime.

Calculation
When calculating damages, the court may take into account the victim's life expectancy, as well as their monthly salary or compensation. This can help to ensure that the damages paid are fair and reasonable and that they adequately compensate the victim for the harm they have suffered.

Additional information:

It's worth noting that not all crimes will result in damages being paid, and the number of damages awarded may vary depending on the specific circumstances of the case.

The convict's ability to pay may also be taken into account, as the court may not want to impose an undue burden on the convict or their family.

While damages are an important aspect of the criminal justice system, they are just one part of a larger effort to ensure justice and provide support to victims of crime.
