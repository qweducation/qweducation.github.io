4. State crisis of 1986 EDSA People Power Revolution


Answer:

The 1986 EDSA People Power Revolution was a political crisis in the Philippines that began in February 1986. The crisis was sparked by allegations of fraud and corruption in the presidential election held in 1986, which was won by incumbent President Ferdinand Marcos. The opposition candidate, Corazon Aquino, challenged the results and called for protests and demonstrations.

The crisis escalated when military officials withdrew their support for Marcos and joined the opposition, creating a situation of political and social instability. Millions of Filipinos took to the streets in peaceful demonstrations, demanding Marcos's resignation and a return to democracy.

The crisis culminated in a four-day peaceful protest at the EDSA Shrine in Manila, where protesters called for the ouster of Marcos and a return to democratic rule. On February 25, 1986, Marcos fled the country with his family, and Corazon Aquino was sworn in as the new President of the Philippines.

The EDSA People Power Revolution is considered a significant event in Philippine history, as it marked the end of the Marcos dictatorship and the restoration of democracy. The crisis also had international implications, as it demonstrated the power of nonviolent protest and inspired similar movements in other countries.

Ang EDSA Revolution ng 1986 ay ang panahon kung kailan nagkaisa ang mga Pilipino upang makamit ang kalayaan at demokrasya mula sa diktaturya ni Ferdinand Marcos. Sa pamamagitan ng 1986 EDSA Revolution, naipakita sa buong mundo ang pagkakaisa ng mga Pilipino sa pagkamit ng pagbabago sa pamamagitan ng mapayapang paraan. II. Mahalaga ang pagdiriwang ng EDSA Revolution 1986 dahil sa pamamagitan nito, nagugunita ang mapayapang pagkakaisa ng mga Pilipino tungo sa pagkamit ng kalayaan at demokrasya.
