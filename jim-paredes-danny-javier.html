Flicker Fade Gone

Flicker Fade Gone is a book by Carl Joe Javier that mainly focused on the difference of living the real life and the life we wish to live in. It shows clear differences between the two and what the main character chose to live in.

Characters of Flicker Fade Gone:

Son/young boy Girl Father Store keeper/ Arcade owner Mother

Once there was a seven year old boy who used to come to the arcade with his father, the boy was quite fascinated with the world the arcade games introduces to him and how he is slowly getting used to the sounds of the gun in the game. He was somehow a normal kid, but one time he got lost in taught while starring at a mirror and got caught by his mother. He then told her that he wanted to disappear; his mother pulled him back to reality and forced him to play outside.

Although he admired playing, he didn’t play once with their neighbor’s children. They used to make fun of his clothing and push him around. He didn’t find comfort playing with real kids or neither with real people. Then, his father brings him to the arcade again there he played and got lost in the momentum of being part of another dimension and how he can easily get and be what he wanted to be.

After years had passed his parents went abroad and he discontinued living a normal life by dropping out of school and just pursuing what mostly entertains him. He got a job in the arcade store and has been working there since. He was so engrossed in being a completely different persona in each game he plays, but that changed when he met a girl.

A girl that comes on Sundays with her two brothers, she usually accompanies them. He noticed the girl and was silently looking forward to see her again. Then, a few days passed he finally saw her again but this time wearing her school uniform and was together with her classmates both girls and boys.  

He noticed that a boy’s arm was around her and was about to come up to them and save her as he usually does in games where he saves a princess. But then, the boy near the girl started calling her undesirable words and was kicked out by the owner. He then guided the girl to the back room to sit, her classmates came to pick her up. Sunday, the girl was with her brothers again; while her brothers were playing the machines stops then she calls him to fix it, the girl smiled once it was done.

The boy realized that the only way people notices him is because he was wearing the nylon strap with keys and just calls him to fix thing no more no less.

16. Who is Carl Joe Javier?


Carl Javier has worked in a number of fields, including journalism, the academe, publishing, development, and social enterprise.

In three years at Smarter Good he provided strategic thinking, writing, and editorial guidance to nonprofits and social enterprises making impact globally.  

He started writing professionally as a college freshman, and for almost two decades has contributed to various publications and outlets. His writing led to work in the publishing industry, as a Business Manager at the UST Publishing House, the Deputy Director of the University of the Philippines Press, and a number of initiatives in independent publishing. His most recent publishing accomplishments were through his work as Managing Editor of Anino Comics, which he helped to relaunch in 2014. Anino titles have won National Book Awards, Komikon Awards, and other distinctions. His comics work has been nominated for the National Book Award twice, first as co-editor of Abangan: The Best Filipino Comics and as writer of the comic book adaptation of Si Janus Silang at ang Tiyanak ng Tabon.  

Carl has authored books of fiction, essays, and poetry and contributed to a wide range of projects from technical writing like process manuals and reports, to biographies and coffee table books, to work in television and film.

He holds a BA in English Studies and an MA in Creative Writing from the University of the Philippines. He currently teaches Creative Writing at the Fine Arts Department of the Ateneo de Manila University.

21. Danny runs 6 kilometers in 2 hours. what is Danny's average speed​


Danny's average speed can be calculated by dividing the distance he ran by the time it took him to run that distance.

Average speed = distance ÷ time

In this case, Danny ran 6 kilometers in 2 hours, so his average speed is:

Average speed = 6 km ÷ 2 hours

Average speed = 3 km/hour

Therefore, Danny's average speed is 3 kilometers per hour.

25. Danny and Esther are Cathy's children. Esther is 5 years younger than Danny and Cathy is three times Danny's age. If Cathy's age 30 years ago was the same as Danny's age now, how old is Esther?


answer: Esther is 10 years old

given:

E=D-5

C=3D

C-30=D

formula: E=(C-30)-5 {{{substitution}}}

find the value for D (substitution again):

3D-30=D

2D=30

D=15

Now find the value for c using the value for D:

C=3D

C=3(15)

C=45


substitute the found value to the original formula:

E=(C-30)-5

E=(45-30)-5

E=15-5

E=10

Answer is 10
