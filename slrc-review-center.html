Police power is the inherent power of the state to regulate the affairs of its citizens in order to promote the general welfare and to protect the health, safety, and morals of the community. It is often used to regulate activities that may pose a potential harm to the public.

If the operation of review centers is seen as having the potential to harm the public, such as by providing inadequate or misleading information to students preparing for exams, then the government may have the authority to regulate their operation through the exercise of police power. 30. differentiate 3 main sorces of water​


Differentiate 3 main sources of water.

- The main sources of water are surface water, groundwater and rainwater.

Surface water is any body of water above ground, including streams, rivers, lakes, wetlands, reservoirs, and creeks. The ocean, despite being saltwater, is also considered surface water.Ground water is water underground in saturated zones beneath the land surface. It fills the pores and fractures in underground materials such as sand, gravel, and other rock.Rainwater is typically devoid of all dissolved solids but contains dissolved gases (oxides of carbon, nitrogen and sulphur) which result in pH values of about 5.5 or lower.
