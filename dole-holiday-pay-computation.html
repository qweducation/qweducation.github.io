1. what is holiday pay?​


Holiday pay is any form of alternative compensation an employer offers employees during holidays. It may take the form of fully or partially paid time off or of a bonus or additional hourly pay for work performed on a holiday.

20. Instructions: Type HO if the statement describes holiday pay; OP if overtime pay; NS if night sift differential; HZ for hazard pay; TM for thirteenth month pay and DM for de minimis benefits Mr. Ferdie works beyond the normal 8 hours. He is entitled for additional compensation.


A holiday pay is given to an employee if he works on a regular or special holiday. Overtime pay is given to an employee if he works beyond his normal eight-hour work. Hazard pay is an additional pay given to employees that are working in a job that is hazardous in nature (e.g., construction worker). Thirteenth month pay is equivalent to a month's pay that is given on or before December 24 of each year. De minimis benefits are minimal amounts of compensation such as laundry allowance, medical allowance, and the like.

Therefore, the answer is OP.
