How does accounting work? What are the steps?

Accounting is a set of steps that a business takes to keep track of its financial transactions. These steps include collecting, identifying, classifying, summarizing, and recording business transactions in the company's books of accounts so that the entity's financial statements can be made. After a set amount of time, the business's profits and financial health can be seen.

Step 1 of the accounting process is to figure out what happened.

The first step in accounting is to figure out what the business transaction was. The business entity needs to figure out what financial and money transactions have happened.

#2: Writing down the events in the journal

After figuring out what transactions have happened, the second step in accounting is to make a Journal entry for each transaction. The point at which transactions are recorded depends on the entity's accounting policy, which is either an accrual basis or a cash basis. In the accrual method of accounting, income and expenses are recorded in the books of the business in the period in which they are earned or incurred, regardless of when the cash is actually received or paid.

#3: Writing in the book

After the transaction is written down in the Journal, each account is posted to the general ledger. It lets the owner or accountant know how much money is in each account.

#4: Trial balance without any changes

The company makes a trial balance to see if the debits and credits add up to the same amount or not. The main goal of the trial balance is to find any mistakes made in the steps above.

#5: Making Changes to Journal Entries

When the accrual method of accounting is used, some of the entries must be made at the end of the accounting year. These include entries for expenses that may have been paid but are not yet in the Journal and entries for some income that the business may have made but has not yet been recorded in the books.

#6: Trial Balance with Changes

After all of the adjusting entries have been made, a trial balance must be made again before the financial statements can be made. This is to make sure that all of the credits and debits are equal after all of the adjusting entries have been made.

#7: Putting together the financial statements

After all of the above steps are done, the company's financial statements are made so that the business's actual financial position, profitability, and cash flow can be seen.

#8: Ending the Entries

This step is the last one in the accounting cycle. These entries move the balances from temporary accounts to accounts that will stay open. The accounts whose balances end in a single accounting year, like sales, purchases, expenses, etc., are called temporary accounts.

The recently held elections shows one such result of the citizens exercising the citizens’ power. The Filipinos have chosen the leaders that they want from a choice of qualified candidates both in the national and local levels. What is most notable about the recently held elections is the number of votes recorded which exceeded the votes recorded during the previous elections. The same can be said about the results for the senatorial elections.

It is important to note that the leaders elected by the citizens will be responsible for leading the country for the years to come. These leaders will be responsible for representing the Philippines in foreign relations and crafting policies to assist the Filipinos as we go through our day to day live. We can be hopeful that the leaders will do everything within their power to ensure that their actions are done with the best interest of the Philippines in mind. However, this is not to say that we must solely rely on the elected leaders to better our lives.
