6. Facts about the history making Game stop short and stock price surge that occured during January 2021​


Answer:

In January 2021, there was a significant surge in the stock price of GameStop, a video game retailer. This surge was largely driven by a group of retail investors on the subreddit forum r/wallstreetbets, who coordinated their efforts to buy GameStop stock in an attempt to squeeze out short sellers.

Short sellers are investors who bet that the price of a particular stock will decline. To execute a short sell, they borrow shares of the stock from another investor, sell the borrowed shares on the market, and then buy back the same number of shares at a lower price in the future to return to the original owner. If the price of the stock does decline, the short seller makes a profit. However, if the price of the stock increases, the short seller incurs a loss.

The retail investors on r/wallstreetbets coordinated their efforts to buy GameStop stock en masse, driving up the price and causing losses for the short sellers. This phenomenon, known as a "short squeeze," can occur when the price of a stock rises sharply, causing short sellers to buy back the stock to cover their positions and avoid further losses. This buying can in turn drive the price of the stock even higher.

The GameStop stock price surge received widespread media attention and became a topic of discussion in financial circles. The situation also prompted regulatory scrutiny and raised questions about the role of retail investors and social media in financial markets.

12. Ruling about the history making Game stop short and stock price surge that occured during January 2021 ​


Answer:

The events that occurred with GameStop in January 2021 were an example of social trading or "meme trading," where individuals on social media and other online trading forums gave confidence to companies that were being shorted by institutional traders and financial analysts. In this case, many individuals spread posts on social media containing tips and recommendations to buy GameStop and other targeted stocks.

The result of this type of social trading was a significant increase in the price of GameStop and other targeted stocks, resulting in significant losses for the institutional traders and financial analysts who were shorting the stocks. At this time, it is not clear what the long-term impact of this type of social trading will be, and there are still many questions about the legality and morality of this type of activity in the financial markets.

Some government and financial agencies have issued warnings about this type of social trading and have called on individuals to be cautious when using tips and recommendations on social media and other online trading forums. Additionally, many financial analysts have expressed their research and views on the companies targeted by individual social traders in order to provide a clearer idea to investors about their potential risks and opportunities.
