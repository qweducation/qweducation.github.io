1. In the Philippines, COMELEC or the Commission on Elections is not under in any of the Branches of the Government, explain why.​


Answer:

The Commission on Elections (COMELEC) in the Philippines is an independent constitutional commission established by the Philippine Constitution to enforce and administer all laws and regulations relative to the conduct of an election, plebiscite, initiative, referendum, and recall. As an independent commission, it is not under the executive, legislative, or judicial branch of the government, but rather operates independently of them. This independence allows the COMELEC to carry out its mandate without interference or influence from other branches of government, and helps to ensure the integrity and impartiality of the electoral process in the Philippines.

19. Ano ang pinagkaiba ng First Philippine Commission sa Second Philippine Commission?​


Answer:

The Philippine Commission was the name of two bodies, both appointed by the President of the United States, to assist with governing the Philippines.

Explanation:

The first Philippine Commission, also known as the Schurman Commission, was appointed by President William McKinley on January 20, 1899 as a recommendatory body.

The second Philippine Commission, also known as the Taft Commission, was a body appointed by the President to exercise legislative and limited executive powers in the Philippines. It was first appointed by President McKinley in 1900 under his executive authority. The Philippine Organic Act was passed by the United States Congress in 1902; this enshrined into law the Commission's legislative and executive authority. As stipulated in the Philippine Organic Act, the bicameral Philippine Legislature was established in 1907, with the Commission as the upper house and the elected Philippine Assembly acting as lower house. The Jones Act of 1916 ended the Commission, replacing it with an elected Philippine Senate as the Legislature's upper house.

22. What are the appropriate measures of the commission of election to ensure the security and honesty during the election process.


Answer:

To ensure the security and honesty of an election process, the election commission should implement measures such as ensuring its independence from political interference, transparency in its operations and decision-making, fair and accessible voter registration, voter education, secure polling stations, tamper-proof ballots, transparent and accurate vote-counting, and prompt reporting of results. These measures can help to build trust in the democratic process among voters.
