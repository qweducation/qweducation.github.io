16. Based on the following information:Net income for period P18,000.; Drawing, P56,000. Capital (January 31, 2021), P20,000. Find the Beginning Capital/Inventory of the owner on December 31, 2020.​


Answer:

To find the Beginning Capital/Inventory of the owner on December 31, 2020, you can use the following formula:

Beginning Capital/Inventory = Capital (January 31, 2021) + Net Income - Drawing

Plugging in the given values, we get:

Beginning Capital/Inventory = P20,000 + P18,000 - P56,000

Simplifying, we get:

Beginning Capital/Inventory = P20,000 + P18,000 - P56,000 = P-18,000

So the Beginning Capital/Inventory of the owner on December 31, 2020 is P-18,000.

This means that the owner has a negative net worth of P18,000 on December 31, 2020.

23. A customer check amounting to p200,000 dated january 2,2022 was included in the december 31, 2021 checkbook balance


It sounds like you are describing a situation where a customer checks with a value of P200,000 that was dated January 2, 2022, was included in the December 31, 2021 checkbook balance.

In this situation, it would be important to consider the timing of the transaction and the impact it has on the accuracy of the checkbook balance. If the check was not cashed or deposited by December 31, 2021, it would not be considered part of the checkbook balance as of that date. Instead, it would be considered a liability, since it represents an obligation to pay the amount indicated on the check at a future date.

On the other hand, if the check was cashed or deposited by December 31, 2021, it would be considered part of the checkbook balance as of that date, and the balance would be adjusted accordingly.

CHECKBOOK BALANCE

A checkbook balance is a record of the current balance in a checking account. It is typically kept in a checkbook register, which is a small notebook that is used to track the checks that have been written, as well as any other debits or credits that have been applied to the account.

To define a checkbook balance in a journal, you would need to record an entry that reflects the current balance in the account. This can be done by debiting or crediting the appropriate account, depending on the nature of the balance. For example, if the checkbook balance is a debit balance (meaning that there is a positive balance in the account), you would debit the account in the journal. On the other hand, if the checkbook balance is a credit balance (meaning that there is a negative balance in the account), you would credit the account in the journal.
