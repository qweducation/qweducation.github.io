Agus River is a river that flows for 36.5 kilometres (22.7 mi)[1][2] from Lanao Lake to Iligan Bay, Philippines. It cuts through the provinces of Lanao del Sur and Lanao del Norte. 23. Pit Senyor Santo Niño de Cebu Sulog One beat, One Dance, One Vision​


Answer:

"Pit Senyor" is a phrase commonly used during the Sinulog Festival in Cebu City, Philippines. It is a chant in honor of the Santo Niño de Cebu, which means "Hail, Lord Senior Santo Niño!" "Sulog" means "current" or "flow," and it refers to the strong current that flows through the channels of Cebu's Pahina River.

"One beat, One Dance, One Vision" is a tagline that represents the unity and solidarity of the people of Cebu during the Sinulog Festival. It symbolizes the idea that despite the diversity of cultures and backgrounds, everyone comes together to celebrate the same thing, which is the devotion to the Santo Niño de Cebu. During the festival, people dance to the beat of the drums and chant "Pit Senyor" as a sign of reverence and devotion to the Holy Child.
