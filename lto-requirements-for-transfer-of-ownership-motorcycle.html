30. A business ownership capitalization divided into hundreds or thousands of transferable shares of stock and must have at least five owners or investors.


Answer: Corporation

A corporation is the type of business ownership where it is based and represented by shares or stocks. The owner/s that has/have their own share/s on the company is/are called shareholder/s or stockholder/s. A stockholder can possess a lot of stocks or shares of the company and can transfer it to a successor or sell it to an interested investor anytime. The least number of owners or investors for the business to be considered a corporation is five (5). All shareholders only have limited liability but limited involvement at the same time (unless one is part of the board of directors who have direct involvement with the control of the company).
