6. What shall be the validity of a license with violation at the time of renewal


VALIDITY OF A LICENSE

    You are not obligated to drive if you do not have a driver's license, according to state rules. You can be a professional driver, but you are not legally required to drive if you do not have a driver's license. As a result, your driver's license certifies you as a licensed driver.

     The validity of a driver's license with a violation at the time of renewal may depend on the specific violation and the laws of the state where the license was issued. In some states, a driver's license may be suspended or revoked if the driver has certain types of violations, such as reckless driving or driving under the influence (DUI). In these cases, the driver may need to complete certain requirements, such as completing a driving course or paying a fine, before the license can be renewed.In other states, the validity of a driver's license with a violation may not be affected at the time of renewal, as long as the violation is not a serious offense that would disqualify the driver from holding a license. However, even if the license is not suspended or revoked, the driver may still be required to pay any fines or fees associated with the violation before the license can be renewed.

It is important to check with the department of motor vehicles (DMV) in your state for specific information on how violations may affect the validity of your driver's license at the time of renewal.

24. what shall be the validity of a license that a driver may have if he/she has traffic violation at the time of renewal?


VALIDITY OF A LICENSE OF A DRIVER (TRAFFIC VIOLATION)

The validity of a driver's license that has a traffic violation at the time of renewal would depend on the laws and regulations of the particular jurisdiction where the license was issued.

In some jurisdictions, the driver's license may be suspended or revoked if there are outstanding traffic violations, while in others, the license may remain valid but with certain restrictions or conditions.

In general, it is important for drivers to follow traffic laws and regulations to avoid traffic violations, as these can have consequences on the validity of their license and their ability to operate a vehicle safely.

Drivers who have received traffic violations should take steps to address the issue, such as paying fines or attending traffic school, to minimize the impact on their license validity.
