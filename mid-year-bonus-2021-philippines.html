7. Employees at Acme Corporation earn an average of $20,000 per year, with a range of $470,000. Every employee will receive the following bonuses this year.  A Christmas bonus of $650.  An incentive bonus equal to 13 percent of the employee's salary. What is the employee bonus’ standard deviation?​


To calculate the standard deviation of the employee bonus, we need to first calculate the bonus amount for each employee.

The incentive bonus for each employee is equal to 13% of their salary, so we can calculate this as:

Incentive Bonus = 0.13 * Salary

Then, we can calculate the total bonus amount for each employee by adding the Christmas bonus of $650 to the incentive bonus:

Total Bonus = Christmas Bonus + Incentive Bonus

Total Bonus = $650 + 0.13 * Salary

Now, we can calculate the bonus amount for each employee in the range of $470,000 as follows:

Minimum bonus = $650 + 0.13 * $20,000 = $3,650

Maximum bonus = $650 + 0.13 * $470,000 = $61,450

The range of bonus amounts is therefore $61,450 - $3,650 = $57,800.

To calculate the standard deviation of the bonus amounts, we can use the formula:

Standard Deviation = Square root of (Σ (X - μ)² / N)

where Σ (X - μ)² is the sum of the squared differences between each bonus amount and the mean bonus amount, and N is the total number of employees.

Since we don't know the number of employees, we can't calculate the exact standard deviation. However, we can estimate it by assuming that the distribution of bonus amounts is roughly normal. In this case, we can use the empirical rule to estimate that about 68% of the bonus amounts will fall within one standard deviation of the mean bonus amount.

Using this assumption, we can estimate that the standard deviation of the bonus amounts is approximately:

Standard Deviation ≈ Range of Bonuses / 6

Standard Deviation ≈ $57,800 / 6 ≈ $9,633.33

Therefore, the estimated standard deviation of the employee bonuses is approximately $9,633.33.

What is Simple Interest and Compound Interest?

The straightforward interest calculation makes figuring out how much interest will be applied to a loan quick and uncomplicated. Multiply the principle, the number of days between payments, and the daily interest rate to get simple interest. Although some mortgages use this technique of calculation, this kind of interest is typically connected to auto loans or short-term loans.

Simple interest is calculated by multiplying the principle by the daily interest rate and the number of days between payments. Simple interest rewards borrowers for making on-time or early monthly payments on their loans. Auto loans and short-term personal loans are two common uses for simple interest loans.

There is also the option for simple or compound interest. Simple interest is calculated using the initial principal of a loan or deposit. Contrarily, compound interest is determined by taking into account both the initial principal and the interest that is accrued over time. Because it just takes the principal into account when doing computations, simple interest is easier to calculate than compound interest.

Compound interest frequently plays a role in real-world commercial deals, investments, and financial items meant to last for a number of months or years. Simple interest is mostly used for straightforward computations, typically those that span one period or less than a year. For long-term situations like credit card bills, simple interest also applies.

26. Mid year acquisition consolidated financial statements

Sometimes, the parent may not acquire the subsidiary at the start of the financial year. Acquisition may occur during the year . i.e. March 1, October 31, July 1, etc. This is called mid-year acquisition.

If it is a mid-year acquisition, instead of adding all of the income and expenses of the parent and subsidiary company, you add all of the parent's income and expenses to the time-apportioned income and expenses of the subsidiary.
