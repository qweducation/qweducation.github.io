Flores de Mayo (Spanish for "flowers of May") is a festival held in the Philippines in the month of May. It is one of the May devotions to the Blessed Virgin Mary and lasts for the entire year.

The Santacruzan (from the Spanish santa cruz, "holy cross") is the ritual pageant held on the last day of the Flores de Mayo. It honors the finding of the True Cross by Helena of Constantinople (known as Reyna Elena) and Constantine the Great. Its connection with May stems from the May 3 date of Roodmas, which Pope John XXIII deleted in 1960 due to the trend at the time to abolish holy days. The Feast of the Exaltation of the Cross on September 14, which commemorates the recovery of the relic by Emperor Heraclius from the Persians instead of the finding by Saint Helen was all that was retained in the calendar of the New Order.

17. The festival celebration in Negros to encourage people to fight back the sugar crisis in the province. A. Ati-Atihan B. Kaamulan C. Masskara D. Santacruzan


Answer:

MassKara festival

Explanation:

The MassKara festival is an annual festival in Bacolod held every 4th Sunday of October. The word MassKara is a combination of mass (people) and cara (face in Spanish). A highlight in the festival are masks decorated with smiling faces, from which the City of Bacolod got is moniker as City of Smiles. The festival started in the 1980s because of sugar crises. Sugar from sugarcane is the primary source of income in Negros but when high-fructose corn syrup entered as sugar substitute, the Negrense economy went down. To cheer up the people and to encourage them to fight the crisis, the then mayor allocated funds to have the city's business and artists hold a festival of smile. Hence, the MassKara festival was born.
