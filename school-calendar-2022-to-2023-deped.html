3. summarize the sample news report three to five sentences news report-deped sets opening of school year 2022-2023 on August 22​


Answer:

The Department of Education (DepEd) on Friday said it is ready for the scheduled opening of classes on Aug. 22.

"All systems go na tayo for the opening of classes," DepEd spokesperson Michael Poa said in a press conference.

Poa said a total of 24,175 public and private schools nationwide or 46 percent will implement five-day face-to-face classes, while 29,721 schools or about 51.8 percent will implement blended learning modality.

Meanwhile, the remaining 1.29 percent or about 1,004 schools will conduct distance learning.

Poa, citing Aug. 19 DepEd data, said the current total enrollment on the basic education level is 27 million learners, which is 96 percent of the agency's target of 28.6 million.

DepEd Undersecretary Epimaco Densing III earlier said schools will be given options of in-person, blended, or full-distance learning only until Oct. 31.

After this, all enrolled students will be required to attend face-to-face classes, under DepEd Order (DO) 034, series of 2022.

"This is mandatory, they are all required to go to school and the mandate is really to eventually go face-to-face. Everybody who's enrolled should abide by the Department Order, parents are also encouraged to ensure that their students go to school. Again, we're trying to do some educational recovery," he said.

According to the same DO 034, there are 203 days for Academic Year 2022-2023 for public schools, which will begin on Aug. 22, 2022 and will end on July 7, 2023.

10. What advise could you give to yourself as a motivation for school year 2022-2023? ​


Answer:

1. Remind yourself why are you doing this

The best way to motivate yourself is to remain yourself why you are doing this and what do you want from a task. So when you need a little boost and a push to start doing something or complete what you are doing, you just remind yourself what is that you really want and why you started this in the first place.

You can also write down your goal and the reason you want to complete a task and stick it somewhere you can see that every day or till that task is completed for example your desk or even on your fridge!

2. Break your goal into the smaller task and make a plan

The worst thing people can make is not making a plan and not making a strategy. See the thing is if your task or goal requires a longer time to complete than it’s easy to get lost midway and that should not happen.

So in order to keep yourself motivated throughout your journey, you should make a plan and break the goal into smaller tasks. This way you will be aware of how much you need to do and how much time it’s going to take. And this will also help you to come up with a plan.

When you break the goal into smaller tasks, it will feel the task is much simpler and doable so you can easily get moving and start doing things without procrastinating.

And this doesn’t just apply for the bog goals, you can also make a plan for a school project, a file you need to complete for office in a day, etc.

3. Come up with a reward system

The best way to keep yourself motivated is to come up with a reward system. So each time you complete your goal, you reward yourself. The reward can be anything from a piece of chocolate cake to a new episode of any series. But make sure you keep your rewards limited so that doesn’t come between your task and make you procrastinate your next project or task.

When you reward yourself it gives you a satisfaction that you have completed your work and this will definitely keep you motivated to move on to the next step!

4. Take a few breaks in between

No matter how much work you are capable of doing in one go, in the end, you are a human who needs breaks. So don’t be too harsh on yourself and make sure that there are enough breaks in between your work.

The thing is, if you work constantly, you might be able to complete the work faster but it will make you less productive. So try to take smaller breaks in between, for example, take 5 minutes off for every hour you work, schedule your break before you start your task.
