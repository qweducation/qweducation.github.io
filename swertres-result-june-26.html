A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

Where:

A is the total amount to be paid (including principal and interest)

P is the principal amount of the loan (P45,000 in this case)

r is the interest rate (14% in this case)

n is the number of times the interest is compounded per year (semi-annually in this case, so n=2)

t is the number of years for which the loan is taken out (4 in this case, since the loan was taken out on December 26, 2006 and the due date is June 26, 2010)

Plugging these values into the formula, we get:

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

= 45,000(1 + 0.14/2)^(2*4)

= 45,000(1 + 0.07)^8

= 45,000(1.4887)

= P67,988.50

n is the number of times the interest is compounded per year

t is the number of years between the loan date and the due date
