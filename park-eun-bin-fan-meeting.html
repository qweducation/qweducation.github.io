A Raising in the Sun, by Lorraine Hansberry, is a play about the Youngers, a family of African Americans living in Chicago in the 1950s. The play focuses on the Younger family's struggles to make ends meet and their dreams of finding the American Dream. Through their story, Hansberry speaks to ideas of race, class, and the American Dream and the obstacles that African Americans face in achieving it.

The major themes of the play are ambition and opportunity. Walter Lee Younger, the head of the family, has ambitions of becoming a successful business owner, but is limited by his lack of capital. He is frustrated with the lack of opportunities afforded to African Americans, and he feels that the only way to climb the economic ladder is to take a risk. As the play progresses, Walter's decisions become more and more desperate, culminating in a plan to use his insurance money to invest in a liquor store.
