8. how normal balance affect the creating of account​


Answer:

In accounting, the normal balance of an account is the side of the account where increases are recorded. The normal balance is important because it affects the creation of an account and how transactions are recorded in that account.

There are two types of normal balances: debit and credit.

Debit Normal Balance: An account with a debit normal balance is increased with a debit entry and decreased with a credit entry. Common examples of accounts with a debit normal balance include assets and expenses.

Credit Normal Balance: An account with a credit normal balance is increased with a credit entry and decreased with a debit entry. Common examples of accounts with a credit normal balance include liabilities, equity, and revenues.

When creating a new account, it is important to determine the normal balance of the account so that transactions are recorded correctly. This is because the normal balance of an account affects the balance of the account and, ultimately, the financial statements.

For example, if an account has a debit normal balance, a debit entry will increase the balance of the account and a credit entry will decrease the balance. On the other hand, if an account has a credit normal balance, a credit entry will increase the balance of the account and a debit entry will decrease the balance.
