7. ano ang tungkulin ng prc


The Professional Regulation Commission, (Filipino: Komisyon sa Regulasyon ng mga Propesyon) otherwise known as the PRC, is a three-man commission attached to Department of Labor and Employment (DOLE). Its mandate is to regulate and supervise the practice of the professionals (except lawyers, who are handled by the Supreme Court of the Philippines) who constitute the highly skilled manpower of the country. As the agency-in-charge of the professional sector, the PRC plays a strategic role in developing the corps of professionals for industry, commerce, governance, and the economy.

22. An accounts receivable is worth 150,000 by the beginning of the year 2022. Credit sales for 2022 is worth 2,000,000. How much would be credited to Allowance for uncollectible accounts by the end of the year is there is a 12% chance that this receivable will not be collected? Use ending accounts receivable balance for the estimation.


Answer:

To calculate the estimated amount of allowance for uncollectible accounts, we can use the percentage of credit sales method. This method estimates the amount of bad debt expense by applying a percentage to the total credit sales.

Here are the steps to calculate the estimated allowance for uncollectible accounts:

Calculate the credit sales for the year 2022:

Credit Sales = $2,000,000

Determine the estimated percentage of credit sales that will not be collected:

Estimated Percentage of Uncollectible Accounts = 12%

Calculate the estimated amount of uncollectible accounts:

Estimated Uncollectible Accounts = Credit Sales x Estimated Percentage of Uncollectible Accounts

Estimated Uncollectible Accounts = $2,000,000 x 12%

Estimated Uncollectible Accounts = $240,000

Calculate the ending accounts receivable balance:

Ending Accounts Receivable Balance = $150,000 (Given)

Subtract the estimated uncollectible accounts from the ending accounts receivable balance to get the estimated net realizable value:

Estimated Net Realizable Value = Ending Accounts Receivable Balance - Estimated Uncollectible Accounts

Estimated Net Realizable Value = $150,000 - $240,000

Estimated Net Realizable Value = -$90,000

Since the estimated net realizable value is negative, this suggests that there may be a need to credit the allowance for uncollectible accounts for the year. In other words, we need to set aside an allowance for the uncollectible accounts to reflect the expected losses from the credit sales.

Credit the allowance for uncollectible accounts by the estimated amount of uncollectible accounts:

Allowance for Uncollectible Accounts = $240,000

Therefore, we would need to credit the allowance for uncollectible accounts by $240,000 by the end of the year to reflect the expected losses from the credit sales.

Ending accounts receivable balance = $150,000 - (0.12 x $150,000)

Ending accounts receivable balance = $132,000

Now, we can use the percentage of credit sales method to estimate the amount to be credited to Allowance for uncollectible accounts:

Estimated uncollectible accounts = Percentage of credit sales x Ending accounts receivable balance

Estimated uncollectible accounts = 5% x $132,000
