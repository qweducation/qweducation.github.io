12. list 5 Philippine government / agency that involved in the communicable diseases ​


Answer:

1.Department of Health (DOH) - The DOH is the primary agency of the Philippine government responsible for the promotion and protection of the health of the Filipino people. It plays a key role in the prevention and control of communicable diseases through programs such as immunization, disease surveillance, and outbreak response.

2.Research Institute for Tropical Medicine (RITM) - The RITM is a research and training center under the DOH that focuses on tropical and infectious diseases. It provides laboratory support for the diagnosis and surveillance of communicable diseases, including COVID-19, HIV/AIDS, and tuberculosis.

3.National Epidemiology Center (NEC) - The NEC is a division of the DOH responsible for disease surveillance and epidemiological investigation. It collects and analyzes data on communicable diseases to support public health decision-making and response.

4.Philippine Red Cross (PRC) - The PRC is a humanitarian organization that provides disaster response, health services, and blood services. It plays a key role in the prevention and control of communicable diseases through programs such as blood safety, community health, and disaster response.

5.Philippine National Police (PNP) - The PNP is the national police force of the Philippines, responsible for law enforcement and public safety. It plays a role in the prevention and control of communicable diseases by enforcing quarantine measures and ensuring compliance with public health regulations during outbreaks.

Roles:  They collaborate to share information and investigate any violations of consumer law in order to defend consumers' collective interests.

Other Information: The CPC is a gathering of national bodies responsible for enforcing consumer protection legislation across the European Union (EU), Iceland, and Norway.

Regulation 2006/2004 on cooperation between national authorities responsible for enforcing consumer protection legislation serves as the Network's legal foundation (the Regulation on Consumer Protection Cooperation).

4. European Consumer Centre Network (ECC-Net)

Roles:

The network's goal is to give consumers with free aid and advise on cross-border transactions, whether online or in person, inside these 30 nations.

ECCs are funded jointly by the European Commission and national governments as part of a European objective to help every European citizen benefit from the single market.

Roles: The goal of FIAGC is to foster member collaboration by exchanging information and experiences on all areas of mutual interest so that public policies for consumer protection may be developed and implemented efficiently.

delivering accurate information and redress to customers;putting an end to rogue traders and unjust business practices;ensuring that only safe products are sold.

29. What agencies are in-charge of implementing the intellectual property code?


The agency in-charge of implementing the Intellectual Property Code or Republic Act No. 8293 is the Intellectual Property Office. It is headed by a Director General who is assisted by two (2) Deputy Director Generals.  The Intellectual Property Office is divided into six (6) bureaus. Each bureau is headed by a Director who is assisted by an Assistant Director. These bureaus are:

Bureau of Patents; Bureau of Trademarks; Bureau of Legal Affairs; Documentation, Information and Technology Transfer Bureau; Management Information System and EDP Bureau; and Administrative, Financial and Personnel Services Bureau.

Functions of the Intellectual Property Office

Examine applications for grant of patent for inventions and register utility models and industrial designs; Examine applications for the registration of marks, geographic indication, and integrated circuits; Register technology transfer arrangements, implement strategies to facilitate technology transfer, and settle disputes involving technology transfer payments; Promote the use of patent information as a tool for technology development; Publish approved patents, marks, utility models, and industrial designs as well as registered technology transfer arrangements regularly in its own publication; Settle contested proceedings affecting intellectual property rights; and Coordinate efforts with other government agencies and the private sector to formulate and implement plans and policies.

30. list 5 agencies under the social work and write their responsibilities​


1. Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD) - responsible for the implementation of social welfare and development programs and services for the disadvantaged and marginalized sectors of society, such as elderly, women, children, persons with disabilities, homeless, and displaced individuals and families.

2. National Council on Disability Affairs (NCDA) - responsible for the development and implementation of policies, programs, and services for persons with disabilities, including their access to education, employment, health care, social protection, and participation in decision-making processes.

3. National Youth Commission (NYC) - responsible for the promotion and protection of the rights and welfare of Filipino youth, especially in areas such as education, employment, health care, social participation, and leadership development.

4. National Commission on Indigenous Peoples (NCIP) - responsible for protecting and promoting the rights and well-being of indigenous peoples in the Philippines, including their ancestral domains, cultural heritage, and political representation.

5. Bureau of Jail Management and Penology (BJMP) - responsible for ensuring the safe and humane management of jails and detention centers in the country, including the provision of basic needs, such as food, shelter, and medical care, as well as rehabilitation and reintegration services for inmates.
