20. mang obet invested his money ti an account the pays 6% compounded quarterly. if he deposits Php 1000 at the end of each month, how much will be in the account after 15 years?​


To find out how much money Mang Obet will have in his account after 15 years, we need to first calculate the interest that his money will earn each quarter. If the account pays 6% interest compounded quarterly and Mang Obet deposits Php 1000 each month, that means that at the end of each quarter (3 months), he will have deposited a total of 1000 * 3 = Php 3000. We can then calculate the interest that his money will earn by multiplying the total amount he has deposited by the interest rate: 3000 * 0.06 = Php 180.

Next, we need to find out how many quarters are in 15 years. Since there are 4 quarters in a year, 15 years is equal to 15 * 4 = 60 quarters.

Now that we know how much interest Mang Obet's money will earn each quarter and how many quarters are in 15 years, we can calculate the total amount he will have in his account after 15 years. We can do this by adding the interest earned each quarter to the amount deposited, and then multiplying that total by the number of quarters in 15 years: (3000 + 180) * 60 = Php 216000.

Therefore, after 15 years, Mang Obet will have Php 216000 in his account.
