Regarding DepEd Memorandum No. 44 and 45, they are both related to the implementation of the K to 12 Basic Education Program in the Philippines.

DepEd Memorandum No. 44, series of 2017, provides guidelines on the conduct of the K to 12 Basic Education Program End-of-School-Year Rites for Grade 6 Completers and Grade 12 Completers. This memorandum sets the guidelines for the graduation ceremonies of Grade 6 and Grade 12 students, which includes the recognition of the students' achievements and the roles of school officials, teachers, parents, and other stakeholders in the ceremony.

DepEd Memorandum No. 45, series of 2017, on the other hand, provides guidelines on the conduct of the K to 12 Basic Education Program End-of-School-Year Rites for Kindergarten, Grades 4 to 10, and Grade 11 Completers. This memorandum sets the guidelines for the moving-up ceremonies of Kindergarten and Grades 4 to 10, and the recognition ceremonies of Grade 11 students, which includes the roles of school officials, teachers, parents, and other stakeholders in the ceremony.

Both memoranda are important for the successful implementation of the K to 12 Basic Education Program in the Philippines, as they provide guidelines for the conduct of the end-of-school-year rites for different levels of students, which are crucial in recognizing their achievements and milestones in their academic journey.

23. Research the list of Special Education Centers in each regions of the Philippines.1. What is the importance of Deped Memorandum No. 44 and 45?please answer your own opinion sentence. thanks ​


DepEd Memorandum No. 44 and 45 were issued to provide guidance and support for the implementation of the Special Education (SPED) program in public schools in the Philippines. Specifically, DepEd Memorandum No. 44, s. 2005 provides the policies and guidelines on the implementation of the SPED program, while DepEd Memorandum No. 45, s. 2005 provides the guidelines for the establishment and administration of SPED centers.

The importance of these memoranda lies in their role in ensuring that students with disabilities receive equal access to quality education in public schools. By providing guidelines and policies for the implementation and administration of SPED programs, DepEd aims to address the needs of students with disabilities and promote their inclusion in the mainstream education system. This helps to ensure that all students, regardless of their abilities, have the opportunity to reach their full potential and contribute to society.
