Problem:

Jordan purchased a computer by obtaining a simple interest loan. The computer costs Php 48,000 and the interest rate on the loan is 12%. If the loan is to be paid back in monthly installments for 2 years, how much will he pay monthly?

Given:

Interest earned (I) = 48,000

Simple Interest rate (R) = 12% or 0.12

Time (T) = 2 years

Principal (P) or amount invested = unknown

Solution:

Solve for P using the formula, the amount invested:

l = PRT48,000 = P(0.12)(2)48,000 = P(0.24)48,000 = 0.24P0.24P/0.24 = 48,000/0.24P = 200,000

Answer:

∴ Therefore, Jordan pay monthly was 200,000.

12. A 45000 loan at 10% compounded monthly is to be amorized every moth in a year. compute monthly payment


4, 125

The total amount that the borrower needs to pay every month is 4, 125. If the principal amount is raised to 10%, the total payable would go up to 49, 500. Divide the total payable by 12 months of the year and that's how we get the monthly payment.

Explanation:

If a person borrows 45 000 and agrees to have 10% interest on the principal amount, we can calculate the total responsibility that he needs to pay in a year. The calculation should be:

(45 000) * (0.10) = 4 500

this means that 4 500 is the interest that should be added to the principal amount.

(45 000) + (4 500) = 49 500

This means that in 12 months, the total amount that the borrower will pay is 49 500. We just need to divide the total by 12 so we can get how much he needs to pay every month.

(49 500) / (12) = 4 125

The regular payment R
