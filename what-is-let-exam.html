8. Suppose your grades on three exams are 80,93, and 91, what grade will you need on your next exam to have an average of 90 on the four exams?


Hello, reapthereaper2322! I will be answering your question today...

---------------------------------------------------------

Your question is:

Suppose your grades on three exams are 80, 93, and 91. What grade will you need on your next exam to have an average of 90 on the four exams?

We will use the formula for calculating the mean average to solve for x where x is the required grade on the next exam to have an average of 90 on all the four exams.

[tex] \large \text{Average} = \frac{\text{sum of the values}}{\text{number of the values}} [/tex]

[tex] 90 = \frac{ 80 + 93 + 91 + x }{4} [/tex]

[tex] 4( 90 = \frac{ 80 + 93 + 91 + x }{4}) 4 [/tex]

[tex] 360 = 80 + 93 + 91 + x [/tex]

[tex] 360 - 80 - 93 - 91 = x [/tex]

[tex] \large \green{x = 96} [/tex]

Therefore, you will need a grade of at least 96 on your last exam to have an average of at least 90 on all the four exams.
