6. Are private school teachers required a license? What about pre-school teachers, ALC teachers, vocational teachers, school heads, Education Supervisors and School Division Superintendents? ​


Answer:

You are packing a lot into this question. A lot of this is dependant upon jurisdictions, but generally speaking, private schools do not require state licensing of any kind. The school’s administrator might have an MBA as opposed to a PHD in Education Management, and they might not hold a credential in School Administration. It’s possible for the teacher at this private school to have a PHD in their respective field, say Physics, but not hold a “single subject teaching credential”.

Does having a particular license make a person a better school teacher or administrator? I would argue “no” on the surface. Experience in the field matters more than a license.

I hold a single-subject credential for English and a Master’s in Curriculum Design, but I have colleagues who have neither but are phenomenal teachers.

Teaching is more about personality than it is about licenses.

At the state level, a person must hold a state-license to perform a particular task, but that does not make them a better educator.

VALIDITY OF A LICENSE OF A DRIVER (TRAFFIC VIOLATION)

The validity of a driver's license that has a traffic violation at the time of renewal would depend on the laws and regulations of the particular jurisdiction where the license was issued.

In some jurisdictions, the driver's license may be suspended or revoked if there are outstanding traffic violations, while in others, the license may remain valid but with certain restrictions or conditions.

In general, it is important for drivers to follow traffic laws and regulations to avoid traffic violations, as these can have consequences on the validity of their license and their ability to operate a vehicle safely.

Drivers who have received traffic violations should take steps to address the issue, such as paying fines or attending traffic school, to minimize the impact on their license validity.
