2. Article related to team "new frontiers of service"


An article discussing a policy brief was released by the Economic Research Institute for ASEAN and East Asia on December 5, 2019. This article discusses the importance of value chains in the service industry.

When we mention value chains, it almost always pertains to the manufacturing industry where raw materials go through a chain of production where it gains more value as it is transformed into a new product.

The service industry also has value chains where instead of raw materials or products, manpower, data, and information travel down the chain and gain more value.

The value chain of the service industry in the ASEAN and East Asia region is a strong economic driver especially since many western companies have turned to offshore outsourcing of operations such as clerical work, customer service, IT, and even finance-related work.

In the Philippines, this is what we commonly refer to as Business Process Outsourcing or BPO. It is not exclusive to call centers as BPO companies provide more than call attendance.

What we do not realize is that we also have other service-oriented value chains other than BPOs. In the Philippines, we have the SIA Engineering Corporation in Pampanga where local and international aircraft are sent for maintenance and repair. There are others too in other industries such as education, finance, and technology.

These chains bring more business to the ASEAN and East Asian regions. This, in turn, should trigger more economic and local policies to encourage and preserve business. Manpower has always been a strong suit in Asian countries. Moreover, technological advances should also be encouraged to produce more valuable services that a region can offer.

14. production possihities frontier


The production possibility frontier (PPF) is a curve depicting all maximum output possibilities for two goods, given a set of inputs consisting of resources and other factors. The PPF assumes that all inputs are used efficiently.

Factors such as labor, capital and technology, among others, will affect the resources available, which will dictate where the production possibility frontier lies. The PPF is also known as the production possibility curve or the transformation curve.

Manila Contemporary was an art gallery and an independent Filipino theatre company at Whitespace, #2314 Chino Roces Avenue (former Pasong T a m o Extension), Barangay Magallanes, Makati City, Philippines. (I am not sure if they are still operating though.) Manila Contemporary was known for being the company that produced Filipino plays, art exhibits, and art performances that also helped and presented Leeroy New in his solo exhibition entitled "Gates of Hell".

 

The exhibit was presented at the main gallery of the said theater company. Gates of Hell’s artist was known for his interdisciplinary practice, large-scale otherworldly landscape, and iconic cast of eccentric creatures such as aliens and monsters.

The Gates of Hell of Leeroy New was described as “A Product of His Generation’s  fascination with pop-culture, graphic novels, science-fictions or sci-fi as well as industrial designs.”

Leeroy New artworks intended to re-articulate the idea of peculiarities and unusualness with his very own brand of mythology. It was purely aesthetic, surreal, and void of usual fear and anxiety invoked by his created creatures.

Leeroy apparently focused on the arbitrary manipulations of the existing clichés and his ingenious and imaginative use of bizarre materials.

30. Why is it called frontier technology?


Answer:

Frontier Technology initially included drones, space, augmented reality, and virtual reality, but the list is changing and growing and includes things like autonomous vehicles.

Frontier technologies are defined as potentially disruptive technologies that can address large-scale challenges or opportunities. Frontier technology is the next phase in the evolution of modern technology. It is the intersection where radical forward-thinking and real-world implementation meet.
