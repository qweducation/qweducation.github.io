2. Learning Task No. 3: Given the beefsteak recipe and its estimated cost, compute for the break-even price and impose a 50% mark up to determine the selling price of your product. 1 kl. Beef 350.00 Soy sauce 10.00 onion 10.00 12.00 garlic 3.00 Black pepper 2.00 Salt​


Answer:

To compute for the break-even price, we need to add up the cost of all the ingredients:

Cost = Beef + Soy sauce + Onion + Garlic + Black pepper + Salt

Cost = 350.00 + 10.00 + 10.00 + 12.00 + 3.00 + 2.00

Cost = 387.00

To impose a 50% mark up, we need to compute for half of the cost:

Half of Cost = 0.5 x 387.00

Half of Cost = 193.50

To get the selling price, we need to add the half of the cost to the original cost:

Selling Price = Cost + Half of Cost

Selling Price = 387.00 + 193.50

Selling Price = 580.50

Therefore, the break-even price is 387.00 and the selling price with a 50% mark up is 580.50.

24. After an increase of 20%the new price of beef is160 per kilo. What was theoriginal Price?​


Answer:

After an increase of 20%, the new price of beef is 160 per kilo. What was the original price?

The original price of the beef per kilo is 133.

Step-by-step explanation:

What is asked?

What was the original price?

What are given?

percentage increased - 20%

new price - 160

Formula:

Finding the Original Price After an Increased

To get the original price if there is an increase occured, we need to do the following steps:

Add a hundred to the percentage increased.Divide the new price by the answer from the first step.Multiply the answer by 100.

Solution:

100 + 20 = 120

160 ÷ 120 = 1.33

1.33 × 100 = 133

Final Answer:

The original price of the beef per kilo is 133.

BREAK-EVEN AND MARK-UP PRICE

How to calculate markup percentage?

Markup percentage = (markup / cost) x 100.

Markup = selling price - cost.

Markup percentage (quotient) = (markup / cost)

(Quotient) x 100.

The value that a seller adds to a product's cost price is referred to as mark up. The term "mark-up" refers to the added value. The cost price plus the markup typically equals the retail price.
