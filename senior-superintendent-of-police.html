5. Research on the contemporary issues, conventions, regulations and codes in ship superintendence?


Explanation:

1. Safety: Ship superintendents must ensure that all safety regulations are followed and that the vessel is in compliance with all applicable laws and regulations. This includes ensuring that the vessel is properly maintained, crew members are adequately trained, and safety equipment is in good working order.

2. Environmental Protection: Ship superintendents must ensure that the vessel is operated in a manner that minimizes environmental impacts. This includes following all applicable laws and regulations related to air emissions, water discharges, and waste management.

3. Security: Ship superintendents must ensure that the vessel is operated in a secure manner at all times. This includes following all applicable laws and regulations related to security, such as those related to port security, cargo security, and crew security.

4. Quality Assurance: Ship superintendents must ensure that the vessel is operated in a manner that meets or exceeds industry standards for quality assurance. This includes ensuring that the vessel is properly maintained, crew members are adequately trained, and safety equipment is in good working order.

5. Regulatory Compliance: Ship superintendents must ensure that the vessel complies with all applicable laws and regulations related to maritime operations, such as those related to navigation, communications, pollution prevention, labor standards, etc.
