14. Annika’s school counselor has suggested that Annika may have low self-esteem. What can Annika do to improve her self-esteem? avoid making her own choices try to compare herself to others set healthy personal boundaries make a list of her least favorite qualities


If Annika has low self-esteem according to the school counselor, it is best for Annika to set set healthy personal boundaries. She should not try to compare herself to others. She also should not avoid making her own choices or listing her least-favorite qualities. This is to ensure that she slowly builds her self-confidence and how she sees herself. This is going to be a long process but it can be done.

What are the ways for building self-esteem?

We are always told to be confident of ourselves. But this is not usually an easy task. Many people, especially the extroverts tend to be natural at being confident. Introverts, however, are usually challenged in being confident.  

The first step is to acknowledge and accept that you are lacking in this aspect of your life. Once you recognize this, you will have a starting point. As you go about your daily life, at the start of the day, look in the mirror and provide yourself with positive affirmations. There is no one else who can validate you other than yourself. Never seek validation and affirmation from others. Starting the day with positive thoughts and small goal you want to achieve can go a long way.   Identify the things that you are good at and then harness and develop those skills. We are all good at something--and bad at many other things. Do not focus on what you cannot do, rather focus on what you can. List them down and then start doing them and teaching these skills to others as well. IN teaching others, you gain self-confidence. If people compliment you, accept them. Do not be resistant. Certain people will see the efforts you are putting in and will praise you for it. Thank them. Do not overly criticize yourself. Our number one enemy is usually ourselves. Let this not be the case. Love yourself. Affirm yourself and at times, reward yourself for a job well done.
