20. Case Study: Nancy is a self- supporting student taking up nursing. She is working part time in the private hospital. She is about to graduate this semester. At the end of the semester she was assigned to handle additional work in the hospital that demanded her stay late at night. Nancy needed to have the work no matter how inconvenient it was for her studies, because it would land her a regular and fulltime job in the hospital. In the night before the final exam, she was not able to study her lesson in the philosophy subject due to heavy work. In the morning she entered the room very tired and sleepless. If Nancy failed this exam, she would not pass the subject and would not graduate. Nancy cheated on the exam by opening her book and looking at the answer of her seatmate! She passed the exam through cheating! Nancy graduated and she is now working in the hospital as regular employee. Analysis of the Human act: Act: cheating for good reason Agent: Recipient: Place: Time: Manner:


Answer:

Analysis of the human act

Act: Nancy cheated on the exam

Agent: Nancy

Recipient: Nancy’s instructor on Philosophy subject

Place: School  

Time: Morning, during exam

Manner: Opening her book and looking at the answer of her seatmate

Please look anything in this scenario relate to the following topic. Explain the scenario to each topic

The morality of human act:  

Nancy cheated on the exam. Nancy clearly acts freely in consequence of her judgment of conscience.  

The moral object:  

Cheating. Nancy cheated by opening her book and looking at her seatmate’s answer.  

Intention:  

Nancy cheated because she wants to pass the exam, graduate, and land a regular full-time job at the hospital.  

Circumstances:  

Nancy was working late the night before her final exam. She came home in the morning very tired and sleepless and she couldn’t study for her final exam. Nancy then resorted to cheating during the exam.  

Indirect Voluntary actions:

Nancy Cheated on the exam because she was very tired, she didn’t have sleep from working overnight at the hospital so she couldn’t study her lessons. She wants to graduate and land a full-time job at the hospital to probably help her family, so she cheated during the exam to pass the subject. Nancy could have studied her Philosophy subject in advance when she knew she’ll be working late on the night before the exam.

Responsibility

Although Nancy was very tired from work, she could have studied the subject in advance, summarized the lessons, and studied them during her breaks. This way she could pass the subject without cheating.  

Merit:

Nancy’s action is morally wrong thus it should merit a punishment. Nancy should admit her wrongdoing and re-take the exam and work hard to achieve what she wants to achieve.  

Utilitarianism:

Nancy got a regular job at the hospital because she passed the exam through cheating and was able to graduate. It brought her good things because she’ll be able to help her family. It does not affect other people negatively, but Nancy will be forever haunted by her conscience.  

Justification:

Nancy is a hardworking student. She’s supporting herself in terms of her studies. She worked late at night and came home very tired in the morning of her exam day. Nancy could have studied in advance or she could have found time to study in between her break at work. Studying isn’t a very hard thing to do. If you will to pass your subjects, you find time to study your lessons. What Nancy did was morally wrong.

30. On her first anatomy and physiology exam, Heather defined homeostasis as "the condition in which the body approaches room temperature and stays there." Do you agree with Heather’s definition?


Answer:

I do not agree with Heather’s definition of homeostasis because, by definition, homeostasis is defined as the ability of a body to create comfortable conditions by being at equilibrium or stabilizing itself internally whenever there are changes externally/outside the body. With regard to homeostasis, the room temperature of the internal part of the body, as with many other things in the body, is dynamic and does not stay at one place when external conditions change—it changes in order to maintain balance or equilibrium; hence, I do not agree with Heather’s definition of homeostasis.
