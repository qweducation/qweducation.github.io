16. national disaster risk reduction council volunteers rbc during calamities if then form​


Answer:

Based on your revised question, I believe you are asking for the form or procedure that the National Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Council (NDRRMC) follows when utilizing volunteers from the Regional Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Councils (RDRRMCs) during calamities.

When a disaster or calamity strikes, the NDRRMC activates its disaster response and management mechanism. One of the crucial elements of this mechanism is the mobilization of volunteers from the RDRRMCs to provide assistance and support to the affected communities. The process typically follows these steps:

1. The NDRRMC activates the response mechanism and assesses the situation in the affected areas.

2. The NDRRMC then contacts the RDRRMCs in the affected regions to inform them of the situation and request assistance from their pool of volunteers.

3. The RDRRMCs coordinate with their local government units (LGUs) to identify and mobilize volunteers who can provide the necessary support and services.

4. The RDRRMCs provide the NDRRMC with the number of volunteers available and their respective roles and responsibilities.

5. The NDRRMC deploys the volunteers to the affected areas and assigns them to specific tasks and functions based on their skills and capabilities.

The specific form or document used during the mobilization of volunteers may vary depending on the region and the type of disaster, but the general process follows the steps outlined above. It is worth noting that the NDRRMC and the RDRRMCs conduct regular training and capacity-building activities for volunteers to ensure that they are prepared and equipped to provide effective and efficient assistance during calamities.
