8. alex can purchase a used car for Php 300 000, which will require him to spend an average of Php 5 000 per month in repairs and maintenance. Or, he can rent a car for Php 30 000 a month, which includes all repairs and maintenance. After how many months will the rented car and purchased car cost the same? answer the questions below. a. wrte a system of equations letting c represent cost in pesos and t represent number of months. b.After how many months would the amount spent on each car be the same?


Answer:

a) [tex]\[ \begin{cases} c_{\text{used}} = 300,000 + 5,000t \\ c_{\text{rented}} = 30,000t \end{cases}\][/tex]

b) 12 months

Step-by-step explanation:

a) Write a system of equations letting c represent cost in pesos and t represent number of months

For the used car, it involves a single large payment and smaller monthly ones. On the other hand the rented car is paid by the month. Hence,

[tex]\[ \begin{cases} c_{\text{used}} = 300,000 + 5,000t \\ c_{\text{rented}} = 30,000t \end{cases}\][/tex]

b) After how many months would the amount spent on each car be the same?

In order to determine that, we equate the two different costs.

[tex]c_{\text{used}} = c_{\text{rented}}\\300,000 + 5,000t = 30,000t\\300,000 = 25,000t\\12 = t[/tex]

Therefore, it will take 12 months for the cost to be equal.

9. at 2:00 pm. Car A left manila for Albay at 45km/hr. An hour later, car B went after car A at 60km/hr. At what time will car B overtake car A?



We will let;
t = time it takes for car A to travel to Albay
         We note that car B travels 1 hour less than car A, so
t - 1 =time it takes for car B to travel to Albay

car A travels at 45km/hr while car B travels 60 km/hr.

We will use the formula d = rt where, d is the distance, r is the rate, and t is the time

For car A
d = rt
d = (45km/hr)(t)
d = 45t

For car B
d = rt
d = (60km/hr)(t-1)
d = 60t - 60

We know that car A and car B travels in the same distance but in different speed.
Therefore, we will equate the two equations for car A and car B
d(car A)=d(car B)
45t = 60t - 60
60 = 60t - 45t
60 = 15t
60/15 = t
4 = t
t = 4

Since, Car A travels at 2:00 pm, then car B overtakes car A after 4 hours.
Therefore, the time car B overtake car A is at 6:00 pm.
