The Alternative Learning System (ALS) is a parallel learning system in the Philippines that provides opportunities for out-of-school youth and adult (OSYA) learners to develop basic and functional literacy skills, and to access equivalent pathways to complete basic education.

A viable alternative to the existing formal education system, ALS encompasses both non-formal and informal sources of knowledge and skills. 10. 3. Teacher Cindy wants to measure the ALS Community Learning Center (CLC). The room floor is n exact square and the one side measures 3 meters. What is the perimeter of the CLC?​


Answer:

12 Meters

Step-by-step explanation:

If one side of the room measures 3 meters, and the room is an exact square, then all sides of the room must also measure 3 meters.

To find the perimeter of the room, we need to add up the lengths of all four sides. Since all four sides have the same length of 3 meters, we can multiply 3 by 4 to get the total perimeter:

Perimeter = 4 x 3 meters

Perimeter = 12 meters

Therefore, the perimeter of the ALS Community Learning Center (CLC) is 12 meters.

Explanation:

As a professional teacher, I have several obligations to different stakeholders:

a. My employer: As a professional teacher, I have an obligation to my employer to provide quality education to my students. This means I must follow the guidelines and standards set by my employer and fulfill the responsibilities assigned to me, including lesson planning, grading, and professional development. I also have an obligation to maintain a safe and respectful learning environment for my students and colleagues.

b. My clients/students: As a professional teacher, I have an obligation to my clients or students to provide them with a quality education that meets their needs and promotes their learning and development. This means I must create a positive and inclusive classroom environment that allows all students to thrive and provide support and resources to help them succeed academically and personally.

c. Other professionals or colleagues: As a professional teacher, I have an obligation to collaborate with other professionals and colleagues to improve the quality of education and support for students. This means I must communicate and work effectively with other teachers, administrators, and support staff to provide the best possible learning experience for students.

d. My profession: As a professional teacher, I have an obligation to maintain the highest standards of professionalism and ethical conduct. This means I must continuously improve my knowledge and skills, follow professional standards and guidelines, and behave ethically in all my interactions with students, colleagues, and other stakeholders.

e. Community/society: As a professional teacher, I have an obligation to serve as a role model and contribute to the well-being and development of the community and society at large. This means I must promote social justice and equality, participate in community service and outreach, and support efforts to improve education and social conditions in the community.

In summary, as a professional teacher, I have obligations to my employer, clients/students, other professionals or colleagues, my profession, and the community/society. Fulfilling these obligations requires a commitment to professionalism, ethical conduct, continuous learning, and a focus on improving the quality of education and the well-being of students and the broader community.
