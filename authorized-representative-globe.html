14. what is the direction that represent the latitude in the globe


Answer:

The direction that represents latitude on the globe is east-west. Latitude lines run parallel to the equator, which is the main line of latitude and islocated at 0 degrees latitude. As you move further north or south of the equator, the latitude lines get closer together, eventually converging at the poles. Therefore, latitude measures the distance from the equator in degrees, with the equatorlocated at 0 degrees latitude. Latitude lines are measured in degrees north or south of the equator, with the north pole being located at 90 degrees north latitude and the south pole being located at 90 degrees south latitude. These lines are important for navigation and locating places on the globe.

Latitude is measured in degrees (°)—from 0˚ to 90˚—north or south of the equator. Degrees of latitude are measured from an imaginary point at the center of the earth.

180 Degrees Longitude is used to define the International Date Line . This is also called the anti meridian. It is used to define the International Date Line. The 180-degree meridian runs through the western Pacific Ocean. The international date line roughly corresponds to the 180-degree meridian, although it deviates with respect to national borders. Because the Earth is divided into 24 time zones, each plus or minus a given number of hours from Greenwich, there arises a line on the Earth where the plus and minus zones meet. 17. 13. What are the directions that represents the latitude in the globe​


Answer:

The directions that represent latitude on the globe are North and South. Latitude measures the angular distance of a location from the equator, which is the imaginary circle around the earth that is equidistant from the North and South Poles. The equator is assigned a latitude of 0 degrees, and latitude lines extend in a parallel fashion to the equator, with positive values for locations north of the equator and negative values for locations south of the equator. Therefore, the North Pole has a latitude of 90 degrees North, and the South Pole has a latitude of 90 degrees South. The latitude lines are also referred to as parallels, and they are numbered in degrees from 0 to 90, both north and south of the equator.
