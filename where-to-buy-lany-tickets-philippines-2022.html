9. William is buying tickets for a school play. Adult tickets cost $2 more than student tickets. He buys 9 student tickets and 5 adult tickets and spends a total of $66. How much is an adult ticket?

William is buying tickets for a school play. Adult tickets cost 2 dollar more than student tickets. He buys 9 student tickets and 5 adult tickets and spends a total of 66 dollar. How much is an adult ticket?

Adult ticket costs 6 dollars.

[tex]------------------[/tex]

[tex]\huge\color{iron}\bold{Solution:}[/tex]

[tex]5 \times x + 9 \times (x - 2) = 66[/tex]

[tex] = 5x + 9(x - 2) = 66[/tex]

[tex] = 5x + 9x - 18 = 66[/tex]

[tex] = 14x - 18 = 66[/tex]

[tex]14x = 66 + 18[/tex]

[tex]14x = 84[/tex]

[tex]x = \frac{84}{14} [/tex]

[tex]x = 6[/tex]

So 6 dollars is the costs of an adult ticket.

28. an adult ticket to a concert cost $35 and a concession ticket cost $18. Mr B. paid $598 in all for 20 such tickets did he buy? How many concession did he buy?


Answer:

Mr. B bought 6 concession tickets.He also bought 14 adult tickets.

Step-by-step explanation:

Adult Ticket: x

Concession Ticket: y

Number of tickets:  20

Equation (1):

x + y = 20

Concession tickets in terms of x:

y = 20-x

Cost of tickets:

Adult ticket: 35x

Concession ticket: 18(20-x)

Total cost of tickets: 598

Equation (2):

35x + 18(20-x) = 598

Solve for adult tickets, x:

35x + 18(20-x) = 598

35x + 360 - 18x = 598

35x - 18x = 598 - 360

17x = 238

17x/17 = 238/17

x = 14 ⇒  adult tickets

Find the number of concession tickets, y:

y = 20-x

y = 20-14

y = 6 ⇒  number of concession tickets

Check:

Total number of tickets:

14 adult tickets + 6 concession tickets = 20 tickets

10 = 20  (True)

Total Cost of tickets:

35(14) + 18(6) = 598

490 + 108 = 598

598 = 598  (True)
