20. A 2500 kg car is moving to the east at 95 m/s collides with a 3500 kg mini truck with a speed of at 75 m/s. After the collision. The 2500 kg car moves to the right at 65 m/s. QUESTION: What is the velocity of the 3500 kg mini truck after collision?​


Answer:

the velocity of the 3500 kg mini truck after the collision is approximately 164.29 m/s to the east.

Explanation:

We can use the law of conservation of momentum to solve this problem:

The total momentum before the collision is:

P_before = m_car * v_car + m_truck * v_truck

where m_car is the mass of the car, v_car is its velocity, m_truck is the mass of the truck, and v_truck is its velocity.

Plugging in the values:

P_before = 2500 kg * 95 m/s + 3500 kg * 75 m/s

P_before = 475000 kgm/s + 262500 kgm/s

P_before = 737500 kg*m/s

After the collision, the total momentum is:

P_after = m_car * v_car_after + m_truck * v_truck_after

where v_car_after and v_truck_after are the velocities of the car and truck, respectively, after the collision.

We know that the car moves to the right (east) with a velocity of 65 m/s, so its momentum after the collision is:

P_car_after = m_car * v_car_after

P_car_after = 2500 kg * 65 m/s

P_car_after = 162500 kg*m/s

To find the momentum of the truck after the collision, we can subtract the momentum of the car after the collision from the total momentum after the collision:

P_truck_after = P_after - P_car_after

P_truck_after = 737500 kgm/s - 162500 kgm/s

P_truck_after = 575000 kg*m/s

Finally, we can use the definition of momentum to find the velocity of the truck after the collision:

P_truck_after = m_truck * v_truck_after

v_truck_after = P_truck_after / m_truck

v_truck_after = 575000 kg*m/s / 3500 kg

v_truck_after = 164.29 m/s

Therefore, the velocity of the 3500 kg mini truck after the collision is approximately 164.29 m/s to the east.
