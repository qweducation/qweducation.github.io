21. if three more strawberries are added to plate a how many strawberries must be added to plate b so that the ratio of the number of strawberries in plate a to the number of strawberries in plate b remains the same as before? plate a:2 strawberriesplateb:8 strawberries​


Answer:

12

Step-by-step explanation:

If plate A has 2 strawberries and plate B has 8, then the ratio is 2:8. Adding 3 strawberries to plate A would make 5.

2 is 1/4 of 8. So, 5 has to be 1/4 of Plate B. 5x4 is equal to 20.

The new ratio is 5:20. Though, that is not the final answer.

If it is asking for how many strawberries is needed so the ratio will be the same, and the ratio is 1/4 to 1, then that means we subtract 8 from 20 which will equal to 12. Hence, the answer is 12.

24. credibility and legitimacy of Fact-Checking and Online Verification​


Answer:

Fact-checking and online verification are important tools for helping to ensure the accuracy and reliability of information that is shared online. Fact-checking involves evaluating the veracity of statements or claims made by individuals or organizations, often in the context of news or political discourse. Online verification involves using various methods, such as checking sources and cross-referencing information with other reliable sources, to confirm that information is accurate and trustworthy.

There are a number of organizations and individuals that specialize in fact-checking and online verification, and their work can be a valuable resource for helping to sort fact from fiction in today's complex and often-confusing media landscape. However, it's important to keep in mind that no fact-checking or verification process is perfect, and it's always a good idea to critically evaluate the credibility and legitimacy of the sources of information that you encounter online.

Here are a few tips for evaluating the credibility and legitimacy of fact-checking and online verification:

Look for transparency: Fact-checking and verification organizations should be transparent about their methods and sources. They should clearly explain how they evaluate and verify information, and should provide links to the sources they use.

Check for bias: It's important to consider whether the fact-checking or verification organization has any biases that could affect its work. Look for organizations that are independent and impartial, and that are not affiliated with any particular political or ideological group.

Look for evidence: Fact-checking and verification organizations should provide evidence to support their claims, and should clearly explain how they arrived at their conclusions. Be wary of organizations that make bold claims without providing supporting evidence.

Check for consistency: The fact-checking and verification process should be consistent and unbiased. Be wary of organizations that seem to have a pattern of only fact-checking or verifying information that supports a particular point of view.

By following these tips, you can help ensure that you are getting reliable and accurate information from credible and legitimate sources.
