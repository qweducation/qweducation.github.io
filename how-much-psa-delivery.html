10. How much force is acting on a 3200 kg delivery truck if it slows down from 80 km/h to 60 km/h in 5s?

[tex]__________________________________[/tex]

How much force is acting on a 3200 kg delivery truck if it slows down from 80 km/h to 60 km/h in 5s?

Given:

Initial Velocity - 80 km/h = 22.22 m/sFinal Velocity - 60 km/h = 16.67 m/sTime - 5 sMass - 3 200 kg

Answer & Solution:

Step 1: Let us find first the acceleration

[tex]\sf A=\frac{vf-vi}{t}[/tex]

[tex]\sf A=\frac{16.6 7 \: m/s-22.22 \: m/s}{5 \: s}[/tex]

[tex]\sf A=\frac{-5.55 \: m/s}{5 \: s}[/tex]

[tex]\sf A=-1.11 \: m/s[/tex]

Step 2: Solve for force

[tex]\sf F=ma[/tex]

[tex]\sf F=(3 \: 200 \: kg)(-1.11 \: m/s^2)[/tex]

[tex]\sf F=- 3 \: 552 \: kg.m/s^2[/tex]

[tex]\sf F=\boxed{\bold{-3 \: 552 \: N}}[/tex]

Hence, the force acting on the delivery truck is -3 552 N

22. A delivery boy pulled a cart, he exerted 40N of force. The cart then moved 20m from its starting point with an angle of 40 degrees. How much work is done by the delivery boy?


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The work done by an object exerting a force F at an angle θ and at a distance d is given by

[tex]\boxed{W = Fd \: \cos \theta}[/tex]

where:

W = work

F = force

d = distance

θ = angle of the force with the horizontal

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for W

W = Fd cos θ

W = (40 N)(20 m)(cos 40°)

[tex]\boxed{W = \text{612.84 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the work done is 612.84 J.
