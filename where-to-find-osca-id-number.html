15. The sum of 4 consective odd numbers id 56.Find the greatest of the 4 numbers. The answer is 17 but how?


Answer:

17

Step-by-step explanation:

The consecutive odd numbers:

First odd number: x

Second odd number: x + 2

Third odd number: x + 4

Fourth and greatest odd number in the set: x + 6

Equation:

(x) + (x+2) + (x+4) + (x+6) = 56

4x + 12 = 56

4x = 56-12

4x/4 = 44/4

x = 11 ⇒  the first odd number

The numbers:

First: x = 11

Second: 11+2 = 13

Third: 11+4 = 15

Fourth: 11+6 = 17

The numbers are 11, 13, 15, 17.

The greatest among the four consecutive odd numbers is 17.

A jar contains 12 blue marbles, 6 green marbles, and 10 red marbles. a. what is the probability that if 2 marbles are selected at random, both are blue. b. what is the probability that if 2 marbles are selected at random, neither is green 3. A 3 digit ID number consists of numbers 1-9. Repetition in selecting a number for the ID is not allowed.what is the probability of choosing at random a 3-digit ID number consisting of only prime numbers


1. a. The probability of getting a heart is 13/52 which reduces to 1/4.
    b. The probability of getting a face card is 12/52 which reduces to 3/4.

2. a. There are 12 blue marbles and a total of 28 marbles in a jar. The probability that first marble selected is blue is 12/28. The probability of the second marble is still blue is 11/27. To get the probability of selecting two blue marbles at random, we will just multiply the two probabilities which gives us 132/756 which reduces to 11/63. Therefore the probability of selecting a blue marble is 11/63.
     b. The probability of the first marble that is not green is 12/28. The probability of the second marble which is still not green is 11/27. We will just multiply the two probabilities to get the probability of selecting a marble which is not green which is 462/756 which reduces to 11/18.

3. There are 5 prime numbers from 1-9 and there are total of 9 numbers from 1-9.
The probability of getting the first number is 5/9, second number is 4/8, third number is 3/7. We will multiply the three probabilities, which gives us product of 60/504 which reduces to 5/42. Therefore the probability of selecting a 3-digit number is 5/42.
