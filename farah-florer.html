Vascular plants (from Latin vasculum: duct), also known as Tracheophyta (the tracheophytes /trəˈkiːəfaɪts/, from the Greek trācheia), form a large group of plants (c. 300,000 accepted known species)[5] that are defined as land plants that have lignified tissues (the xylem) for conducting water and minerals throughout the plant. They also have a specialized non-lignified tissue (the phloem) to conduct products of photosynthesis. Vascular plants include the clubmosses, horsetails, ferns, gymnosperms (including conifers) and angiosperms (flowering plants). Scientific names for the group include Tracheophyta,[6][4]:251 Tracheobionta[7] and Equisetopsida sensu lato. Some early land plants (the rhyniophytes) had less developed vascular tissue; the term eutracheophyte has been used for all other vascular plants.

Poe writes Gothic Tales. Poe began by writing burlesques of Gothic stories, but soon began to write genuinely in the Gothic genre. Gothic stories frequently include features like as basements, ghosts, and decaying castles with secret pathways, as well as conditions of mystery and terror.

5. Mo Farah won the 10,000 meter race in the 2012 Olympics with an official time of 27 minutes, 30.42 s. To the correct number of significant figures, what was Farah’s average speed in m/sec?


Given: 

Traveled distance = 10, 000 m

Time taken = 27 minutes, 30.42 seconds

Solution:

1. Convert the time in terms of second : 1 minute = 60 seconds 

    27 minutes x 60 seconds = 1620 seconds

Time = 1620 seconds + 30.42 seconds

          = 1650. 42 seconds

2. Calculate the average speed: distance / time

     10, 000 m/ 1650.42 s

    = 6.059064 m/s

The similarities between the two texts:

The Name Jar by Yangsook Choi and One Green Apple by Eve Bunting, Ted Lewin (Illustrator) both deal with the theme of belonging and acceptance. In The Name Jar, the main character, Unhei, is a Korean girl who has recently moved to the United States and is struggling to fit in with her new classmates. She is worried that her name is too difficult for them to pronounce, so she puts her name in a jar and asks her classmates to help her pick a new one. In One Green Apple, the main character, Farah, is a Muslim girl who has recently immigrated to the United States with her family. She is struggling to fit in with her new classmates, who are mostly white and do not share her cultural background.
