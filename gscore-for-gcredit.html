2. how credit scoring works​


Answer:

Credit scoring works by analyzing various factors related to a person's credit history, such as their payment history, credit utilization, length of credit history, types of credit used, and recent credit inquiries. Each factor is assigned a weight or importance, and a score is calculated based on the overall analysis of these factors.

The most commonly used credit scoring model is the FICO score, which ranges from 300 to 850. A higher score indicates a lower risk of default, while a lower score indicates a higher risk of default. Lenders use credit scores to determine whether to approve a loan or credit application, and to set the terms and interest rates for the loan.

It is important to maintain a good credit score by paying bills on time, keeping credit utilization low, and avoiding excessive credit inquiries. A good credit score can help individuals and businesses qualify for better loan terms, lower interest rates, and other financial benefits.
