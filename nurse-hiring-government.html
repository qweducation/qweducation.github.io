9. The u.s. government hires 10 chinese-language experts from china to train u.s. workers. why decrease


There are a few possible reasons why decreasing the number of Chinese-language experts hired from China may be concerning or problematic:

1.) Loss of authentic immersion experience.

Having native Chinese speakers from China train American workers provides authentic immersion in the language, culture and perspectives needed to develop true fluency and nuance. Fewer Chinese experts could mean less immersive learning experiences and shallower understandings.

2.) Reduced access to current knowledge.

Experts from China would have access to the latest vocabulary, idioms, cultural references and ways of thinking currently prevalent in Chinese communities. This up-to-date knowledge is invaluable for becoming truly conversant and capable of interacting appropriately with Chinese speakers. With fewer experts, training may lag or overly rely on historic references.

3.) Challenges with long-distance learning.

While technology enables remote education, in-person guidance from experienced teachers is still ideal, especially for learning a language. Non-native or less experienced teachers, even if still fluent, cannot replicate the insights and nuances Chinese experts could provide through direct interaction.

4.) Appearance of bias or lack of investment.

Decreasing funding or support for hiring qualified Chinese experts could seem like a lack of real commitment to or valuing of Chinese language fluency and cultural proficiency. This may undermine goals for developing this crucial capability.

5.) Harm to US-China relations.

By decreasing Chinese experts hired, the US could be perceived as less interested in truly engaging with or understanding Chinese perspectives, views and positions. This could potentially damage the partnership and cooperation needed between the two nations.

The work considers how the 1904 city plans for Baguio and Manila by

Daniel Burnham, schemes created shortly after the end of the

Philippine-American War (1899-1902), strengthened an embryonic

sense of national character as defined by US imperial administrators,

and how recourse to the ‘modern’ art of civic design and reliance on

a new kind of expert, the city planner, helped to articulate this

identity. The study will systematically examine how the

conceptualization and construction of Baguio and Manila spoke to

America’s yearning to disassociate the Philippines from its past as an

‘uncivilized’ place so as to create a fresh culture, environment, and

identity that epitomized imperial hopes, principles and pride: a

cultural and physical landscape that would in addition quell any

threats posed by Filipino nationalism evident at that time.

Using well-established research methods to align the Philippines’

political and cultural transition with the realization of an instantly

recognizable vision of nationhood in built environmental form, the

paper probes the connection between civic design and nation building through a detailed consideration of images, meanings, and

associations. A proper understanding of how the arrangement of

urban environments in the Philippines interacted with the

construction of nationhood is thus crucial for determining the close

alliance between civic design, governance, and identity production

so that our comprehension may be deepened as to how cultural,

political, artistic, and environmental forces operated upon and

affected each other during the early years of the last century, i.e. a

time when urban planning became professionalized.

Explanation:

The government should implement a law that prioritizes women in the hiring process. This is because women have historically faced discrimination and unequal opportunities in the workforce. Despite the progress made towards gender equality, women are still underrepresented in certain industries and positions, and they are often paid less than their male counterparts for the same work.

By prioritizing women in the hiring process, the government can help level the playing field and ensure that women have equal access to job opportunities. This can help address the gender pay gap and promote gender equality in the workplace.

Furthermore, studies have shown that having a diverse workforce can lead to better decision-making and improved business performance. By prioritizing women in hiring, companies can benefit from a wider pool of talent and perspectives, which can ultimately lead to better outcomes.

Some may argue that prioritizing women in hiring is unfair to men. However, it is important to remember that the goal is not to discriminate against men, but rather to address the historical discrimination and inequality faced by women. By promoting gender equality in the workforce, we can create a more just and equitable society for all.
