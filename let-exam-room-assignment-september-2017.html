11. Assignment: (SCIENCE) will be checked on September 6, 2022. A. Find the definition of the following terms: 1. Epicenter 2. Magnitude 3. Focus 4. Seismic Waves 5. Triangulation


Answer:

Definition of the following :

1. Epicenter - the part of the earth's surface directly above the focus of an earthquake.

2. Magnitude - a measure of the total amount of energy released by an earthquake, as indicated on the Richter scale.

3. Focus - the focus of an earthquake is a point at some depth in the earth from where radiation of seismic waves starts.

4. Seismic Waves - are caused by the sudden movement of materials within the Earth, such as slip along a fault during an earthquake. Volcanic eruptions, explosions, landslides, avalanches, and even rushing rivers can also cause seismic waves.

5. Triangulation - the measurement of the elements necessary to determine the network of triangles into which any part of the earth's surface is divided in surveying.

28. On her first anatomy and physiology exam, Heather defined homeostasis as "the condition in which the body approaches room temperature and stays there." Do you agree with Heather’s definition?


Answer:

I do not agree with Heather’s definition of homeostasis because, by definition, homeostasis is defined as the ability of a body to create comfortable conditions by being at equilibrium or stabilizing itself internally whenever there are changes externally/outside the body. With regard to homeostasis, the room temperature of the internal part of the body, as with many other things in the body, is dynamic and does not stay at one place when external conditions change—it changes in order to maintain balance or equilibrium; hence, I do not agree with Heather’s definition of homeostasis.
