29. What is a Straight thru Cable?


What is a straight through cable?  

A straight through cable is a type of cable that is normally used in Local Area Network (LAN) to connect computers and devices in a network. This is also known as patch cable. Normally, straight through cables are composed of twisted copper wires and with RJ-45 connectors. This type of cables are ideal for two different devices that you would want to connect in a network. For example, you wanted to connect your printer to your desktop, instead of using cross over, use straight through cables. In any case that you would use a wrong cable, your network will not work correctly.

To create a straight through cable, you may use T-568 standard which means, you have to follow color arrangements or order for wire colors upon inserting them to the connector. The color arrangement should be:

White Orange Orange White Green Blue White Blue Green White Brown Brown

Both cable ends should have this order. Then you may crimped the cable to lock it on the connector.
