Answer:

WATER AND ALCOHOL

When you mix the rubbing alcohol with water, the latter's molecules make hydrogen bonds with the water molecules. The alcohol dissolves in the water to form a homogenous solution, so you cannot distinguish the alcohol and the water anymore.

WATER AND COOKING OIL

First you should have noticed that when you added the oil to the water they did not mix together. Instead the oil created a layer on the surface of the water. This is because oil is less dense than water and therefore it floats to the surface.

WATER AND SOY SAUCE

When you mix soy sauce with after, what happens is exactly what most people would expect…you get diluted soy sauce. There is no magical kitchen chemistry happening. Nothing foams or explodes. The characteristics of the soy sauce’s molecular structure are not altered.
