25. Direction: Provide the answer being asked for. 1. Compute the margin of error of the 90% confidence interval estimate of µ when sigma = 12 , n = 16. 2. Compute the 90% confidence interval estimate of µ given the following: s = 12 n = 18 and x = 36. 3. A photocopy company wanted to estimate the pages-per-toner copy rate of their newly designed photocopier machine. Using a random sample of 20 units of the same model, it resulted in 2600 pages per toner with a standard deviation of 30. Compute the pages-per-toner rate of the new model using a 95% confidence interval.​


Answer:

1. The margin of error of the 90% confidence interval estimate of µ when sigma = 12, n = 16 can be calculated as:

Margin of error = z*(sigma/sqrt(n))

where z is the z-score corresponding to the 90% confidence level. From the standard normal distribution table, the z-score is 1.645.

Plugging in the values, we get:

Margin of error = 1.645*(12/sqrt(16)) = 6.225

Therefore, the margin of error is 6.225.

2. The 90% confidence interval estimate of µ can be calculated as:

Confidence interval = x ± z*(s/sqrt(n))

where x is the sample mean, s is the sample standard deviation, n is the sample size, and z is the z-score corresponding to the 90% confidence level. From the standard normal distribution table, the z-score is 1.645.

Plugging in the values, we get:

Confidence interval = 36 ± 1.645*(12/sqrt(18)) = (30.52, 41.48)

Therefore, the 90% confidence interval estimate of µ is (30.52, 41.48).

3. The pages-per-toner rate of the new model using a 95% confidence interval can be calculated as:

Confidence interval = x ± t*(s/sqrt(n))

where x is the sample mean, s is the sample standard deviation, n is the sample size, t is the t-score corresponding to the 95% confidence level with (n-1) degrees of freedom. From the t-distribution table, the t-score for (n-1) = 19 degrees of freedom and 95% confidence level is 2.093.

Plugging in the values, we get:

Confidence interval = 2600 ± 2.093*(30/sqrt(20)) = (2561.34, 2638.66)

Therefore, the pages-per-toner rate of the new model using a 95% confidence interval is between 2561.34 and 2638.66 pages per toner.
