Answer:

you start with 450,000

your growth each year is going to be .023 * 450,000 = 10,350

multiply that by 5 and you get a total growth of 5 * 10,350 = 51,750

your losses each year is going to be .012 * 450,000 = 5,400.

multiply that by 5 and you get a total loss of 5 * 5,400 = 27,000.

your net growth is your growth minus your loss which is equal to 51,750 - 27,000 = 24,750.

add 24,750 to 450,000 and you get 474,750.

the formula for the net growth in this problem will be:

f = p + (p * (g - l) * n)

f is the future value

p is the present value

g is the annual growth rate which is the annual growth percent divided by 100

l is the annual loss rate which is the annual loss percent divided by 100

28. different sets of r items are to be chosen from a group of n items. if the number of possible permutations nPr is 132 and the number possible combinations nCr is 66, then what are the two numbers n and r​


Answer:

[tex]r=2\\n=12[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

Value of Permutation can be solved using this formula:

[tex]P(n,r)=\frac{n!}{(n-r)!}[/tex]

while this is the formula to get the Combination

[tex]C(n,r)=\frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!}[/tex]

Given:

[tex]P(n,r)=132[/tex]

[tex]C(n,r)=66[/tex]

Solve for r:

We will use the formula for combination and substitute the term [tex]\frac{n!}{(n-r)!}[/tex] by 132 from permutation value since they are equal.

[tex]C(n,r):\\\\\frac{132}{r!} =66\\\\r=2[/tex]

Expanding the value of [tex]n![/tex] is [tex]n(n-1)(n-2)(n-3)...[/tex] same thing with the value of [tex]r![/tex] is [tex]r(r-1)(r-2)(r-3)...[/tex]

Solve for n:

We will use the formula of permutation since we now have the value of r

[tex]P(n,r)\\\\\frac{n!}{(n-2)!} =132\\\\\frac{n(n-1)(n-2)}{(n-1)(n-2)} =132[/tex]

n-2 will cancel out each other since they are both in the numerator and the denominator

[tex]n(n-1)=132\\\\n^{2} -n-132=0\\\\(n+11)(n-12)=0[/tex]

we will take the constant with positive value when transposed:

[tex]n=12[/tex]
