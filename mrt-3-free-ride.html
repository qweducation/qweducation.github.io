14. Behavior is a manifestation or acting out of the attitudes an individual has. Take a look at the following situation to illustrate how attitude and behavior are related: Riding an overcrowded MRT couch and experiencing all sorts of physical sensations such as heat, smell and body contact can create an unfavorable attitude that riding the MRT is an unpleasant and abhoring experience. As a result, the individual with this attitude will express this disgust by either avoiding the MRT or totally shutting down and becoming oblivious to the distasteful experience, or become aggressive and unpleasant to other people while onboard the train. If this individual continues to ride the MRT with the same attitude and corresponding behavior, other people will get affected by this individual's behavior, who in turn, will act out a similar aggressive or uncivil behavior onboard the train. This may affect other commuters and a chain reaction will follow. Question for reaction: 1. How do you differentiate attitude and behavior? 2. Can attitudes be changed? 3. If attitudes can be changed, how?​


ANSWER:

1. Attitude and behavior are closely related, but they are not the same thing. Attitude refers to an individual's evaluation or opinion about something, such as a person, place, or thing. Behavior, on the other hand, refers to the actions or reactions that an individual exhibits based on their attitudes or beliefs. In the given scenario, the individual's attitude towards riding the MRT is that it is unpleasant and abhorrent, and this attitude manifests in their behavior of either avoiding the MRT, shutting down and becoming oblivious to the experience, or becoming aggressive and unpleasant towards other people while onboard the train.

2. Yes, attitudes can be changed. Attitudes are not fixed or predetermined, but rather they can be influenced by a variety of factors such as experiences, education, socialization, and exposure to new information or perspectives. In the given scenario, if the individual with the unfavorable attitude towards riding the MRT were to have positive experiences, such as riding a less crowded train or meeting friendly fellow passengers, this could potentially change their attitude towards the experience.

3. Attitudes can be changed through various means such as education, exposure to new perspectives, cognitive dissonance, and social influence. Education and exposure to new perspectives can help individuals gain new information and insights that may challenge their existing attitudes and beliefs. Cognitive dissonance occurs when individuals experience discomfort or inconsistency between their attitudes and behavior, which may motivate them to change their attitudes to reduce the discomfort. Social influence can also be a powerful factor in changing attitudes, as individuals may conform to the attitudes and behaviors of their peers or significant others. In the given scenario, if the individual with the unfavorable attitude towards riding the MRT were to have positive experiences and receive positive reinforcement for their behavior, this could potentially change their attitude towards the experience.
