22. 12) Ralph, Royce, and Randolf weigh 119 kgs altogether. Royce weighs twice as heavy as Ralph. Ralph weighs 7 kg less than Randolf. What is the mass of Randolf?​


Answer:

Let's start by assigning variables to each person's weight.

Let's call Ralph's weight "R", Royce's weight "O", and Randolf's weight "D".

From the problem, we know that:

R + O + D = 119 (they weigh 119 kgs altogether)

O = 2R (Royce weighs twice as heavy as Ralph)

R = D - 7 (Ralph weighs 7 kg less than Randolf)

We can use the second equation to substitute "2R" for "O" in the first equation:

R + 2R + D = 119

Simplifying:

3R + D = 119

We can also use the third equation to substitute "D - 7" for "R" in the above equation:

3(D - 7) + D = 119

Simplifying:

4D - 21 = 119

Adding 21 to both sides:

4D = 140

Dividing both sides by 4:

D = 35

Therefore, Randolf weighs 35 kgs.

25. 10 years ago, Jane was 4 times as old as Bianca. Now she is twice as old as Bianca. Find their present ages. Show your solution.


Age Problem:

Bianca is 15 years old while Jane is 30 years old.

Solutions:

Given that Jane is 4 times as old as Bianca, represent their ages.

x - Bianca's age

4x - Jane's age

Since this is a representation of their ages 10 years ago then,

4x + 10 = 2(x + 10)

Solve for x.

4x + 10 = 2(x + 10)

4x + 10 = 2x + 20

4x - 2x = 20 - 10

2x = 10

2x/2 = 10/2

x = 5

Therefore, Bianca is only 5 years old 10 years ago.

Find Jane's age using 4x.

4(5) = 20

Thus, Jane's age 10 years ago is 20.

Find their current ages.

x + 10 - Bianca's age

5 + 10 = 15

Therefore, Bianca is now 15 years old.

Find Jane's age.

4x + 10 = 4(5) + 10 = 20 + 10 = 30

Thus, Jane is now 30 years old.
