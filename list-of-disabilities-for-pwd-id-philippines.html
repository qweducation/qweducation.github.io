30. Write a letter for a PWD (Person w/ disability)


Answer:

Greetings, fellow patrons with disabilities!

I'm writing this open letter to let you know that, no matter how lonely our lives can seem at times, we are never alone. I'm not going to be gullible. I remember how difficult it was to be singled out as a child simply because we couldn't walk or needed assistance with everyday activities. I understand that growing up in a particular way and being mistreated as a result leaves a mark on the spirit. It's an experience we'll never forget. Unfortunately, no matter how hard we try to ignore it, humiliation is an emotion that will never go anywhere.

It's as if the devil on our shoulder wants us to remember the hurtful feelings and people in our lives. I understand if you're stuck at home because everybody else in your family works. I understand that your home becomes your safe haven as a result, but I also recognize the value of contact. It is important to sustain the bond by conversing with others. Social media serves as a social lifeline for me. I understand that it can be stressful at times, but it has proven to be an invaluable tool for me in terms of interacting with the outside world.

We may feel alone as a result of our chronic pain and disabilities, but this is not our fault. We have to make the most of what we've been given. Despite the challenges, I am here to remind you that life can still be fun. Our attitude has a significant impact.
