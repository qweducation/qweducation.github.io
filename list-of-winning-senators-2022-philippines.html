12. president of senate in the philippines


THE SENATE PRESIDENT OF THE PHILIPPINESWHO IS THE SENATE PRESIDENT?

- The presiding Senate President of the Philippines is Vicente "Titto" Castelo Sotto III

- Vicente Sotto is a former actor, comedian, and songwriter.

- He currently participates actively in the field of politics.

- He was elected into the position of Senate President on May 21, 2018.

- He is currently on his fourth term inside the senate, his prior years at 1992-2004, 2010-2016, and 2016-present.

- He also served as a Vice Mayor of Quezon City from 1988-1992

THE RESPONSIBILITIES AND ABILITIES OF THE SENATE PRESIDENT

- It is the responsibility of the senate president to preside over the sessions of the senate at the designated dates and hours. This includes maintaining the order of the senate, ensuring that quorums are properly recorded, and to dispose of matters in the senate in accordance with the handbook.

- The decision to all points of order lies within the senate president.

- It is their duty to sign all joints, memorials, joint and concurrent solutions, and other forms of documents that are relevant to the senate or senate president.

- To see to it that all the agreed upon resolutions by the senate are complied with.

- They have general control of all corridors, rooms, and offices associated with the senate.

- They are tasked with ensuring order in all corridors, rooms, and offices associated with the senate.

- It is their responsibility to designate a Sergeant-At-Arms whenever the acting Sergeant-At-Arms becomes incapacitated.

- It is their responsibility to appoint the subordinate personnel of the Senate in accordance with the General Appropriations Act.

- To dismiss any employee with cause that conforms to the Civil Service Law

- To adjust the number of personnel by separating or joining them whenever it conforms with the General Appropriations Act.

CHOOSING A SENATE PRESIDENT

- In order to be elected as a senate president, the majority of the senators, including those who are not currently present in the aforementioned meeting.

- Should there be more than one candidate for the position, nominal votes shall be done.

- Otherwise, the elections shall also be done by viva voce, or resolution.

21. qualifications of a senator in the philippines


THE SENATORIAL REQUIREMENTS OF THE PHILIPPINESTHE QUALIFICATIONS FOR SENATORIAL ELIGIBILITY

1.) Be a natural-born citizen of the Philippines

- This means that the individual running for the senatorial elections did not have to undergo any form of naturalization process as they were born, and are therefore an automatic citizen of the Philippines as soon as they were born.

2.) Is at least 35 years old at the day of the election

- This means that at the very day of the election period, the aforementioned person that is running for a senatorial position must be at least 35 years of age.

3.) Able to read and write

- Probably the least complicated, and most obvious requirement. The individual running for the senatorial position must be able to read and write.

4.) A registered voter

- The individual running for a senatorial role must have already expressed their right to suffrage.

5.) A resident of the Philippines

- The individual running for a senatorial role must be residing in the Philippines as a resident for no less than two years before the election date.

THE QUALITIES OF A GOOD SENATOR

* While there are no set qualities that define whether or not a senator is good, there are some general guidelines that the majority of the population typically follow:

1.) Instinct and drive for social issues

- A senator must have the capability to take all the issues of their country to heart.

-They must have the drive and motivation to fix these problems, and fulfill these resolutions properly.

2.) Diligence

- A senator must ensure that whatever resolution they promised to the people are completed within due time.

3.) A large curiosity

- A senator that is chained within the religious system of the government is not a good senator.

-While some senators who take their curiosity too far might be considered reckless, senators must have the curiosity to investigate further in the problems of their country if they wish to fix it.

4.) Honest

- A dishonest senator is a corrupt senator, and the Philippines is already suffering from corruption itself.

- Having a senator who is not honest to the people, is having a senator that does not even trust themselves.

5.) A leader

- A senator is part of the legislative department, the department mainly responsible for the development of laws.

-As such, a senator must be a good representative and a good leader of their people.

-They act as the speaker for the opinions and hopes of the people of their country.
