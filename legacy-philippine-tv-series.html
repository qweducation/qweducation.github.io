11. what literature is associated with the philippine legends prehistory and colonial legacyA. Asian LiteratureB. Afro-Inglo Literature C. American Literature D. Philippine Literature ​


Answer:

D. Philippine Literature ​

Explanation:

Philippine literature is literature associated with the Philippines from prehistory, through its colonial legacies, and on to the present.  Pre-Hispanic Philippine literature was actually epics passed on from generation to generation, originally through an oral tradition. However, wealthy families, especially in Mindanao, were able to keep transcribed copies of these epics as family heirloom. One such was the Darangen, an epic of the Maranaos.

19. what are the legacy and impacts of education during 19th century in the Philippine ​


During the 19th century, education in the Philippines had a significant impact on the country's history and legacy. The introduction of formal education by the Spanish colonial government paved the way for the establishment of schools and universities, which provided Filipinos with access to knowledge and skills.

One of the most significant legacies of education during this period was the emergence of a new class of educated Filipinos, who became leaders in various fields such as politics, literature, and journalism. These individuals played a crucial role in the fight for Philippine independence and the development of a national identity.

Education also had a profound impact on the country's economy, as it provided Filipinos with the skills and knowledge necessary to participate in modern industries and businesses. This led to the growth of a middle class, which contributed to the country's economic development.

However, education during this period was not without its challenges. The curriculum was heavily influenced by Spanish culture and religion, and many Filipinos were forced to learn in a language that was foreign to them. This resulted in a loss of indigenous knowledge and culture, which had a lasting impact on the country's identity.

Overall, the legacy and impact of education during the 19th century in the Philippines were complex and multifaceted. While it provided Filipinos with access to knowledge and skills, it also had negative consequences such as the loss of indigenous culture. Nevertheless, education remains a vital component of Philippine society and continues to shape the country's future.

- Aquino's administration is known for restoring democracy in the Philippines after the Marcos dictatorship.

- She also established the 1987 Constitution, which ensured the protection of human rights and the separation of powers in government.

(Source: "Corazon C. Aquino," Official Gazette of the Republic of the Philippines, https://www.officialgazette.gov.ph/presidents/corazon-c-aquino/)

- Duterte's administration is known for its controversial war on drugs, which has led to thousands of deaths and human rights violations.

- He implemented tax reform through the Tax Reform for Acceleration and Inclusion (TRAIN) Law, which aimed to generate revenue for the government and fund infrastructure projects.

- He also signed the Universal Access to Quality Tertiary Education Act, which provided free tuition to students in state universities and colleges.
