z-score = (score - mean) / standard deviation

If Ana's z-score is 1, then we can write:

1 = (score - 50) / 5

Rearranging this equation, we get:

score = (1 * 5) + 50 = 55

If Ana's z-score is -1, then we can write:

-1 = (score - 50) / 5

Rearranging this equation, we get:

score = (-1 * 5) + 50 = 45

Therefore, Ana's score can either be 55 or 45, depending on whether her z-score is 1 or -1, respectively.

Since 55 is greater than the mean score of 50, we can say that Ana's score is above the mean score if her z-score is 1. On the other hand, since 45 is less than the mean score of 50, we can say that Ana's score is below the mean score if her z-score is -1.

Therefore, the correct answer is:

b. Ana's score is below the mean score if her z-score is -1, and Ana's score is above the mean score if her z-score is 1.
