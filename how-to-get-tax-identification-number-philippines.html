Step-by-step explanation:

We have the numbers 0 to 9 as possible first n number combinations, meaning there are 10 possible numbers to choose for the first digit.

For the second n number, we can still choose from 0 to 9, suggesting that there are still 10 numbers to choose.

The sequence goes on for these 12 digits.

With that, we can have [tex]n^{x} = 10^{12} = 1,000,000,000,000[/tex]

Explanation: Over the next ten years, it is expected to save private businesses more than a trillion pesos in taxes. MSMEs will be the biggest beneficiaries of CREATE, as the country's corporate income tax rate will be reduced from 30% to 20% for the first time. Large corporations will also benefit from an immediate reduction in the corporate income tax rate from 30% to 25%.

CREATE also provides additional types of tax relief as part of the government's economic recovery package, which addresses the various demands and concerns of the business sector as a result of the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic. CREATE also includes a performance-based, time-bound, targeted, and transparent tax incentive system. During hearings and conversations, parties universally agreed on these principles.

The CREATE tax incentive scheme balances the interests of all stakeholders while adhering to core values and keeping the country's fiscal concerns in mind. CREATE has implemented a tier-based incentive system based on industry and location. This allows our industrial policy to be reflected in the fiscal incentive system.

Activities in more sophisticated sectors and less developed areas will receive longer incentives. To summarize, all businesses seeking tax breaks will continue to work with investment promotion agencies, or IPAs. The IPAs will approve incentives unless the proposed project or activity exceeds a one billion peso investment capital threshold.

Above this threshold, applications for incentives will be decided on by the Fiscal Incentives Review Board, an interagency Cabinet-level body charged with overseeing the granting of fiscal incentives. The Philippines' Strategic Investment Priority Plan will cover the industrial tiers (SIPP). The SIPP includes programs or activities that encourage long-term development and progress. Because the first SIPP has yet to be produced, the Board of Investments' 2020 Investment Priorities Plan serves as a transitional SIPP until the first SIPP is released.

Activities under the 2020 IPP may be eligible for incentives under the Tier I classification, as suggested by the BOI and authorized by the FIRB, without prejudice to upgrading to Tiers II or III if qualified under the new SIPP. The powers and functions of the Fiscal Incentives Review Board (FIRB) are expanded under the CREATE Act as part of its oversight mandate. The FIRB structure under CREATE ensures that, in addition to tax subsidies, tax incentives are granted and properly monitored by relevant agencies. The FIRB is an interagency government body authorized by Philippine law to grant tax breaks to registered businesses.

22. why high end cars here in Philippines get higher tax rate and higher SRP'S​


Answer:

High-end cars in the Philippines are subject to higher tax rates and have higher suggested retail prices (SRPs) due to several factors. One of the main reasons is that luxury cars are considered non-essential items and are seen as a luxury or indulgence, rather than a necessity.

High-end cars are subject to higher excise taxes compared to more affordable vehicles. The Tax Reform for Acceleration and Inclusion (TRAIN) law, which took effect in 2018, imposes higher excise taxes on automobiles with higher net retail prices (NRPs) or base prices. The excise tax rate for luxury cars is up to 50% of their NRP, while the rate for more affordable vehicles is lower.

Moreover, luxury car manufacturers also tend to price their vehicles higher in the Philippine market due to the country's importation taxes and tariffs. The cost of importing luxury cars is often higher due to additional taxes and fees imposed by the government, which are then passed on to the consumer.

Lastly, the demand for luxury cars in the Philippines is typically lower compared to more affordable vehicles, which means that manufacturers need to set higher prices to cover their costs and make a profit. The limited market for luxury cars also means that dealerships and manufacturers need to maintain higher margins on sales to remain profitable.

Overall, the combination of higher taxes, importation fees, and lower demand for luxury cars in the Philippines leads to higher suggested retail prices for high-end vehicles.

25. what are the current tax policies in the philippines and how do they impact the economy and society?​


Answer:

The current tax policies in the Philippines have been a topic of discussion for quite some time now. The country's tax system is composed of national and local taxes, which are collected by the Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) and the Bureau of Customs (BOC). The government has implemented several tax reforms in recent years, including the Tax Reform for Acceleration and Inclusion (TRAIN) law, which aims to generate more revenue for the government and provide relief for low-income earners.

The impact of these tax policies on the economy and society has been significant. The TRAIN law has increased the take-home pay of low-income earners, but it has also resulted in higher prices for basic goods and services. The government has also implemented measures to combat tax evasion, which has improved revenue collection and helped fund infrastructure projects and social services.

Despite these efforts, there are still challenges that need to be addressed. The tax system remains complex and difficult to understand, which makes compliance difficult for many businesses and individuals. There is also a need to improve the efficiency and transparency of tax collection and administration to ensure that the government is able to collect the revenue it needs to fund essential services and programs.

The Philippines has recently ratified the final version of the proposed Corporate Recovery and Tax Incentives for Enterprises Act (CREATE), which reduces the income tax rate to 25% from 30% for corporations with a net taxable income of up to PHP 5 million ($99,000) and 20% for those with over PHP 5 million ($99,000). The new law also provides a five-year income tax holiday for new enterprises and a reduced rate of 15% for those located in less developed areas. The government hopes that these measures will help businesses recover from the economic impact of COVID-19.

The COVID-19 pandemic has had a significant impact on the Philippine economy, with a global recession possibly on the horizon. The pandemic has slowed down economic activity, which has led to job losses and reduced incomes l. If the country is unsuccessful in navigating through this pandemic-induced economic crisis, then it may enter into a social and political crisis.
