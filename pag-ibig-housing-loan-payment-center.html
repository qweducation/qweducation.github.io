9. Single payment loan ​


Answer:

Example: Jane wants to borrow $5,000 from a lender to purchase a new car. The lender agrees to give her the loan at an interest rate of 10% per year. Jane agrees to repay the loan in one lump sum payment at the end of the 1-year loan term.

Step-by-step explanation:

The interest on the loan is calculated as $5,000 x 10% = $500.

So, Jane needs to pay $5,000 + $500 = $5,500 at the end of the loan term.

In this case, Jane only needs to make one payment at the end of the loan term to repay the entire loan amount including interest. This type of loan is typically used for short-term needs and is not suitable for long-term financial needs.
