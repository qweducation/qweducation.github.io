Ang mga nabanggit na tao ay mayroong malaking ambag sa mga rebolusyon sa enlightenment at industriyal na naganap noong 17th at 18th century. Narito ang kanilang mga ambag:

John Locke - Isinulong niya ang konsepto ng karapatan sa pag-aari at kalayaan. Nakapagbigay ito ng inspirasyon sa mga rebolusyonaryong kilusan.

Thomas Hobbes - Nakapagbigay ng konsepto ng "Leviathan" kung saan ang pamahalaan ay mayroong malaking kontrol sa mga mamamayan upang mapanatili ang kaayusan.

Jean-Jacques Rousseau - Nakapagbigay ng konsepto ng "Social Contract" kung saan ang mga mamamayan ay tumatanggap ng karapatan at proteksyon mula sa pamahalaan, samantalang sila naman ay mayroong tungkulin na sumunod sa mga batas at regulasyon.

Immanuel Kant - Nakapagbigay ng konsepto ng "Enlightenment" kung saan ang tao ay dapat magkaroon ng kalayaan sa pag-iisip at makapagdesisyon sa sarili.

Baron de Montesquieu - Nakapagbigay ng konsepto ng "Separation of Powers" kung saan ang pamahalaan ay mayroong tatlong sangay na magkakaroon ng balanse sa pagpapatupad ng batas.

James Hargreaves - Nakaimbento ng spinning jenny na nagpatulong sa pagpapabilis ng paggawa ng tela at nakatulong sa pag-unlad ng industriya ng tekstil.

Eli Whitney - Nakaimbento ng cotton gin na nakatulong sa pagpapabilis ng pagproseso sa mga pananim ng cotton.

Henry Cort - Nakaimbento ng puddling process na nagpatulong sa paggawa ng high-quality iron na nakatulong sa pag-unlad ng industriya ng metal.

Henry Bessemer - Nakaimbento ng bessemer process na nagpatulong sa paggawa ng high-quality steel.

John McAdam at Thomas Telford - Nakapagbigay ng kontribusyon sa pagpapabuti ng mga kalsada at imprastraktura sa transportasyon.

Joseph Marie Jacquard - Nakaimbento ng Jacquard loom na nagpatulong sa paggawa ng intricate designs sa mga tekstil.

Michael Faraday - Nakaimbento ng electric generator at transformer na nagpatulong sa pag-unlad ng industriya ng kuryente.

Samuel Morse - Nakaimbento ng telegraph at Morse code na nagpatulong sa pagpapabilis ng komunikasyon sa malalayong lugar.

Alexander Graham Bell - Nakaimbento ng telephone na nagpatulong sa pagpapabilis ng komunikasyon sa mga tao.

Henry Ford - Nakapagbigay ng kontribusyon sa pagpapabuti ng produksyon ng mga sasakyan at nakaimbento ng assembly line.

Thomas Edison - Nakaimbento ng light bulb at iba pang mga kagamitan na nagpatulong sa pag-unlad ng industriya ng kuryente.

17. For John Henry Newman, “Growth is the only evidence of life”. what does Newman mean by this? Do you agree with him? Explain


Answer:

John Henry Newman was a British theologian, philosopher, and writer who lived in the 19th century. His statement, "Growth is the only evidence of life," suggests that in order to prove that something is alive, it must show signs of development, change, and progress over time. In other words, if something is not growing or changing, it is stagnant and lifeless.

Newman believed that growth was essential to both individual and societal well-being. He argued that human beings were created to grow, to learn, and to develop in all aspects of their lives. He believed that growth was necessary for intellectual, emotional, and spiritual health, and that stagnation or decline was a sign of illness, decay, or death.

Newman's statement is relevant in many areas of life. For example, it is true in the realm of personal development, where growth is necessary for individuals to reach their full potential. It is also true in the realm of education, where students must demonstrate growth in order to show that they are learning and making progress. In the business world, companies must show growth in order to demonstrate that they are successful and competitive.

Yes i agree, because Newman's statement makes sense, as growth and progress are indeed signs of vitality and life. However, it is important to note that growth can take many forms, and it is not always necessary or desirable for growth to be constant or linear. 19. who predicted new elements based on the atomic number of known elements a Ernest b john newlands c henry mosely D plato​


Answer:

C.

Explanation:

The scientist who predicted new elements based on the atomic number of known elements is Henry Moseley.

Who predicted new elements based on the atomic number of known elements?

a. Ernest b. John newlands c. Henry Mosely d. Plato

Henry Moseley was a British physicist who conducted experiments to determine the atomic number of elements. He discovered that the atomic number, which is the number of protons in the nucleus of an atom, was a more fundamental property of an element than its atomic mass. He found that the elements could be arranged in order of increasing atomic number, which led to the modern periodic table of elements. Moseley's work also allowed him to predict the existence of several new elements, including elements with atomic numbers 72, 75, and 85, which were later discovered and named hafnium, rhenium, and astatine, respectively. Therefore, it was Henry Moseley who predicted new elements based on the atomic number of known elements.
