There’s a technology that is rapidly changing medical coding in health-information management. Computer assisted coding (CAC) has the ability to accurately generate medical codes directly from clinical documentation. With computer assisted coding, healthcare organizations can become more compliant with payer and quality reporting requirements, while also improving their bottom line.

Electronic health record systems (EHRs) are a major source of electronic documentation. However, it can be a complex process to integrate EHR data into the coding process. A. If the letters and numbers can be repeated in the same plate number, then there are 26^3 (26*26*26) possible arrangements for the letters, and 10^3 (10*10*10) possible arrangements for the numbers. Therefore, the total number of possible plate numbers is:

26^3 * 10^3 = 17,576,000

B. If the letters and numbers cannot be repeated in the same plate number, then there are 26*25*24 possible arrangements for the letters (since the first letter can be any of the 26 letters, the second letter can be any of the remaining 25, and the third can be any of the remaining 24), and 10*9*8 possible arrangements for the numbers. Therefore, the total number of possible plate numbers is:

26*25*24 *10*9*8 = 11,232,000

Therefore, there are 17,576,000 possible plate numbers if the letters and numbers can be repeated, and 11,232,000 possible plate numbers if they cannot be repeated.
