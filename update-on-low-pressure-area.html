FEATURES OF WEBSITE

It pertains to a grouping of online pages that are typically related and arranged in various ways.

A site is a set of files that can be accessed via the internet, covering a specific topic or theme, and are controlled by a specific individual or group. A home page is the first page of the website.

SLIDER

A website's slider is a dynamic content area with various "slides" of images or text. The slides may have a sizable image with text and buttons put on top of it.

It refers to the changing content area that presents 'slides' of visual information including large images, texts, announcements, updates and promotions.

Additional Information:

Homepage

It refers to the web browser start page displayed when the application initially launches. The home page is typically found at the top of the website's domain or subdomain.

Header

The header is the upper-most section of the page in the layout of a website. As the area that visitors first see when they first visit the website and before scrolling the page, it is unquestionably a strategically important part of the page.

Navigation Menu

A navigation menu is a set of connections to additional web pages on a website, typically internal pages. Visitors can easily access the most useful pages with the help of navigation menus, which are most frequently found in page headers or sidebars on websites.

Sidebar

is a column that is positioned to the right or left of a webpage's main content section. Frequently, they are employed to provide consumers with various kinds of further information.

Call to action

An instruction to the visitor on a website is meant to motivate them to take some sort of action. An example of a call to action would be the text like call us now, find out more, or subscribe to our newsletter and the like.

Footer

is a section that appears below the primary body material at the bottom of every website page. The word "footer" originates from the print industry, where it refers to a unified design element that appears on all pages of a document.
