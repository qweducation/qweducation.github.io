Answer:

To find the 4th decile of the given sales data, we need to arrange the sales values in ascending order:

200, 300, 360, 440, 460, 520, 540, 560, 580, 620

The decile is a measure of position that divides the data into 10 equal parts. The 4th decile would be the data point that lies at the 40th percentile, or 40% of the way from the smallest value to the largest value.

To find the 4th decile, we first need to calculate the total number of data points:

n = 10

Then we can calculate the index of the data point that lies at the 40th percentile:

index = (40/100) * n

= 0.4 * 10

= 4

The 4th data point in the sorted data set is 440. Therefore, the 4th decile of the sales data is 440.

Interpreting the result, we can say that 40% of the sales values are less than or equal to 440 and 60% of the sales values are greater than 440. Additionally, since the average sale per day lies on the 4th decile, we can say that Aling Marie's daily sales are relatively low compared to the entire sales dataset.
