1. Risks and disadvantages of using non-license software​


Answer:

•It doesn't work

•No ongoing support

•Security risks

•Productivity risks

•Economic risks

•Penalties.

Explanation:

It doesn’t work

Many software companies have implemented a way of checking the registration, so it might work for a while but then you receive an update which renders it unusable unless you make a purchase.

No ongoing support

In addition to problems with the software itself, pirated programs lack access to customer support, making it more difficult to get help when problems occur. If something did go wrong with your package, who could you ask for help?

Security risks

Unlicensed software can carry viruses and other types of malware that infect computers. According to the Harrison Group, 24% of pirated copies of Windows were either infected or they automatically downloaded malware as soon as they connected to the Internet. Even if a piece of software isn't infected itself, it can pose a security risk through a lack of updates.

Productivity risks

Aside from security holes, using outdated or pirated software can cause users to encounter bugs and glitches, leading to lost work, lost time and frustration. The hacks used to run these types of software can also interfere with how it operates, such as preventing a program from accessing online features in order to avoid detection.

Economic risks

Unlicensed software takes away sales of legitimate software. Aside from the obvious effect on software development companies, piracy also harms businesses completely outside the software industry. It is also worth noting that the use of pirated software also drives up the costs for legitimate users.

Penalties

Unlicensed software use is illegal and can result in both civil as well as criminal penalties. Copyright infringement from using unlicensed software cost small UK business owners over £900,000 in 2016, according to new research.

7. how many 6 digit license plate can be manufactured if no license number begins with zero​


Answer:

If no license number can begin with zero, then there are 9 choices for the first digit (1-9) and 10 choices for each of the remaining five digits (0-9).

Therefore, the total number of possible 6-digit license plates is:

9 x 10 x 10 x 10 x 10 x 10 = 9,000,000

So, there can be 9,000,000 different 6-digit license plates manufactured if no license number begins with zero.

How Does a Driver's License Get Revoked? - FindLaw

The types of conduct that can cause a driver to have his or her driver's license revoked are similar to offenses that can result in suspension, only more serious (and in many cases, after repeated violations following a suspension). And since driving on public streets and highways is considered a privilege and not a right, states generally have a lot of leeway with regard to the revocation of drivers' licenses for both driving- and non-driving-related offenses.

If your driver's license is revoked, you will have to wait a specified period of time before you may apply for a new one. But even then, your state may deny your application for a number of reasons, including the failure to pass required tests. This article offers a general answer to the question of "How can your license be revoked?" However, you should check with your state's department of motor vehicles for specifics.

Procedure [edit]

Apply In-Person[edit]

Here's how you can apply for a Permit to Carry Firearms at Firearms and Explosives Office (FEO). First, you will need to secure an application form from FEO, ask an officer where can you get one.

Complete the application form and submit it together with your authenticated copy of your License to Own and Possess Firearms (LTOPF) and Firearms Registration. You will also need to submit your NBI clearance, Threat Assessment issued by PNP, Drug Test Certificate and any additional requirements.

After submitting all the requirements, pay the licensing fee.

You will be informed by the FEO personnel when can you come back to collect your Permit to Carry For Outside Residence (PTCfor).

Required Documents [edit]

Basic requirements:

Your Notarized filled-out application form with attached 2x2 picture

License to Own and Possess Firearms (LTOPF)

Firearms Registration

NBI Clearance

Drug Test Certificate (Crime Laboratory or any DOH Accredited Testing Center)

Threat Assessment issued by PNP

Note: Drug test is waived if the date of approval of LTOPF is within 1 year

Additional requirements:

Government officials and employees:

Oath of Office or Appointment Order

Office identification card

PNP, AFP and other Law Enforcement Agents:

Certificate of Non-pending case and duty status

Appointment Order

Agency ID

J9 Clearance and AADT for Military Reservists

Encumbent elected officials:

Oath of Office

Office identification card

Retired government officials and employees:

Retirement order

Retiree ID

Businessman:

DTI Registration or SEC Registration

ITR and Business Permit

Private Individual:

Certificate of Employment or Proof of Income

Valid ID

Former elected officials:

Certification from the COMELEC that the applicant is a former elected official

Valid ID

Note: You may be asked by FEO to present additional documents.
