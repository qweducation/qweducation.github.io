18. What are some of the benefits and privileges provided to Filipino citizens with disabilities under this law?​


Answer:

Republic Act No. 10745, also known as the "Act Providing for the Development, Promotion and Recognition of the Philippine National Sign Language for the Deaf, and the Creation of the Philippine National Sign Language Council", provides several benefits and privileges to Filipino citizens with disabilities. Some of these benefits and privileges include:

Access to educational materials in Philippine National Sign Language (PNSL) - Schools and learning institutions are required to provide educational materials in PNSL to students who are deaf or hard of hearing.

Accessible government services - All government offices and agencies are required to have personnel who are proficient in PNSL and other forms of sign language to provide assistance to individuals with disabilities.

Employment opportunities - Private companies and government agencies are required to allocate at least one percent of their positions for persons with disabilities.

Discounts and benefits - Persons with disabilities are entitled to at least 20 percent discount on goods and services, such as medical and dental services, transportation fares, and purchase of basic commodities.

Accessibility to public transportation - Public transportation systems are required to provide accessible facilities and priority seats to persons with disabilities.

These benefits and privileges aim to promote the rights and equality of Filipino citizens with disabilities by providing them with equal access to education, employment, and government services, and by recognizing their contributions to society. They also address historical and structural inequality by addressing the barriers that prevent persons with disabilities from participating fully in society.

24. Habilitation is concerned with people who have acquired disabilities


The correct answer is False.


It is not habilitation; rather, it is rehabilitation which is concerned with people who have acquired disabilities. Rehabilitation helps an individual regain his/ her skills, abilities, or knowledge that may have been lost or compromised because of acquiring disabilities or due to a change in one’s disability or circumstances. Examples, skills or functioning lost due to stroke, head injury, illness or other cause.


On the other hand, habilitation is concerned with developmental disabilities. Its services are for those who may not have ever developed the skill, such as a child who is not talking as expected for his or her age.  Also, adults can also benefit from habilitative services, particularly those with intellectual disabilities or disorders such as cerebral palsy who may benefit from services at different points in their life to address functional abilities.
