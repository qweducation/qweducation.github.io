4. Kris and tin are comparing the number 10 and negative 10.Kris says that the number 10is farther than zero than negative 10. Tin says that negative 10 is greater than 10. Who is correct? Explain


Step-by-step explanation:

Kris and Tin are comparing the numbers 10 and -10.

Kris says that 10 is farther from zero than -10. This is correct because the distance from a number to zero is the absolute value of that number (i.e., the distance is always positive). The absolute value of 10 is 10, while the absolute value of -10 is also 10. So both numbers are the same distance from zero. However, since Kris said "farther," which implies distance, Kris is incorrect.

Tin says that -10 is greater than 10. This is incorrect because when we compare two numbers, we are usually referring to their size or magnitude. The magnitude of a number is its distance from zero (as mentioned above). Since the absolute value of -10 is 10 and the absolute value of 10 is also 10, neither number is greater than the other. However, if we are talking about the relative position of the two numbers on a number line, then we can say that -10 is to the left of 10 (since negative numbers are to the left of zero), but this does not mean that -10 is greater than 10.

Therefore, Kris is incorrect and Tin is also incorrect.

15. how to write a authorisation letter to get my TIN number at BIR?​


TIN Registration Process for Individuals

Individuals are automatically assigned a tax identification number based on their Bank verification number (BVN) or National Identification Number (NIN).

STEP 1: Visit the verification portal – Joint Tax Board.

STEP 2: Select your date of birth.

STEP 3: Select your preferred search criteria (BVN, NIN or registered number) from the drop-down menu.

STEP 4: Enter the digit of the search criteria you selected in the previous step. e.g. if you selected BVN in STEP 3: enter your BVN digit.

STEP 5: Click the reCAPTCHA box to verify that you are not a robot.

STEP 6: Click search to get your TIN

TIN Registration Process for Non-individuals

Here are the TIN registration process for non-individuals (Companies, Enterprises, Cooperative societies, etc.)

STEP 1: Visit the verification portal (https://tinverification.jtb.gov.ng/Template/TINVerification)

STEP 2: Select the date of incorporation of your organization

STEP 3: Select your preferred search criteria (BVN, NIN or registered number) from the drop-down menu

STEP 4: Enter the number in the search criteria you selected in the previous step. e.g. if you selected BVN in step 3, enter your BVN digit. Note that the CAC registration number should be typed in BN1234 or IT1234 or RC1234 format.

STEP 5: Click the reCAPTCHA box to verify that you are not a robot.

STEP 6: Click search to get your TIN

How to verify TIN Pin

STEP 1: Visit https://apps.firs.gov.ng/tinverification/

STEP 2: Select your preferred search criteria (BVN, NIN or registered number) from the drop-down menu

STEP 3: Enter the digit of the search criteria you have chosen. CAC registration number should be entered in BN1234 or IT1234 or RC1234 format.

STEP 4: Input the correct captcha image displayed

STEP 5: Click on search to verify your TIN pin.

19. The number n of tin cans produced by a machine varies directly to the amount of time t. How many tin can will be produced in 10 hours if the machine produces 12,500 tin cans in 14 hours?


DIRECT VARIATIONGiven: The number n of tin cans produced by a machine varies directly to the amount of time t. How many tin cans can be produced in 10 hours if the machine produces 12,500 tin cans in 14 hours?

It is a direct variation for if the number of tin cans produced by a machine increase then the amount of time also increases, and if the number of tin cans produced by a machine decrease then the amount of time also decreases.

EQUATION: [tex]n = kt[/tex]

[tex]12 \: 500 = k \: 14[/tex]

[tex]k = \frac{12 \: 500}{14} [/tex]

[tex]k = 893[/tex]

————————

[tex]n = kt[/tex]

[tex]n = (893)(10)[/tex]

[tex]n = 8 \: 930[/tex]

Final Answer:

8, 930 tin cans can be produced in 10 hours if the machine produces 12,500 tin cans in 14 hours.
