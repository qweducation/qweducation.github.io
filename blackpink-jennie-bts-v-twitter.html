1. The speed v of an object is given by the equation v = At3 – Bt, where t refers to time. (a) What are the dimensions of A and B? (b) What are the SI units for the constants A and B?​


Answer:

Dimensions of A and B are as follows: A= u/[tex]t^3[/tex] or [tex]\frac{length/time}{time^3}[/tex] B= [tex]\frac{length}{time^2}[/tex] and their units are A= [tex]\frac{m}{s^4}[/tex] and B = [tex]\frac{m}{s^2}[/tex]

Explanation:

Speed or velocity has the unit of length per time specifically in SI it is in meters per seconds. In the given problem we have an equation where velocity varies with time and with constants A and B.

From the equation of motion we know that v= u + at where

v= final velocity with unit m/su= initial velocity with unit m/sa= acceleration with unit m/[tex]s^{2}[/tex]t= time with unit s

Based on this motion equation we can see that u = A[tex]t^3[/tex] and at = -Bt

We need v to have a unit of m/s, that means both of the terms in the given velocity equation to have a unit of m/s.

By dimensional and unit analysis, constant A should have a dimension of [tex]\frac{u}{t^3}[/tex] or [tex]\frac{length per time}{time^3}[/tex] with unit of [tex]\frac{m/s}{s^3}[/tex] or simply m/[tex]s^4[/tex], while constant B should have dimension of a or  [tex]\frac{length}{time^2}[/tex] with unit of m/[tex]s^2[/tex].

17. Alex and Jenny are twin. Alex (at age 21) travels with speed 82% speed of light. When he returns home, Jenny is 61 years old. How old is Alex? γ=11−(v/c)2√ and t=t0γ , where t0 is Alex's perception of time-pass and t is the Jenny's perception of time-pass a. 88.9 b. 57.8 c. 54.0 d. 76.9 e. 43.9


We can use the time dilation formula to solve this problem. According to the formula, the time experienced by Alex (t) is related to the time experienced by Jenny (t0) by the following equation:

t = t0 / γ

where γ is the Lorentz factor given by:

γ = 1 / sqrt(1 - (v/c)^2)

Here, v is the speed of Alex relative to Jenny, and c is the speed of light.

Since Alex travels at 82% the speed of light, we have:

v/c = 0.82

Substituting this into the equation for γ, we get:

γ = 1 / sqrt(1 - 0.82^2) = 2.75

Now, we know that when Alex returns home, Jenny is 61 years old. Let's denote Alex's age by x. Then, according to the problem:

t = x - 21 (since Alex travels for some time and returns home at age 21)

t0 = 61 (since Jenny's age is given)

Substituting the values into the equation for time dilation, we get:

x - 21 = 61 / 2.75

Solving for x, we get:

x = 21 + 61 / 2.75 = 76.9

Therefore, Alex is 76.9 years old. Answer: (d) 76.9.
