9. Pit Senyor Santo Niño de Cebu Sulog One beat, One Dance, One Vision​


Answer:

"Pit Senyor" is a phrase commonly used during the Sinulog Festival in Cebu City, Philippines. It is a chant in honor of the Santo Niño de Cebu, which means "Hail, Lord Senior Santo Niño!" "Sulog" means "current" or "flow," and it refers to the strong current that flows through the channels of Cebu's Pahina River.

"One beat, One Dance, One Vision" is a tagline that represents the unity and solidarity of the people of Cebu during the Sinulog Festival. It symbolizes the idea that despite the diversity of cultures and backgrounds, everyone comes together to celebrate the same thing, which is the devotion to the Santo Niño de Cebu. During the festival, people dance to the beat of the drums and chant "Pit Senyor" as a sign of reverence and devotion to the Holy Child.

Ano ang tawag sa Laguna noong 1500-1600?

Saysaybagin (Nobyembre 14, 2014)

Ayon sa pinakaunang diksiyonaryo (1613 Vocabulario de Lengua Tagala), ang tawag sa Laguna ay “Pulilan” :

“Laguna : Pulilan pp : M. de Bay en respeto de Manila, namumulilan .3.ac. ir a contratar de Manila y sus contornos a la laguna de Bay, ypinamumulilan .3.P. lo q llenan, aco, y, namumulilan, voy a la laguna a tratar, tagapulilan ca, eres natural de la laguna? preguntan en Manila “ (pahina 383)

(“Namumulilan” ang tawag kung pupunta sa Laguna mula Maynila. “Taga-Pulilan” ang tawag sa mga taga-Laguna ng mga taga-Maynila).

“Doongan” naman ang tawag sa Laguna ng mga Tinguianes:

“Laguna : Doongan pp : respeto de los del Bondo y Tinguianes, tutugpa aco sa doongan, boy a la laguna doon sasang doongan man, aunque sea alla en toda la laguna.” (pahina 383).
