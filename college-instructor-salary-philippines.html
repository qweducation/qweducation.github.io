12. 2. The newly-appointed president of one university wanted to know the mean salary of college deans in other private universities. Reports claim that the college deans in other private universities have mean monthly salary of Php52,000. A sample of 35 college deans receives a mean monthly salary of Php55,000. Using α = 0.01, test the claim that the college deans in other private universities have mean monthly salary of Php52,000 monthly. The standard deviation of the population is Php4750. Use = 10%. A. State the null and alternative hypotheses B. Determine the statistical test to be used C. State the decision rule D. Show and compute the test-statistics value (computed value) E. State the decision to make based on the decision rule F. Interpret the result.


Step-by-step explanation:

The null and alternative hypotheses are:

H0: μ = 52,000 (the mean monthly salary of college deans in other private universities is Php52,000)

Ha: μ > 52,000 (the mean monthly salary of college deans in other private universities is greater than Php52,000)

We will use a significance level of α = 0.01, which means we will reject the null hypothesis if the p-value is less than 0.01.

The test statistic for a one-sample t-test is:

t = (x̄ - μ) / (s / √n)

Where x̄ is the sample mean, μ is the hypothesized population mean, s is the sample standard deviation, and n is the sample size.

Plugging in the given values, we get:

t = (55,000 - 52,000) / (4,750 / √35)

t = 5.99

Using a t-table with 34 degrees of freedom (df = n-1 = 35-1), we find that the critical value for a one-tailed test with α = 0.01 is 2.711. Since our calculated t-value of 5.99 is greater than the critical value of 2.711, we can reject the null hypothesis.

To calculate the p-value, we look up the area under the t-distribution curve to the right of our calculated t-value of 5.99. Using a t-distribution table or calculator, we find that the p-value is less than 0.0001.

Since the p-value is less than 0.01, which is our significance level, we reject the null hypothesis and conclude that there is sufficient evidence to support the claim that the mean monthly salary of college deans in other private universities is greater than Php52,000.
