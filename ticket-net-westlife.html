1. A charity organization had to sell a few tickets to their fundraiser just to cover necessary production costs. After selling 10 tickets, they were still at net losss of $800 (due to production costs). they sold each ticket for $70. Let y represent the net profit (in dollars) when they have sold x tickets. complete the equation for the relationship between the net profit and number of tickets sold. y=?


Answer:

y = 70x - 1500

Step-by-step explanation:

The problem can be best represented by the slope-intercept form with the slope as 70.

y = mx + b

-800 = 70(10) + b

-800 = 700 + b

-1500 = b

We can then substitute this to the general equation,

y = 70x - 1500

20. five hundred tickets for a benefit show netted php 3,355. Balcony tickets are sold at php 7.50 each and orchestra tickets at php 2.50 each. how many tickets of each kind were sold?​


The balcony tickets sold were 425 while the orchestra tickets were 75.

Step-by-step explanation:

Be discovered:

Five hundred tickets for a benefit show netted php 3,355.

Balcony tickets are sold at php 7.50 each and

Orchestra tickets at php 2.50 each.

Question:

How many tickets of each kind were sold?​

Asked:

Let balcony ticket = x and orchestra ticket = y

x + y = 500

7.50x + 2.50y = 3,355

Eliminate y to get x.

x + y = 500                      |*2.50|     2.50x + 2.50y = 1,250

7.50x + 2.50y = 3,355    |*1      |    7.50x + 2.50y = 3,355 -

                                                       -5x                   = -2,100

                                                           x                   = 425

Substitute the value of x to get the value of y.

x + y = 500

425 + y = 500

y = 500 - 425

y = 75

The balcony tickets sold were 425 while the orchestra tickets were 75.
