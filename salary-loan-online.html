A cost that the lender assesses you for using its funds is known as interest. It is the additional amount you must pay toward the loan each month, on top of the amount you borrowed.

Understanding the components that determine your interest payment—principal, loan period, and interest rate—is essential before learning how personal loan interest is calculated.

The amount you borrow from the lender is known as the loan principal. The more principle you have, the more interest you'll have to pay.

The period it will take you to repay your loan in full is known as the loan term (or tenor). Typically, it is stated in terms of months or years. 23. Problem: A salary loan amounting to ₱15,000 is to be paid annually for 4 years with an interest rate of 6% compounded annually. The annual amortization is ₱4,328. 87.


Answer:

To determine the total amount of interest paid over the 4-year period of the loan, you can use the following formula:

total interest = loan amount x interest rate x number of years

Plugging in the values given in the problem, we get:

total interest = 15,000 x 0.06 x 4

total interest = ₱3,600

To determine the total amount paid over the 4-year period, you can add the total interest to the loan amount:

total amount paid = loan amount + total interest

Plugging in the values, we get:

total amount paid = 15,000 + 3,600

total amount paid = ₱18,600

To determine the total amortization paid over the 4-year period, you can multiply the annual amortization by the number of years:

total amortization = annual amortization x number of years

Plugging in the values, we get:

total amortization = 4,328.87 x 4

total amortization = ₱17,315.48

Therefore, the total amount of interest paid over the 4-year period is ₱3,600 and the total amount paid is ₱18,600. The total amortization paid is ₱17,315.48.
