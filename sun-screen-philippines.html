11. how much sun screen do i need to place to cover my whole body?


Answer:

You should apply 1 shot glass full (about 1 to 1.5 ounces) of sunscreen to cover your entire body. This works out to about a teaspoon for your face and 1 teaspoon for each arm/hand.

Explanation:

Most adults need about 1 ounce — or enough to fill a shot glass — to fully cover their body. Don't forget to apply to the tops of your feet, your neck, your ears and the top of your head. Apply sunscreen to dry skin 15 minutes before going outdoors.

20. A television screen are measured by the length of the diagonal screen. Find the width of a 21 inches television screen if the diagonsl makes an angle of 36Â° with the base of the screen.


Answer:

15.22 inches

Step-by-step explanation:

Let us denote the television screen as rectangle ABC with vertices A, B, C, and D.

We will also denote the line segments as the following:

AB = Upper length

CD = Lower length/base

AC = left side width

BD = right side width

AD and BC = diagonals

For the problem, we will use CD as the base, diagonal BC and the width BD. If you illustrate this one, you will come up with a right triangle.

So this means that our hypotenuse is the diagonal BC and the angle BCD measures 36°.

Here are the measurements of the right triangle

[tex]m \angle BCD = 36 \degree \: \\ m \angle C = 90 \degree \\ m \angle CBD = 180 \degree - 36 \degree + 90 \degree \\ = 54 \: \degree[/tex]

[tex]CD= 21 inches \\

BC= unknown \\

BD = unknown[/tex]

To find the width, we will use the formula:

[tex]tan \theta =\frac{opposite}{adjacent} \\ [/tex]

[tex]tan 54 \degree = \frac{21}{BD} \\1.38= \: \frac{21}{BD} \\ \frac{1.38}{21} = \frac{1}{BD} \\ \frac{21}{1.38}=BD \\ BD=15.22 [/tex]

Therefore the width is 15.22 inches.
