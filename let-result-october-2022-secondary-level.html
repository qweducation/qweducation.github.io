1. Primary Prevention

Primary prevention of COVID-19 should aim at avoiding the development of a disease or disability in healthy individuals. Most population-based health promotion activities, such as encouraging social distancing (figure 2), adopting handwashing practices and correct wearing of masks to reduce risk of acquiring the COVID-19 infection.

2. Secondary Prevention

The focus of secondary prevention is early disease detection, making it possible to prevent the worsening of the disease and the emergence of symptoms, or to minimize complications and limit disabilities before the disease becomes severe. In this case of COVID-19 the secondary prevention also includes the detection of disease in asymptomatic patients with screening or diagnostic testing and preventing the spread of this communicable disease. Examples in this strategy of prevention include screening for the COVID-19 to all travellers coming into the country. This level of prevention is expensive as it involves use of diagnostic equipment and other supplies. 3.1.3

3. Tertiary Prevention

The goal of tertiary prevention is to reduce the negative impact of an already-established disease by restoring function and reducing disease related complications. Tertiary prevention also aims to improve the quality of life for people with COVID-19 disease. In the situation of COVID-19 this could involve use of antiretroviral drugs to prevent the multiplication and damaging effects of the virus in the lungs to be particular. The COVID-19 associated pneumonia can be so severe that the patient could require mechanical ventilation. Answer: SIMPLE PAST TENSE OF VERB; Typically, you would form the past tense as follows: Take the root form of the verb (the one you will find in our amazing dictionary) and add –ed to the end. If the verb ends in -e, you would just add a -d. For example, the simple past tense of look is looked, and the simple past tense of ignite is ignited.

 PAST PERPECT TENSE OF VERB; The past perfect tense is formed by using the word had followed by the past participle of the verb. For regular verbs, the past participle is a form of the verb that ends in -ed or -d. For example, the past participle of watch is watched

THIS IS THE DEFFERENCE; These two tenses are both used to talk about things that happened in the past. However we use past perfect to talk about something that happened before another action in the past, which is usually expressed by the past simple.
