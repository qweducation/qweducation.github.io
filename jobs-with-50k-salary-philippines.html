9. In finding a new job even with this pandemic. What should I choose? A low salary job but fits my passion or the high paid job but never my passion?


Choosing between a low-salary job that fits your passion and a high-paid job that doesn't may depend on your personal priorities and circumstances. Here are some points to consider:

1. Financial stability: If you have immediate financial needs, a high-paid job may be necessary to cover your expenses. However, if you have enough savings or support to cover your expenses, you may prioritize your passion over salary.

2. Long-term goals: If your goal is to have a long-term career in a certain field, pursuing a job that fits your passion may provide more opportunities for growth and fulfillment in the long run.

3. Work-life balance: Pursuing a job that fits your passion may provide more job satisfaction and work-life balance, even if the salary is lower. However, a high-paid job may require longer working hours and more stress, which can affect your well-being.

4. Skills and experience: If you are just starting out in your career or have limited experience, taking a high-paid job that is not your passion may provide valuable skills and experience that you can use later in pursuing a job that fits your passion.

Ultimately, the decision depends on your personal circumstances and priorities. You may weigh the pros and cons of each option and decide which one aligns best with your goals and values.

Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

For 5 years, she earned $187,345

Then for a year (at previous job), she earns

[tex] \implies\frac{187345}{5}[/tex]

[tex]=37469[/tex]

For a year, she's earning $37,469 at previous job

Each year, Francesca earns a salary that is 2% higher

Now she earns

[tex] =\$37,469+2\%[/tex]

[tex] =\$37,469+(37,469\times2\%)[/tex]

[tex] =\$37,469+\left(37,469\times\frac{1}{5}\right)[/tex]

[tex] =\$37,469+\left(\frac{37,469}{5}\right)[/tex]

[tex] =\$37,469+7,493.8 = \bold{\$44,962.8} [/tex]
