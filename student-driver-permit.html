C) A local high school needs to purchase a park permit for $250.00 for their upcoming senior class picnic. Each student attending the picnic pays $0.75. Each guest pays $1.25. Suppose 200 students attend the picnic. What is x, the number of guests needed to cover the cost of the permit?

Step-by-step explanation:

Inequality equation:

(0.75)(200) + 1.25x ≥ 250.00

Where:

Number of senior students: 200

Amount paid by each student: US$ 0.75

Part permit cost: US$250.00

Number of guests: x

Amount paid by each guest: US$1.25

Condition:

The upcoming senior class picnic activity requires park permit fee of US$ 250.00. The class must collect fees from students and guests with a total minimum of US$250.00.

ANSWER (C.):

C) A local high school needs to purchase a park permit for $250.00 for their upcoming senior class picnic. Each student attending the picnic pays $0.75. Each guest pays $1.25. Suppose 200 students attend the picnic. What is x, the number of guests needed to cover the cost of the permit?

To solve for the minimum number of guests (x) to meet the parking fee cost:

0.75(200) + 1.25x ≥ 250.00

150.00 + 1.25x ≥ 250.00

1.25x ≥ 250.00 - 150.00

1.25x ≥ 100.00

1.25x/1.25 ≥ 100.00/1.25

x ≥ 80

80 is the minimum number of guests required to meet the parking permit fee.
