30. A young engineer borrowed P12,000 at an interest rate of 10 percent per annum. Find the lump sum that he will pay at the end of 4 years. Answer: P17,569.20


Answer:

Explanation:

To calculate the lump sum that the young engineer will pay at the end of 4 years, we can use the formula for compound interest:

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

where:

A = the total amount to be paid (lump sum)

P = the principal amount borrowed

r = the interest rate per annum (as a decimal)

n = the number of times the interest is compounded per year

t = the time period in years

In this case, P = P12,000, r = 0.10, n = 1 (compounded annually), and t = 4. Plugging these values into the formula, we get:

A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)

A = P(1 + r/1)^(1*4)

A = P(1 + 0.10)^4

A = P(1.10)^4

A = P(1.4641)

A = P17,569.20

Therefore, the young engineer will pay a lump sum of P17,569.20 at the end of 4 years.
