18. 241 95 Am is used in many home smoke alarms. It has a decay half-life of 457 years. How long does it take for the americium in a smoke alarm to decay to 6.25% of its original mass?


✏️HALF-LIFE

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

The problem asks to solve for the time taken by Am-241 to decay to 6.25% of its original mass. First, we need to identify the quantities to be used. These are

[tex]m_{0} =[/tex] original mass of the sample

m = final mass of the sample

t = time of decay

[tex]t_{\frac{1}{2}} =[/tex] half-life

k = rate constant

Second, we need to identify also the given values. These are

[tex]m = 6.25\% m_{0} = 0.0625m_{0}[/tex]

[tex]t_{\frac{1}{2}} =[/tex] 457 years

Third, we need to calculate the rate constant. The rate constant is

[tex]k = \dfrac{ln(2)}{t_{\frac{1}{2}}}[/tex]

[tex]k = \dfrac{ln(2)}{\text{457 years}}[/tex]

k = 0.00151673 /year

Finally, we can now solve for the time taken by Am-241 to decay. Therefore, the time taken is

[tex]t = -\dfrac{ln(\frac{m}{m_{0}})}{k}[/tex]

[tex]t = -\dfrac{ln(\frac{0.0625m_{0}}{m_{0}})}{\text{0.00151673 /year}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{t = \text{1,828 years}}[/tex]
