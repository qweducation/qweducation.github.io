What are ions? 

- When an atom gains an electron from other atoms or loses an electron, they will become ions. A negatively charged ion (acquired electrons) is called an anion, and a positively charged ion (stripped-off electrons) is called a cation. 

The kind of attraction created when two opposite charges interact, like an anion (negative) and a cation (positive), is called an ionic bond or ionic bonding. Positive attracts negative, and both positive and negative pairs repel. This creates an ionic bond.

What is an atom?

The atom is the tiniest particle of a particular element that is able to exist on its own or in combination with other elements.

Parts of an atom

1. Neutron - The uncharged or neutral elementary particle found inside the nucleus of an atom.

2. Electron - Negatively-charged particle that moves and vibrates around the nucleus of an atom.

3. Proton - The positively-charged particle found inside the nucleus along with the neutron.

an = a1 +d(n - 1)

a1 - first term

d - common difference

n - number/order of terms
