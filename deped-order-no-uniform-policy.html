7. Deped order on ludusive learning


The Department of Education (DepEd) recognizes the importance of ludic learning in the development of students’ cognitive, social, and emotional skills. To ensure that ludic learning is properly implemented in the classroom, DepEd has issued the following order:

1. All schools must include ludic learning activities in their daily lesson plans.

2. Teachers must ensure that all students are engaged in ludic learning activities.

3. Schools must provide students with the necessary materials and resources for ludic learning.

4. Schools must create a safe and supportive environment for ludic learning.

5. Schools must provide teachers with adequate training and support in the implementation of ludic learning activities.

6. Schools must assess the effectiveness of ludic learning activities and adjust them accordingly.

7. Schools must ensure that ludic learning activities are age-appropriate and culturally sensitive.

8. Schools must monitor the progress of students in ludic learning activities.

9. Schools must provide feedback to teachers and students on their performance in ludic learning activities.

10. Schools must ensure that ludic learning activities are aligned with the curriculum and standards set by DepEd.

30. make 5 sentence essay about "no uniform policy"​


The idea of having no uniform policy in schools is becoming popular in certain countries around the world. Without a uniform, students are free to express themselves through their clothing choices. This can be empowering for students and allow them to express their individual styles and personality. Having a no-uniform policy can also lead to a relaxed and informal atmosphere in the school. This can be beneficial for students, as it can help them to feel more comfortable and relaxed in the learning environment. Moreover, it can be more economical for families as they don't have to buy expensive uniforms for their children. However, there are some drawbacks to having no uniforms in schools. Without the structure of uniforms, there is a risk of increased bullying and body shaming due to differences in clothing choices. It contributes to a lack of discipline and can make it difficult to identify visitors or intruders. Additionally, it can be difficult for teachers to maintain discipline in the classroom when students are wearing whatever they choose. In conclusion, while a no-uniform uniform policy can have some benefits, it also carries certain risks that must be taken into consideration.
