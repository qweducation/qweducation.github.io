20. explain how too much carbon dioxide in the atmosphere affects the oceans pH.​


Excess carbon dioxide (CO2) does not remain in the atmosphere and are often absorbed by large bodies water such as ocean, seas ... etc

Absorption of carbon dioxide by ocean reduces seawater pH, makes the ocean more acidic.

Lower pH- = The more acidic it is

The chemical reaction between Water (H2O)  and Carbon Dioxide (CO2) forms Carbonic Acid (H2CO3). Carbonic acid, while weak as compared to strong acids, still has the ability to dissolve solids and produce Hydrogen Ions (H+).

Higher Hydrogen Ion = The more acidic the ocean is.

Oceans are also rich in Calcium Carbonate, which is what shellfish and skeletal organisms are made up of. The absorption of CO2 greatly affects the abundance of this mineral which then affects the ability of some organisms to produce and maintain their shells.

26. the pH of lemon juice is 2 and the pH of orange is 4 which of these is more acidic and by how much? what does this mean?​


Answer:

The pH is a 1 to 14 scale that measures how acidic or basic is a solution, at lowest number the solution will be acid, in 7 the solution will be neutral, and in higher values will be basic. It is a logarithmic scale, so with each number you move, the acidity or basicity will be increased by a factor of 10. So, lemon juice is more acidic than orange juice by a factor of 100.
