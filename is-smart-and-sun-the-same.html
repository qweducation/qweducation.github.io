12. what is Comparative of smart and what is Superlative of Smart?​


Answer:

smarter (comparative)

smartest (superlative)

Answer:

Adjectives have three degrees that compare one thing to another.

The three degrees of adjectives are positive, comparative and superlative. The comparative and superlative degrees are used to compare between two or more subjects or objects.

For example,

Tina is smarter than Abhishek

In this sentence, the comparative degree (smarter) of the adjective 'smart' is used to compare between the two persons.

Tina is the smartest girl in the class

In this sentence, the superlative degree (smartest) of the same adjective is used to compare a person against two or more people.

The superlative adjective is also used to qualify a person or an object as the best among all its counterparts. For example, Mount Everest is the highest mountain in the world.

22. Can a smart tv have both WI-FI and LAN connectivity? If not, why? If so, can it both work at the same time?​


QUESTION

======

Can a smart tv have both WI-FI and LAN connectivity? If not, why? If so, can it both work at the same time?​

ANSWER

======

Depends on your POV, and your Router.

ETHERNET is the LOCAL wiring, or wireless, to your local router / gateway.

ONLY the local router / gateway is on the internet (hopefully). It’s a DOOR, allowing only OUTGOING, not INCOMING messages on your local ethernet.

For your TV to be connected to the Internet, it has to have 3 things. A local ethernet address (192.168.x.x), a SUBNET Mask the Router supports (Usually 255.255.x.x., but this can vary), and a gateway address (the router’s internal address). So, if your TV is not “Connected to the Internet”, it doesn’t have one of these three parameters correct.

Probably, it doesn’t have the gateway address of the router.
