3. Accountingwhat is the journal entry of this?The bank issued a debit memo, P1,000 for interest charges.​​


Answer:

The journal entry for this transaction would be:

Debit: Interest Expense (or Interest Payable) 1,000

Credit: Bank 1,000

This entry records the increase in the Interest Expense account, which represents the cost of borrowing money from the bank, and the decrease in the Bank account, which represents the reduction in cash due to the payment of interest charges. The debit memo from the bank serves as the source document for this transaction.

8. what is credit and debit?​


Answer:

Debits and credits are equal but opposite entries in your books. If a debit increases an account, you will decrease the opposite account with a credit. A debit is an entry made on the left side of an account. It either increases an asset or expense account or decreases equity, liability, or revenue accounts

18. ACCOUNTING: I HAVE THE ANSWER & SOLUTION, I JUST NEED SHORT EXPLANATION (1 PARAGRAPH) REGARDING THE SOLUTION. How much is the unadjusted book disbursements for December? a. P1,080,275 c. P1,068,175 b. P1,089,925 d. P1,083,875 Answer: a Solution: Adjusted Book Disbursements P1,089,925 Error (1,800) NFS Check, returned by bank in Dec. but recorded in January (4,300) NFS Check, returned by the bank and recorded in December (5,200) Debit memo - this month (2,100) Debit memo - last month 3,750 Unadjusted Book Disbursements P1,080,275 Explanation: ?


Answer:

The unadjusted book disbursements for December are P1,080,275. This is the final amount after adjusting for various errors, including an error of P1,800, an NFS check that was returned by the bank in December but recorded in January for P4,300, an NFS check that was returned by the bank and recorded in December for P5,200, a debit memo for this month for P2,100, and a debit memo for last month for P3,750. By subtracting the total adjustments from the adjusted book disbursements of P1,089,925, we arrive at the unadjusted book disbursements of P1,080,275.
