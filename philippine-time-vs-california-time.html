5. What do the graphs of distance vs. time and distance vs. time² suggest?


Answer: The graph of distance vs. time represents the motion of an object with constant velocity. In this type of motion, the distance covered by the object is directly proportional to the time elapsed. Therefore, the graph of distance vs. time is a straight line with a constant slope, which represents the object's velocity.

The graph of distance vs. time² represents the motion of an object with constant acceleration. In this type of motion, the distance covered by the object is proportional to the square of the time elapsed. Therefore, the graph of distance vs. time² is a parabolic curve, which represents the object's acceleration.

It's important to note that in real-world scenarios, most objects do not experience constant velocity or constant acceleration. In these cases, the graphs of distance vs. time and distance vs. time² may not accurately represent the object's motion. Instead, more complex mathematical models are needed to describe the object's motion.
