Answer:

Baron traded in his old car for a new pick-up truck and the monthly payment for his old car was 7,286.45 with an annual insurance fee of 19,249.76. The insurance fee for the new pick-up truck is 29,363. We need to find out how much more or less Baron is spending on his new pick-up truck compared to his old car.

First, let's find out how much Baron spent on his old car in one year. We know that the monthly payment was 7,286.45 and there are 12 months in a year, so we can multiply those two numbers to find out how much Baron spent in one year.

7,286.45 * 12 = 87,437.40

Then we can add the annual insurance fee of 19,249.76 to find out how much Baron spent in total on his old car in one year.

87,437.40 + 19,249.76 = 106,687.16

Now let's find out how much Baron is spending on his new pick-up truck in one year. We know that the insurance fee is 29,363.

106,687.16 - 29,363.00 = 77,324.16

So Baron is spending 77,324.16 on his new pick-up truck, which is 29,363.00 less than he was spending on his old car.
