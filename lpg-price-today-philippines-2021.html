18. High Price of commodities ( onion,gas,LPG,eggs,sugar ) Viewpoint: 1. 2.3.​


Viewpoint 1: Economic Perspective

From an economic perspective, high prices of commodities such as onions, gas, LPG, eggs, and sugar can be attributed to the law of supply and demand. If the demand for a certain commodity exceeds the supply, the price of that commodity will increase. In the case of onions, for example, a shortage in supply due to weather-related issues or disruption in transportation can drive up the price. Similarly, an increase in demand for gas, LPG, eggs, or sugar due to various factors such as population growth, changes in consumer preferences, or global market trends can also lead to price increases.

Viewpoint 2: Consumer Perspective

From a consumer perspective, high prices of commodities can be a cause for concern, especially for those who have limited income or are on a tight budget. High prices can make it difficult for some consumers to afford basic necessities such as food, fuel, and cooking gas. For example, high onion prices can make it difficult for households to prepare meals that require onions, while high gas and LPG prices can make it expensive for households to cook their meals. High prices of eggs and sugar can also make it challenging for some consumers to purchase these items regularly.

Viewpoint 3: Environmental Perspective

From an environmental perspective, high prices of commodities such as gas and LPG can be an opportunity for promoting the use of renewable energy sources. As the prices of non-renewable fuels such as gas and LPG increase, consumers may be more willing to invest in renewable energy sources such as solar power, wind power, or biomass. Similarly, high prices of commodities such as onions and eggs may encourage consumers to support local farmers and producers, reducing the carbon footprint associated with transportation and storage of these items.
