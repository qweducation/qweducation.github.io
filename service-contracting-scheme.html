In business, a debtor is an entity that owes money to another entity under a contract. A creditor is an entity to which another entity owes money under a contract. Contrary to public order, debtors have certain rights and protections under the law.

Debtors have the right to be treated fairly and with respect. They have the right to clear and concise information about their debt and the options available to them. They also have the right not to be harassed or abused by creditors.

Creditors, on the other hand, have certain obligations under the law. They must act in good faith and deal fairly with debtors. They must provide accurate information about the debt and give debtors reasonable time to pay. Creditors are also prohibited from engaging in unfair or deceptive practices when collecting a debt. If a creditor does not abide by the law, there are several steps that can be taken to resolve the problem.

A stipulation is a condition that must be met in order for something to happen. In contrast, public order is the general welfare or well-being of the public.
