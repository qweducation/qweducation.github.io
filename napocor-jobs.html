1. How do the Maranao people view NAPOCOR?


What are the culture of Maranao?

Together with the Illanun and Maguindanao, the Maranao are one of three indigenous Muslim groups native to the island of Mindanao in the southwestern part of the Philippines, this tribal group share in a generalized Southeast Asian culture but has its own cultural identity.

The Guimba Incident:NAPOCOR:

Answer:

The Maranao people view NAPOCOR as unarmed and ununiformed Armed Forces of the Philippines (AFP).

Explanation:

The Guimba incident would explain why NAPOCOR was viewed as unarmed and ununiformed AFP's. It was on November 3, 1978 when members of the Philippine Constabulary soldiers and several civilians were killed and wounded in an ambush that took place at Guimba, Marawi City. This group was on their way to restore power line that had been bombed earlier by the MNLF. Due to this, innocent civilians were arrested and maltreated including Hadji Salik Kaluwasan, CHDF head in Marawi City who was reported killed in the camp of the 25th infantry battalion. People recall that the incident had changed their peaceful lives into a horrifying experience.

They even recalled that the incident happen on a fine Friday when the members of the 25th infantry battalion had entered their mosques and captured 20 of their male Maranaos including Salik Kaluwasan. These soldiers also ransacked their houses and have planned to pay them an amount of 80, 000 pesos for all the damages including the death of their 20 male members. As form retaliation, workers of the NAPOCOR were ambushed after several months. Since then, the Army and the NAPOCOR were the same. The first was viewed as an  armed and uniformed NAPOCOR unit, while the second was seen as an unarmed and ununiformed AFP (Armed Forces of the Philippines) Unit.
