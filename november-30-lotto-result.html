17. ACCOUNTING: I HAVE THE ANSWER, I JUST NEED SOLUTION & SHORT EXPLANATION. On November 1, 2016, GLOBE CORPORATION, a Philippine company said merchandise to SMART COMPANY, a foreign firm. GLOBE measured and recorded the account receivable from the sale at P78,000. SMART paid for this account on November 30, 2016. Spot rates for the Foreign Currency (FC) on November 1 and November 30, respectively, were P.080 and P0.78 If the sale of the merchandise was denominated in the foreign currency (FC), the November 30 entry to record the receipt of payment from SMART included a A. credit to Accounts Receivable for P76,050 B. credit to Exchange Gain for P1,950 C. debit to Cash for P78,000 D. debit to Exchange Loss for P1,950 ANSWER: D SOLUTION: ? EXPLANATION: ?


Answer:

SOLUTION:

The sale of merchandise was denominated in foreign currency, which means that the account receivable of P78,000 was recorded in FC. On November 30, when SMART paid for the account, GLOBE received FC at a spot rate of P0.78. However, the spot rate on November 1 was P0.80, which means that the FC appreciated against the Philippine peso (PHP).

To record the receipt of payment, GLOBE needs to convert the FC into PHP using the spot rate on November 30. The formula for the conversion is:

FC x Spot Rate = PHP

Using this formula, the conversion of the account receivable of P78,000 on November 30 is:

P78,000 x P0.78 = P60,840

Therefore, the entry to record the receipt of payment from SMART on November 30 is:

Debit Cash P60,840 Debit Exchange Loss P1,950 Credit Accounts Receivable P78,000

The debit to Exchange Loss is necessary because the FC appreciated against the PHP, which means that GLOBE received less PHP than what it recorded as account receivable. The difference between the amount recorded and the amount received is the exchange loss of P1,950.

EXPLANATION:

The entry to record the receipt of payment from SMART on November 30 involves converting the FC into PHP using the spot rate on that day. The difference between the amount recorded as account receivable and the amount received is either an exchange gain or an exchange loss, depending on whether the FC appreciated or depreciated against the PHP. In this case, the FC appreciated, which means that GLOBE incurred an exchange loss of P1,950.

22. On november 1, 20x7, green valley farm entered into a contract to buy a p75,000 harvester from john demarsha. The contract required green valley farm to pay p75,000 in advance on november 1, 20x7. The harvester (cost of p55,000) was delivered on november 30, 20x7. The journal entry for john demarsha to record the contract on november 1, 20x7 includes a:.


Explanation:

On November 1, 20x7, John Demarsha would record the contract with Green Valley Farm in his accounting records by making the following journal entry:

Debit: Accounts Receivable (asset) - P75,000

Credit: Cash (asset) - P75,000

This entry records the receipt of the P75,000 payment from Green Valley Farm as an accounts receivable, which represents an amount that the company is owed by a customer for goods or services that have been delivered or performed. The cash is also recorded as an asset because it represents an inflow of resources that the company can use to meet its financial obligations.

On November 30, 20x7, when the harvester is delivered to Green Valley Farm, John Demarsha would make the following journal entry to record the sale:

Debit: Sales (revenue) - P55,000

Credit: Accounts Receivable (asset) - P55,000

This entry records the sale of the harvester to Green Valley Farm as revenue and reduces the accounts receivable balance, as the customer has now paid for the goods or services provided.

It's important to note that these journal entries are made in accordance with the accrual basis of accounting, which means that transactions are recorded when they occur, regardless of when payment is received or made. This is in contrast to the cash basis of accounting, which only records transactions when cash is received or disbursed.
