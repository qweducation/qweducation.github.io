1. is it allowed to drive a motorcycle in a public road pending release of the certificate of registration?


Answer:

It is generally not allowed to drive a motorcycle on a public road pending the release of the certificate of registration. In most cases, the certificate of registration is required to legally operate a motorcycle on a public road. The certificate of registration serves as proof of ownership and is required to be displayed on the motorcycle at all times when it is being operated on a public road.

If you are caught driving a motorcycle on a public road without a valid certificate of registration, you may be subject to fines and other penalties. It is important to ensure that your motorcycle is properly registered and that you have the necessary documents, including the certificate of registration, before operating it on a public road.

6. Evidences that makes the news validor reliable:​


Answer:

A reliable source is one that provides a thorough, well-reasoned theory, argument, discussion, etc. based on strong evidence. Scholarly, peer-reviewed articles or books -written by researchers for students and researchers.

These sources may provide some of their articles online for free.

Good news sources clearly mark opinion columns as opinion, disclose conflicts of interest, indicate in stories where information was obtained and how it was verified, and provide links to sources. In general, information published by the government is both current and based on reliable research, even if no one author is listed. In general, print publications with authors and listed sources tend to be reliable because they provide sources which readers can verify.
