13. reflection sunday mass october 12nonsense report^^​


Answer:

October 12, 2014—Twenty-Eighth Sunday in Ordinary Time

                                     Matthew 22:1-14

                                                by

                          Father Oscar Lukefahr, C.M.

Imagine this. Your son has been serving in the military and is returning home. He asks you to contact his two closest friends and invite them to dinner. He isn’t sure whether he’ll arrive home on Thursday or Friday, so you call the friends and ask them to save both dates. They enthusiastically agree. Later, your son calls and says that Friday is the day. You notify his friends, but they shock you by saying, "Sorry, we’ve made other plans."

Something like this is behind today’s parable. A king announced the approximate time for his son’s wedding banquet, telling invited guests that the exact date would be given later. Guests who said yes to the advance invitation and no to the later one would insult the king. Those invited were the Israelites. They had accepted God’s invitation to be his people, guests at his banquet. But when Jesus provided the exact date, they refused to come.

Jesus’ parable was meant for the people of his time. It applies also to us. At Baptism and Confirmation we accepted God’s invitation to be members of his kingdom and guests at his heavenly banquet. But saying yes is not a one-time thing. It requires ongoing commitment. We must not be so distracted by everyday events that we forget eternal life. We must not fill ourselves with the junk food of this world and miss God’s heavenly banquet.

Consider Christ’s invitation to the Eucharist: "Do this in memory of me." Do we believe his word that those who eat this bread will live forever? At Mass do we carefully consider the Scriptures and apply them to our lives? Do we appreciate Holy Communion as a foretaste of the heavenly banquet?

We must not imitate the man who came to the feast without wearing proper clothing (which would have been provided at the door). We must come to the Eucharist wearing the garment of God’s grace, decked out in a spirit of enthusiastic participation, eager for the food of God’s Word and Christ’s Body and Blood.

Jesus invites us to the Eucharist ... and to eternal life – invitations to banquets not to be missed!
