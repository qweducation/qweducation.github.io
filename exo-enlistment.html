26. Enlist the science process skills in order


The science process skills can be listed in the following order:

Observing - using the senses to gather information about the world
Communicating - sharing information and data with others
Comparing - identifying similarities and differences between objects and events
Classifying - grouping objects or events based on their characteristics
Measuring - using tools and techniques to quantify or determine the properties of objects or events
Inferring - drawing conclusions based on observations and data
Predicting - making educated guesses about future events based on observations and data
Hypothesizing - proposing possible explanations or solutions to a problem or question
Controlling variables - designing and conducting experiments to test hypotheses while controlling variables
Analyzing data - interpreting data and identifying patterns or trends
Drawing conclusions - making decisions or forming opinions based on evidence and data
Evaluating - assessing the validity and reliability of evidence and data
Applying - using knowledge and skills to solve real-world problems and make informed decisions.
