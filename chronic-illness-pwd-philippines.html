3. how to handle it chronic illness or injury ​


Answer:

Dealing with a chronic illness or injury can be a challenging and overwhelming experience, but there are several steps you can take to help manage and cope with your condition. Here are some strategies you can use to handle a chronic illness or injury:

1.Seek medical attention: The first step to managing a chronic illness or injury is to seek medical attention from a qualified healthcare provider. Your doctor can provide you with a diagnosis, explain your treatment options, and help you develop a care plan that meets your specific needs.

2.Learn about your condition: Educate yourself about your illness or injury. Understanding your condition and its symptoms can help you make informed decisions about your care and lifestyle.

3.Develop a support system: Reach out to family members, friends, and support groups to build a strong support system. Sharing your feelings and experiences with others who are going through similar challenges can be incredibly helpful.

4.Prioritize self-care: Take care of your physical, emotional, and mental health. This may include eating a healthy diet, getting regular exercise, practicing stress-reducing techniques, and getting enough sleep.

5.Manage your medications: If you are taking medication to manage your condition, make sure you understand how to take it properly and any potential side effects. Keeping track of your medications and scheduling refills can help you stay on top of your treatment.

6.Make lifestyle adjustments: Depending on your condition, you may need to make adjustments to your lifestyle. This may include modifying your diet, avoiding certain activities or environments, or learning new coping strategies.

7.Stay positive: Maintaining a positive attitude and outlook can help you cope with the challenges of living with a chronic illness or injury. Focus on what you can do rather than what you can't, and celebrate your successes along the way.

Remember that managing a chronic illness or injury is a journey, and it may take time to find the right treatment plan and coping strategies for you. Be patient with yourself and don't be afraid to ask for help when you need it.

Stunted growth​ is a condition that hampers a person's growth due to severe illness and malnutrition.

Stunting Growth

Stunting growth is a decrease in the rate of growth in human development. It is a major manifestation of malnutrition and recurrent infections, such as diarrhea and helminthiasis, in early childhood and even before birth, due to malnutrition during fetal development caused by an undernourished mother.

Children who are stunted may never regain the height lost by stunting, and most children will never gain the appropriate weight. Living in an environment where many people defecate in the open due to poor sanitation, is an important cause of stunted growth in children.

Causes of Stunting Growth:

Poor maternal nutrition: A child needs the best care both during pregnancy and after birth. Stunted growth in children is thought to be caused, in part, by inadequate breastfeeding and poor maternal nutrition.

Sanitation: The level of sanitation in the home is thought to have an impact on children's development. Poor sanitary conditions frequently cause children to lose their appetite and contract illnesses like diarrhea, which stunts their growth.

One of the main factors contributing to children's stunted growth is stunting malnutrition. Children need the right nutrients, and their growth is negatively impacted by their lack. Due to this, families and communities with high levels of poverty are where incidences of stunted growth in children are most frequently discovered.

Hereditary: It has been discovered that certain children's stunted growth is influenced by hereditary factors. A family with a history of slow growth frequently continues to generate offspring with inadequate physical structure development.

Mother's drug use: Both external factors and the mother are responsible for the child's stunted growth. Children that have a mother who has used illegal drugs, alcohol, or tobacco for an extended length of time frequently have underdeveloped children.

Feeding Methods: Stunted growth in babies can result from inadequate feeding, a lack of essential nutrients, and insufficient calorie intake. To prevent malnutrition, the food should meet the minimum requirements at the right intervals.

27. refers to the wasting of the body due to chronic illness.


Cachexia refers to the wasting of the body due to chronic illness. It is characterized by extreme weight loss and muscle wasting (called atrophy, due to non-use). This syndrome typically inflicts patients who are in the late states of chronic diseases like cancer, COPD, kidney disease, heart failure and those in coma. The term comes from "kakos" from the Greek bad and "hexis" from the Greek meaning condition or "bad condition". More information on the conditions leading to weight-loss due to chronic illness is discussed below.

Categories of Cachexia Prechachexia - is signaled by the involuntary loss of up to 5 percent of the body weight. There is also appetite lost, inflammation and changes in the person's metabolism. Cachexia - is the involuntary lost of more than 5% of body weight over a period of 12 months or less. Refractory Cachexia - refers to individuals with cancer.  

Cachexia is different from regular weigh loss because this is involuntary. If you are losing weight, with no known condition, then it is just regular weight loss. 29. Name two ways to prevent many chronic lung and respiratory illnesses.​


AVOID SECOND- AND THIRD-HAND SMOKE. Breathing the smoke from cigarettes and pipes boosts your risk for the same diseases that affect people who smoke.

STOP SMOKING. Smoking damages your lungs and increases your risk for a number of diseases including lung cancer and etc.

Answer:

-Don't Smoke

Cigarette smoking is the major cause of lung cancer and chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), which includes chronic bronchitis and emphysema. Cigarette smoke can narrow the air passages and make breathing more difficult. It causes chronic inflammation, or swelling in the lung, which can lead to chronic bronchitis. Over time cigarette smoke destroys lung tissue and may trigger changes that grow into cancer. If you smoke, it's never too late to benefit from quitting.

-Avoid Exposure to Indoor Pollutants That Can Damage Your Lungs

Secondhand smoke, chemicals in the home and workplace, and radon all can cause or worsen lung disease. Make your home and car smokefree. Test your home for radon. Avoid exercising outdoors on bad air days. And talk to your healthcare provider if you are worried that something in your home, school or work may be making you sick.

30. what the title of the law for PWD ans senior citizen in the philippines?​


Answer:

The law for PWD (Persons with Disabilities) is the Republic Act No. 7277 or the Magna Carta for Disabled Persons, while the law for senior citizens is the Republic Act No. 9994 or the Expanded Senior Citizens Act of 2010.
