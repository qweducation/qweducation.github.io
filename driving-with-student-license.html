EXPLANATION:

Basically, have, has, and had are primary auxiliary verbs alias main auxiliary verbs. These three verbs have no meaning, but their role is very important in the formation of tenses so that a sentence can give a complete meaning. In addition, the presence of has/have/had will greatly affect grammar or grammar in English sentences. So, as an auxiliary verb, has/have/had acts to help modify the meaning of the other verbs that accompany it.

However, what is unique is that apart from being able to function as auxiliary verbs, have/has/had can also be positioned as main verbs that stand alone. Also, it can work as a modal verb. You can distinguish has and has from the type of subject. Note, have applies to the subjects I, you, we, they, while has applies to the subjects he, she, it.

More specifically, I, you, we are first and second person pronouns. Meanwhile, they are third person plural pronouns. Oh yes, have also applies to subjects with plural nouns. Well, if he/she/it is third person singular pronouns (third person singular pronouns). Then, you can also use has for singular nouns. According to this definition, we can conclude that has applies to men (he) and women (she).
