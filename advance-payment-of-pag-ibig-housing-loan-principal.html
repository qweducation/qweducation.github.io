9. A portion of each payment is for interest while the remaining amount is applied towards the __________________________ balance. a. principal b. interestc. loan term d. interest rate ​


PRINCIPAL BALANCE

A portion of each payment is for interest while the remaining amount is applied toward the principal balance.

Key Points

Each payment made toward a debt has an amount for interest and an amount that is put into the principal balance and amortized.The debtor is responsible for repaying the loan's principal and interest.Paying off a loan can feel like a dance between paying off the principal quickly enough to lower the amount of interest without having large payments because interest accrues on both the primary and previously accumulated interest.Early repayment of the loan has advantages (lower overall cost due to less interest accrued), but disadvantages (less use of the principal).A portion of each payment is for interest while the remaining amount is applied toward the principal balance.The balance of the money you actually borrowed is known as the principle. You begin with a $1,000 "principal balance" if you borrow $1,000.The original loan amount, the interest rate, and the term, or length of the loan, all determine how much of the principle balance is decreased with each payment. It is impossible to provide any kind of detailed answer without knowing these three details because the quantity would change as each of those variables changed.
