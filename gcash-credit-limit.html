7. The Philippines has a large informal economy. What economic problem does this pose? A. Reduced tax revenue for the government B. Increased competition for formal businesses C. Limited access to credit for informal workers


Answer:

A. Reduced tax revenue for the government

Explanation:

The large informal economy in the Philippines poses a problem of reduced tax revenue for the government. Informal workers typically do not pay taxes, which can result in a significant loss of revenue for the government. This can make it challenging for the government to fund important programs and services, such as healthcare, education, and infrastructure development. Additionally, informal workers may not have access to the same protections and benefits as formal workers, such as social security and healthcare benefits. This can contribute to income inequality and poverty.

Answer:

The answer is A, reduced tax revenue for the government, as it is the most pressing economic problem that arises from having a large informal economy in the Philippines.

Explanation:

All of the options listed can be considered as economic problems that arise from having a large informal economy in the Philippines. However, if we had to choose the most pressing economic problem, it would be option A, which is reduced tax revenue for the government.

30. The Philippines has a high level of external debt. What economic problem does this pose? A. Reduced government spending on social welfare programs B. Increased borrowing costs for the government C. Limited access to credit for private businesses


Answer:

B. Increased borrowing costs for the government.

Explanation:

When a country has a high level of external debt, it poses a risk of default, and investors may require higher interest rates to compensate for the increased risk. This means that the government will have to pay more interest on its borrowings, reducing the funds available for other important government programs such as social welfare programs.
