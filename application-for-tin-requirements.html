13. If 21 tins of milk cost $63,What will be the cost of 16 tins


Answer:

The cost of 16 tins is $48.

Step-by-step explanation:

Ratio:

Tins of milk : Cost

Let x be the cost of milk for 16 tins:

Direct Proportion:

In direct proportion, the product of means is equal to the product of extremes.

21 : 63 = 16 : x

Means of proportion: 63 and 16

Extremes of proportion: 21 and x

Solve for x:

21x = (63)(16)

21x/21 = 1,008/21

x = 48

The cost of 16 tins is $48.

25. A cylinder tin can measure 18 cm high and 20 cm in diameter. How many square centimeters of tin will be required for 2.5 dozen cans? Use π = 22/7.


Answer:

To find the surface area of the tin can, we need to calculate the area of the top and bottom circles and the area of the cylinder's curved surface.

The area of one circle is given by:

A = πr^2, where r is the radius of the circle.

Since the diameter is given as 20 cm, the radius is half of that or 10 cm.

So, the area of one circle is A = (22/7) x 10^2 = 314 sq. cm

The area of the curved surface of the cylinder is given by:

A = 2πrh, where r is the radius and h is the height.

So, the area of the curved surface for one can is A = 2 x (22/7) x 10 x 18 = 1131.43 sq. cm (approx.)

The total surface area of one can is the sum of the areas of the top and bottom circles and the area of the curved surface:

Total surface area = 2A(circle) + A(curved surface)

Total surface area = 2 x 314 + 1131.43

Total surface area = 1759.43 sq. cm (approx.)

Since we are asked for the surface area of 2.5 dozen cans, we need to multiply the surface area of one can by 2.5 x 12 = 30:

Total surface area for 2.5 dozen cans = 30 x 1759.43

Total surface area for 2.5 dozen cans = 52783.04 sq. cm (approx.)

Therefore, approximately 52,783 square centimeters of tin will be required for 2.5 dozen cans.
