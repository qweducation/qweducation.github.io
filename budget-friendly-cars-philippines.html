2. What are some budget-friendly accommodations in Palawan?​


Palawan is a popular tourist destination in the Philippines, and there are many budget-friendly accommodations available for travelers. Here are some options:

1.) Hostels - Hostels are a great option for budget-conscious travelers. There are many hostels in Palawan that offer dormitory-style rooms or private rooms at affordable prices. Some popular hostels in Palawan include Mad Monkey Hostel and El Nido Overlooking.

2.) Guesthouses - Guesthouses are another budget-friendly option for travelers. They offer private rooms with basic amenities at affordable prices. Some popular guesthouses in Palawan include La Natura Resort and Puerto Pension.

3.) Homestays - Homestays are a great way to experience local culture and hospitality while staying on a budget. They offer private rooms in local homes at affordable prices. Some popular homestays in Palawan include Ala Amid Bed & Breakfast and Apulit Guesthouse.

4.) Camping - Camping is a great option for adventurous travelers who want to experience the great outdoors. There are many campsites in Palawan that offer basic amenities and beautiful natural scenery. Some popular campsites in Palawan include Nacpan Beach Glamping and Sabang Camping.

These are just a few of the budget-friendly accommodations available in Palawan. It is always a good idea to research and compare prices and amenities before booking to find the best option for your budget and preferences.

21. National Budget proposal for 2020 in the Philippines?


Answer:

President Rodrigo Duterte and his Cabinet approved the national budget proposal prepared by the Department of Budget and Management (DBM) during the monthly Cabinet meeting on Monday, August 5.

The proposed 2020 budget is 12% higher than the 2019 national budget of P3.662 trillion. (READ: Winners and losers under Duterte's 2019 budget)

The DBM aims to submit to Congress the proposed 2020 National Expenditure Program before the August 21 deadline.

The budget will then undergo the scrutiny of the newly-convened 18th Congress, then sent to Duterte's desk for approval.

"This budget proposal is designed to respond to the needs of the majority of our countrymen longing to be uprooted from decades of want of basic necessities, inadequate supply of basic services, lack of infrastructures required to spur economic growth, absence of accountability on government coffers, vexing bureaucratic rigmarole, deprived education and unchanged poverty," Presidential Spokesman Salvador Panelo said in a statement on Tuesday, August 6.

As in previous years, the Department of Education has been allocated the largest slice of the pie, followed by the Department of Public Works and Highways. Also getting big allocations are the Department of Transportation and the Department of Health.

Panelo gave assurances that the funds will be "spent wisely" in order to create a "vibrant economy that will be felt by the citizenry."

Newly-named Acting Budget Secretary Wendel Avisado was present at the Cabinet meeting, as well as Undersecretary Janet Abuel who helmed the department while the 2020 budget proposal was being prepared.

30. IS THE PHILIPPINES A GENDER FRIENDLY WORKPLACE? JUSTIFY YOUR ANSWER


Answer:

In recent years, the Philippines has taken steps towards becoming a more gender-friendly workplace. The country has laws and policies that promote gender equality and prohibit discrimination in the workplace. The Labor Code of the Philippines, for example, prohibits discrimination based on gender, among other factors, and mandates equal pay for equal work.

Additionally, some companies in the Philippines have implemented programs and policies that support the needs and rights of women in the workplace. This includes providing parental leave, breastfeeding breaks, and safe and accessible facilities for women.

However, despite these efforts, there are still challenges that women face in the workplace, such as limited representation in leadership positions, sexual harassment, and unequal pay. The Philippines, like many countries, still has a long way to go in achieving full gender equality in the workplace.
