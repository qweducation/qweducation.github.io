NOTABLE COMPOSER OF ORATORIO

George Frideric Handel, a late Baroque English composer of German ancestry, is best known for his operas, oratorios, and instrumental works. Messiah, the most renowned of all oratorios, was written by him in 1741.

Oratorio is a large-scale musical composition for solo vocals, chorus, and orchestra on a holy or semisacred theme. The text of an oratorio is frequently based on scripture, and the narrative required to go from scene to scene is provided by recitatives performed by different voices to pave the way for airs and choruses. An oratorio, like most operas, features a chorus, soloists, an instrumental ensemble, recognizable characters, and arias.

The three main schools of oratorios are the Italian, which is basically a sort of sacred opera; the German, which arose from the presentation of the Passion tale; and the English, which was created by composer George Frideric Handel from a variety of sources.

18. 5 notable chemist and their contribution ​


Answer:

Here are five notable chemists and their contributions:

1. Marie Curie: Marie Curie was a Polish-born chemist and physicist who is famous for her pioneering work on radioactivity. She was the first woman to win a Nobel Prize and the only person to win Nobel Prizes in two different sciences. Her work laid the foundation for modern nuclear physics and led to the development of new medical treatments.

2. Dmitri Mendeleev: Mendeleev was a Russian chemist who is famous for his creation of the periodic table of elements. He organized the elements by their atomic mass and predicted the properties of undiscovered elements, which were later found and confirmed.

3. Linus Pauling: Pauling was an American chemist who is known for his work on the nature of chemical bonds and the structure of molecules. He developed the concept of hybridization, which describes how atomic orbitals combine to form new hybrid orbitals that determine the shape of molecules. He also worked on the structure of DNA and the effects of radiation on living organisms.

4. Rosalind Franklin: Franklin was a British chemist who made significant contributions to the discovery of the structure of DNA. She used X-ray crystallography to obtain images of DNA, which were used by James Watson and Francis Crick to develop their model of the double helix structure.

5. Robert Boyle: Boyle was an Irish chemist who is known as the founder of modern chemistry. He is famous for his work on the nature of gases and the relationship between pressure and volume, which became known as Boyle's Law. He also made important contributions to the study of chemistry through his experiments on combustion, acids and bases, and the behavior of gases.
