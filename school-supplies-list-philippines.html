Answer:

A requisition slip is a documented form used for procurement of necessary goods for a specific project.

One of the most important things in working with a project are the tools and materials. Discuss and identify the tools or materials needed within a group or department.

Canvass for the unit cost of identified tools and materials.

Indicate the quantity of each item in the "Quantity" section and specify the appropriate unit of measurement in the "unit" section.

Submit slip to an authorized person and seek for approval.

State the description of each item in the "Description" section.

List items to be requested and specify the availability of each item on "Materials & stores" section.

Suggest where requested items can be purchased.

Submit purchasing order to the purchasing officer once approved.

Purchasing officer will write his/her name and affix his/her signature on the "Purchasing Officer" section.

Indicate the cost per unit of each item on the "Unit Cost" section.

Calculate the total cost per item by multiplying unit cost to quantity. Indicate it on the "Total" section.

Write the name of the authorized requisitioner and affix signature on the "Proposed by" section.

Indicate the date of the request on the "Date" section.

Indicate the address of the requisitioner on the "Address" section.
