16. A. Define the following concept: 1. Ad libitum feeding 2. Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR) 3. Restricted feeding 4. Feeding 5. Rationpls answr this​


Answer:

Ad libitum feeding: Ad libitum feeding is a feeding method in which the animal has access to feed at all times, and can eat as much as it wants whenever it wants. This feeding method is also known as free-choice feeding and is commonly used in research settings to ensure that animals are consuming sufficient nutrients.

Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR): Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR) is a measure of how efficiently animals convert feed into body mass or product. It is calculated by dividing the amount of feed consumed by the animal by the amount of weight gain achieved during the same period. A lower FCR indicates that the animal is converting feed more efficiently.

Restricted feeding: Restricted feeding is a feeding method in which the animal's access to feed is restricted to a certain amount or time period each day. This method is commonly used in commercial animal production to optimize growth and minimize feed costs.

Feeding: Feeding is the process of providing animals with food to meet their nutritional requirements. It involves selecting appropriate types and amounts of feed, as well as providing the feed in a manner that the animal can easily consume.

Ration: A ration is a specific amount of feed provided to an animal over a given period. Rations are designed to meet the animal's nutritional needs and can be adjusted depending on the animal's stage of development, activity level, and other factors

Explanation:

In summary, ad libitum feeding refers to animals having access to feed at all times, feed conversion ratio is a measure of efficiency in converting feed to body mass, restricted feeding involves limiting an animal's access to feed, feeding involves providing animals with food to meet their nutritional requirements, and a ration is a specific amount of feed provided to an animal over a given period, designed to meet their nutritional needs.

Answer:   [tex]\frac{1}{90}[/tex]

Explanation:

Step 1: Determine the over-all number of possibilities.

Since a 5-digit PIN code cannot start with zero, therefore the first digit would be restricted by one less digit, and then the rest would be composed of any of the numerical digits from 0 to 9.

Let N be the number of possible PIN number outcomes. Therefore:

N = 9*10*10*10*10

N = 90000 PIN Numbers

Step 2: Determine the number of possible outcomes

To determine the number of possible outcomes, we must identify the constraints first. The constraint is that the pin code should begin in 7 and end at 8, therefore, we have to lock them in on the first and last position. So, the first and last slot should have no other possibility but 7 and 8 in the first and last digit, respectively. This way, we guarantee that the second, third, and fourth digits are the only digits have to worry about the number of possible PIN codes:

Let M be the number of possible codes that starts with 7 and ends with 8. Therefore:

M = 1 * 10 * 10 * 10 * 1

M = 1000 PIN numbers

Step 3: Finalize.

Then, we have to put the two numbers in rational form. Out of 90,000 PIN numbers that are available, there are only 1000 which starts at 7 and ends with 8. In numeric form, it looks like:

P = [tex]\frac{1000}{90000}[/tex]

Then, switch them to lowest terms:

P = [tex]\frac{1}{90}[/tex]

Therefore, there is a [tex]\frac{1}{90}[/tex] chance that the PIN code begins with a 7 and ends with an 8.


24. 1. What are the Advantages and Disadvantage of Restricted Feeding?2. What are the Advantages and Disadvantages of Ad libitum Feeding?pls help :(​


Explanation:

Advantages:

1.Require less knowledge and work: The dogs choose when and how much they eat.

2.Each animal can eat: When several dogs are housed together, ad libitum feeding allows all dogs to eat peacefully. With set meal feeding, dominant dogs sometimes prevent other dogs from eating.

3.Calming effect within kennels: As there is less competition for food, ad libitum feeding usually has a calming effect within kennels.

4.Less coprophagy: Coprophagy (the consumption of feces) is less frequently seen in kennels were dogs are fed ad libitum.

Disadvantages:

1.Loss of appetite and health problems are less obvious: As the dogs are fed less frequently and food consumption is as a consequence monitored less accurately, loss of appetite and health problems are often harder to detect with ad libitum feeding.

2.Some dogs eat too much: Some dogs are not able to regulate their food intake by themselves and have a tendency to eat too much. With greedy dogs, ad libitum feeding is not a good feeding method. Growing dogs younger than 1 year should never be fed ad libitum as pups eat too much when fed this way.
