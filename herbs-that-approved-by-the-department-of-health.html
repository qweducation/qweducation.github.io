22. why is it need a rabies vaccine to be approved by WORLD health organisation even approved of food and drugs​


VACCINE

The World Health Organization (WHO) is a global authority that promotes and protects public health. Rabies is a serious viral disease that affects animals and humans, and it is almost always fatal if left untreated. Therefore, the WHO plays a crucial role in ensuring that the rabies vaccine is effective, safe, and accessible to people around the world, especially in high-risk areas.

The approval of the rabies vaccine by the WHO carries weight and credibility, and it helps to establish an internationally recognized standard for vaccine quality and efficacy. This helps to ensure that people receive the highest quality vaccines available and that they are protected against rabies infection.

Overall, the approval of the rabies vaccine by the WHO is important because it helps to regulate and standardize the quality and safety of vaccines, protect public health, and prevent the spread of dangerous infectious diseases like rabies.
