8. • How important an attribute is salary, and at what point would a higher salary override for you the nonmonetary benefits of the lower-paid position?


ANSWER:

Salary is an important attribute for employees as it is a major factor that affects their quality of life, financial stability, and job satisfaction. Employees need to earn a fair and competitive salary to support themselves and their families, and to maintain their standard of living.

However, there are other nonmonetary benefits that may be more important than salary. For example, flexible work hours, work-life balance, opportunities for career growth and advancement, health and wellness programs, and a supportive work environment are also valuable benefits that can affect employee happiness, productivity, and overall well-being.

At some point, a higher salary may override the nonmonetary benefits of a lower-paid position, especially if the difference in pay is significant and the employee's financial needs outweigh the value of nonmonetary benefits. However, this also depends on the individual's personal priorities, career goals, and overall job satisfaction. Some employees may prioritize career advancement and opportunities for growth over salary, while others may value work-life balance and a supportive work environment.

In general, it is important for employees to carefully consider both salary and nonmonetary benefits when considering a job offer, and to determine their own priorities and needs before making a decision.

10. 5 functions of DENR​


Answer:

1. Assure the availability and sustainability of the country's natural resources through judicious use and systematic restoration or replacement, whenever possible;  

2.  Increase the productivity of natural resources in order to meet the demands for forest, mineral, and land resources if a growing population;  

3.  Enhance the contribution of natural resources for achieving national economic and social development;  

4.  Promote equitable access to natural resources by the different sectors of the population; and  

5.  Conserve specific terrestrial and marine areas representative of the Philippine natural and cultural heritage for present and future generations.

21. How important an attribute is salary, and at what point would a higher salary override for you the nonmonetary benefits of the lower-paid position?​


Money is a tool. It is a tool that allows me to provide for my family, help people and causes I desire to help, and do the things I want to do. If I am in a situation where I have enough of anything or everything for my family, additional money is not an incentive.

My current job provides health benefits, flexible scheduling, and 54 days off per year (holidays plus time off of my choosing, not including weekends). I can take care of my family and spend free time with them and supporting the things I want to support.

In my mind, the only salary bump that I would consider to leave a situation such as this would be one that would allow me to obtain true financial independence (no work and adequate cash flow for all of my needs through investments) within 2–5 years, and even that would be a close thing if you had kids at home and wouldn’t want to sacrifice your free time and flexibility.

To try to put a dollar figure on it:

I live in a low cost of living state in the U.S. where a family that owns their home can easily get by on about $3,000 per month ($36k per year). I just double checked our monthly budget and we could get by comfortably on less than $2k per month ($24k per year) if we needed to.

To walk away from a steady job, I would want at least $1M in an investment portfolio generating a steady return, so I would want to get a net salary of around $250k for 5 years to hit that number, or a salary before taxes of around $350k per year to leave my current job. Without that, I will plug away and enjoy the benefits and free time that my current job provides.
