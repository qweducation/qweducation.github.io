Tribal dance

Include non-Christian dances from the Cordilleras.

A folk dance

is a dance developed by people that reflect the life of the people of a certain country or region. Not all ethnic dances are folk dances. For example, ritual dances or dances of ritual origin are not considered to be folk dances. Ritual dances are usually called "Religious dances" because of their purpose.

Life-cycle dances- dances

that serve as ritual as one passes to a different stage in life such as from birth to childhood to adulthood; from singlehood to marriage; and from life to death. Examples: Courtship Dances, Wedding dances, Funeral dances.

Mimetic or drama dances-

dances that mimic animals, inanimate objects, or other people.

War dances-

express feud and enmity wherein two male dancers engage in physical combat with Spartan-like intensity

Muslim dances-

dances from the people of the Southern Islands of the Philippines such as Mindanao and Sulu.
