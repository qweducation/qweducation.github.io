1. how many moles of CuSO4 are required to react with 13.5g of Al?​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Balance the chemical equation.

2Al + 3CuSO₄ → Al₂(SO₄)₃ + 3Cu

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of CuSO₄ required to react with Al using dimensional analysis.

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 2 moles of Al is stoichiometrically equivalent to 3 moles of CuSO₄.

The molar mass of Al is 26.98 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of} \: \text{CuSO}_4 & = \text{13.5 g Al} \times \frac{\text{1 mol Al}}{\text{26.98 g Al}} \times \frac{\text{3 mol} \: \text{CuSO}_4}{\text{2 mol Al}} \\ & = \text{0.750556 mol} \\ & \approx \boxed{\text{0.751 mol}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, 0.751 mol of CuSO₄ is required to react with 13.5 g of Al.

4. Consider the reaction NaOH + Al → Na3AlO3 + H2. How many moles of aluminum are required to produce 2.4 moles of hydrogen?​


✏️STOICHIOMETRY

[tex]\tt{\huge{\green{Solution:}}}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{gathered} \begin{array}{l} \sf{(a) \: How \: many \: moles \: of \: aluminum \: are} \\ \sf{required \: to \: produce \: 2.4 \: moles \: of \: hydrogen?} \end{array} \end{gathered}[/tex]

The problem asks for the number of moles of aluminum required to produce 2.4 moles of hydrogen. First, we need to balance the chemical equation. The balanced chemical equation is

6NaOH + 2Al → 2Na₃AlO₃ + 3H₂

Second, we need to find the mole ratio (based on the balanced chemical equation) to be used to solve for the number of moles of aluminum. The mole ratio to be used is

mole ratio = 2 mol Al : 3 mol H₂

Finally, we can now solve for the number of moles of aluminum required using dimensional analysis. Therefore, the number of moles of aluminum required is

[tex]\text{moles of Al} = \text{2.4 mol} \: \text{H}_{2} \times \dfrac{\text{2 mol Al}}{\text{3 mol} \: \text{H}_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{moles of Al = 1.6 mol}}[/tex]

----------------------------------------------------

[tex]\begin{gathered} \begin{array}{l} \sf{(b) \: How \: many \: grams \: of \: Na_{3}AlO_{3} \: can \: be} \\ \sf{formed \: from \: 25.5 \: grams \: of \: NaOH?} \end{array} \end{gathered}[/tex]

The problem asks for the mass of Na₃AlO₃ formed from 25.5 grams of NaOH. First, we need to find the molar masses of NaOH and Na₃AlO₃. The molar masses of NaOH and Na₃AlO₃ are

• For NaOH

molar mass of NaOH = 22.99 g/mol + 16.00 g/mol + 1.008 g/mol

molar mass of NaOH = 40.00 g/mol

• For Na₃AlO₃

molar mass of Na₃AlO₃ = 3(22.99 g/mol) + 26.98 g/mol + 3(16.00 g/mol)

molar mass of Na₃AlO₃ = 143.95 g/mol

Second, we need to find the mole ratio (based on the balanced chemical equation) to be used to solve for the mass of Na₃AlO₃ formed. The mole ratio to be used is

mole ratio = 2 mol Na₃AlO₃ : 6 mol NaOH

Finally, we can now solve for the mas of Na₃AlO₃ formed using dimensional analysis. Therefore, the mass of Na₃AlO₃ formed is

[tex]\text{mass of} \: \text{Na}_{3}\text{AlO}_{3} = \text{25.5 g NaOH} \times \dfrac{\text{1 mol NaOH}}{\text{40.00 g NaOH}} \times \dfrac{\text{2 mol} \: \text{Na}_{3}\text{AlO}_{3}}{\text{6 mol NaOH}} \times \dfrac{\text{143.95 g} \: \text{Na}_{3}\text{AlO}_{3}}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Na}_{3}\text{AlO}_{3}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of} \: \text{Na}_{3}\text{AlO}_{3} = \text{30.6 g}}[/tex]

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{1. \: Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of NaOH by using dimensional analysis.

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 3 moles of NaOH is stoichiometrically equivalent to 1 mole of Al(NO₃)₃. Note that the molar mass of Al(NO₃)₃ is 213.0 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of NaOH} & = 2.50 \: \cancel{\text{g} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3} \times \frac{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3}}{213.0 \: \cancel{\text{g} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3}} \times \frac{\text{3 mol NaOH}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3}} \\ & = \text{0.0352 mol NaOH} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Calculate the volume of NaOH solution.

Note that 1 molar (M) is equal to 1 mole per liter (mol/L).

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{volume of NaOH solution} & = \frac{\text{moles of NaOH}}{\text{molarity of NaOH solution}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.0352 mol}}{0.300 \: M} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.0352 mol}}{\text{0.300 mol/L}} \\ & = \text{0.117 L} \\ & = 0.117 \: \cancel{\text{L}} \times \frac{\text{1000 mL}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{L}}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{117 mL}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the volume of NaOH needed is 117 mL.

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{2. \: Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of Al(NO₃)₃.

Note that 25 mL is equal to 0.025 L.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3 & = \text{molarity of} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3 \: \text{solution} \times \text{volume of} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3 \: \text{solution} \\ & = 1.0 \: M \times \text{0.025 L} \\ & = \text{1.0 mol/L} \times \text{0.025 L} \\ & = \text{0.025 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Calculate the mass of Al(OH)₃ produced using dimensional analysis.

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 1 mole of Al(NO₃)₃ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 1 mole of Al(OH)₃. Note that the molar mass of Al(OH)₃ is 78.00 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3 & = 0.025 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3} \times \frac{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Al}(\text{NO}_3)_3}} \times \frac{\text{78.00 g} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3}{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{2.0 g} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the mass of Al(OH)₃ produced is 2.0 g.

3H2SO4 + 2Al(OH)3 → Al2(SO4)3 + 6H2O
