[tex]C \: = 3 \times {10}^{15} farads[/tex]

Solution

Q is the chargeC is the capacitance in FaradsV is the potential difference ( Voltage )

[tex]Q = CV \\ 0.3 \times {10}^{6} = C(1 \times {10}^{ - 10} ) \\ \frac{ 0.3\times {10}^{6}}{1 \times {10}^{ - 10} } = \frac{ C(1 \times {10}^{ - 10} )}{(1 \times {10}^{ - 10})} \\ \\ C \: = 3 \times {10}^{15} farads[/tex]
