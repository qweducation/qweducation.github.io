1. In a farm, there are 48 chickens and rabbits. If the number of animal legs is 130, how many rabbits are there in the farm?* 2 points 18 17 30 3 ​


System of Linear Equations

If there are a total of 48 animals on the farm composed of chickens and rabbits, and the number of legs is 130, there will be 17 rabbits and 31 chickens on the farm. We use a system of linear equations to solve this question.

Here is the complete solution for your activity:

Equation 1: x + y = 48

Let's assume that x represents the rabbits and y represents the chickens.

Equation 2: 4x + 2y = 130

Here, the coefficients are the number of legs represented by the animals on the farm. Rabbits have 4 legs and chickens have 2 legs.

Step 1:

Transforming the first equation in terms of y.

So the first equation from x + y = 48 will become:

y = 48 - x

Step 2:

Substitute the value of y from the first equation to the second equation and perform the operation.

4x + 2y = 130

4x + 2(48 - x) = 130

4x + 96 - 2x = 130

2x = 34

x = 17

Since x represents the rabbits, therefore, the number of rabbits on the farm is 17 but we still need to find the number of chickens.

Step 3:

Substitute 17 for any equations to get chickens.

if x = 17, then

x + y = 48

17 + y = 48

y = 48 - 17

y = 33.

Answer:

There are 17 rabbits and 33 chickens on the farm.

5. A rabbit gives birth to two rabbits . If a rabbit gives birth to 2 rabbits, and two rabbits in turn gives birth to two rabbits each. How many rabbits will there be after 6  number of births???


This problem involves geometric progression..
Remember that the summation of terms in a geometric progression is defined by the formula, [tex] S_{n} = a_{1} \frac{1-r^n}{1-r} [/tex]
where Sn is the total number, a1 is the first term, r is the common ratio or the multiplier to have the next term and n is the number of terms.
Knowing that the first term is 1, r is 2 and n is 6 then you'll have it as:
[tex] S_{n} = 1( \frac{1 - 2^6}{1 - 2} )[/tex]
[tex] S_{n} = 63[/tex]

27. there is a total of 16 chicken and rabbits in a farm. The total number of legs (chicken and rabbits) is equal to 50. how many chickens and how many rabbits are there?


LEGS==============================Question:

There is a total of 16 chicken and rabbits in a farm. The total number of legs (chicken and rabbits) is equal to 50. how many chickens and how many rabbits are there?

Answer:

[tex] \implies \large \sf \orange{7 \: chickens}[/tex]

[tex] \implies \large \sf \orange{9\: rabbits}[/tex]

==============================

Solve:

Let c be the number of chickens and r for rabbits, with a total of 16 animals and 50 legs. Let c multiplied to two because chickens have two legs and four for rabbits.

[tex] \begin{cases} \sf \large c + r = 16 \tt \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: ( \red{eq.1})\\ \sf \large c(2) + r(4) = 50 \: \: \: \! \tt (\red{eq.2})\end{cases} \:[/tex]

[tex] \: [/tex]

Find the the equation of c or r, but I choose chickens. Use the equation 1 for transposing and equation 2 for substituting.

[tex] \begin{cases}\sf \large c = 16 - r \\ \sf \large(16 - r)(2) + r(4) = 50 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \: [/tex]

Solve for the second equation.

[tex] \implies \large\sf(16 - r)(2) + r(4) = 50[/tex]

[tex] \implies \large\sf32- 2r + 4r = 50[/tex]

[tex]\implies \large\sf32 + 2r= 50[/tex]

[tex]\implies \large\sf 2r= 50 - 32[/tex]

[tex]\implies \large \sf 2r= 18[/tex]

[tex]\implies \large \sf \frac{ \cancel2r}{ \cancel2} = \frac{18}{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex]\implies \large \therefore \boxed{\sf \orange{r = 9}}[/tex]

[tex] \: [/tex]

So there are 9 rabbits on the farm, find the number of chickens by using equation 1.

[tex] \implies \large \sf{c} = 16 - 9[/tex]

[tex]\implies \large \therefore \boxed{\sf \orange{c = 7}}[/tex]
