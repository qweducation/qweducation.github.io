There are many different types of families according to Healthy Children Organization. The 7 basic types of family are childless family, reconstituted family, extended family, nuclear family, grandparent family, unconventional family, and single-parent family. Below are the definitions of what each type of family means.

Childless Family
One of the most forgotten types of family. Parents do not want or have children based on their personal or medical reasons. There are several reasons why they cannot have a child. One of the parents could be suffering from certain medical issues so they are advised not to have one. They may be trying to have one but still unsuccessful.

Reconstituted Family
Also called a reconstructed family or a stepfamily. When both parents remarried and at least one of the offsprings from either side are brought up, it is called a reconstituted or reconstructed family.

Extended Family
The most basic type of family is Nuclear Family. If this kind of family extends to the uncles and aunties, grandparents and cousins, it becomes an extended family. Extended families are living in one household or just nearby.

Nuclear Family
The most basic type of family that makes up the majority of our households today. It is composed of two parents and their children living together in harmony. It does not necessary if the children are biological or not (adopted). Aside from these, gender is not a deciding factor to identify if the family is nuclear or not. Parents could be of the same or different sex. More than 70% of the world's family setup falls under this category.

Grandparent Family
If a child or children are raised by grandparents, they belong to the grandparent family type. Regardless of the reason why their are not accompanied by their parents, this type of family occurs mostly in nuclear families where grandparents took over the responsibility of raising their children.
Unconventional Family
The most unexpected type of family in certain norms. In some areas of the world, this type of family is normal. Polygamy is the best example of this. They are composed of children, a father, and multiple mothers.

Single-Parent Family
If one of the parents is widowed, divorced but not remarried, or unmarried, it is called a single-parent family. Whether the household is headed by parents uncles or grandparents individually raising a child, it falls under this category.
