11. wind speed of typhoon yolanda in laoag


Ans: Below 30 kph

During the occurrence of typhoon Yolanda in the Philippines last November 8, 2013, Laoag City in Ilocos Norte is not one of the affected places since it is located in the northern part of Luzon (knowing that typhoon Yolanda mainly affected the places in the Visayas area). 27. Why does the NSO need to survey the number of residents and population of each barangay?


In the study of Social Science, Pumapasok ang ganyang usapin sa "Demography", Demography means study of changes over a period of time in human population.

Example nito ay Birth and Death

Dito, pumapasok ang usaping Survey na yan, para malaman ng gobyerno kung ilan ang bawat meyembro sa isang pamilya. Dito rin na dedetermine yung mga sakit at ilang taon na ang bawat isa.

Halimbawa nito, sa isang baranggay na kalat ang dengue, makikita sa survey ng NSO, kung ilan at kung anong edad ang tinatamaan ng ganung sakit. Kaya nakakagawa ng Hazards o proteksiyon upang maiwasan ang isang problema sa lipunan.

Mahalaga ito, dahil ito ang pinagbabatayan ng gobyerno, maipapasok rin ang tungkol sa economics na pag didistribute ng kakailanganin ng isang buong mamamayan. Dito mapapasok ang bilang ng serbisyo at deman na kailangan sa isang baranggay.


28. wind speed of typhoon yolanda in laoag


Typhoon Haiyan (locally known as Bagyong Yolanda) is the super typhoon and the most powerful typhoon the Philippines has ever experienced. The typhoon had a landfall at Guiuan, Eastern Samar last November 8, 2013. A lot of people have died and a lot properties have been destroyed by this natural disaster. Its wind speed reached 300 kilometer per hour (kph). Signal numbers have been announced on provinces that will be affected by the typhoon as a warning. The city of Laoag in Ilocos Norte was never included among the affected areas of the Typhoon Yolanda since the path of the typhoon was constantly moving northwest with only a slight increase in altitude. The estimated wind speed in Laoag City during the occurrence of Yolanda was just below 30 kph (since the lower end of Signal Number One is 30 kph). Unlike the affected places like Tacloban experienced a wind speed of at least 315 kph as they are classified under Signal Number 4, the whole province of Cebu experienced an average wind speed ranging from 61 to 185 kph (depending if its Southern Cebu that is under Signal Number 2, Metro Cebu that is under Signal Number 3, and Northern Cebu that is under Signal number 4).
