10. What are the belief about solar and lunar eclipse in the Philippines ​


1. The Bakunawa: According to Filipino folklore, eclipses are caused by a sea serpent-like creature called the Bakunawa. The Bakunawa is said to devour the sun and moon, causing the eclipse. To ward off the Bakunawa, people would make loud noises and bang pots and pans to scare it away.

2. Harmful effects: Some people believe that solar and lunar eclipses can cause harm to pregnant women and their unborn children. In some areas, pregnant women are advised to stay indoors during an eclipse and avoid using sharp objects.

3. Rituals and offerings: Some Filipinos believe that eclipses are a time when spirits and ancestors are particularly active, and therefore perform rituals and offerings to appease them. For example, some people offer food and flowers to ancestors during an eclipse.

4. Bad omens: In some parts of the Philippines, eclipses are seen as a bad omen. It is believed that an eclipse can bring disaster, illness, or death. Some people avoid making important decisions or starting new projects during an eclipse.

Overall, the beliefs and superstitions about solar and lunar eclipses in the Philippines are deeply rooted in the country's culture and folklore. While some of these beliefs may seem superstitious or unfounded, they are still respected and observed by many Filipinos.
