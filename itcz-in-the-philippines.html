Answer:

MANILA – The Intertropical Converge Zone ( ITCZ ) affecting Southern Luzon, the Visayas, and Mindanao, will bring rains over most parts of the country, the weather bureau said Friday.

15. difference of monsoon and ITCZ​


Answer:

ITCZ - a zonally elongated axis of surface wind confluence of northeasterly (NE) and

southeasterly ( S E ) trade winds in the tropics.

Monsoon Trough - the portion of the ITCZ which extends into or through a monsoon circulation,

as depicted by a line on a weather map showing the location of minimum sea level pressure. This

line coincides with the maximum cyclonic curvature vorticity, with southwesterly (SW)

monsoonal flow prevailing south of the trough axis.

Monsoon and ITCZ are distinct meteorological phenomena, although they are related. The ITCZ is a zone near the equator where trade winds converge, creating a low-pressure area that results in heavy rainfall. Monsoon is a seasonal change in wind patterns that brings moisture-laden air from the sea towards the land, leading to heavy rainfall over a large region.

18. occurrence of land and sea breezes,monsoons and intertropical convergence zone(ITCZ) in the Philippines ​


Answer:

Explanation:

Land and sea breezes: During the day, the land heats up faster than the surrounding water, causing the air above the land to rise and draw in cool air from the sea, resulting in a sea breeze. At night, the land cools down faster than the water, causing the air above the water to rise and draw in cool air from the land, resulting in a land breeze. These daily cycles of winds occur in coastal areas of the Philippines.

Monsoons: The Philippines experiences two distinct monsoon seasons. During the southwest monsoon, which occurs from May to October, warm moist air from the Indian Ocean and the South China Sea bring heavy rains to the western part of the country. During the northeast monsoon, which occurs from November to April, cool dry air from Siberia brings colder temperatures and less rain to the eastern part of the country.

Intertropical Convergence Zone (ITCZ): The ITCZ is a belt of low pressure near the equator where the trade winds of the Northern Hemisphere and Southern Hemisphere meet. It is characterized by a band of clouds and thunderstorms that move north and south of the equator depending on the season. The ITCZ brings heavy rainfall to the Philippines during the summer months of June to September.
