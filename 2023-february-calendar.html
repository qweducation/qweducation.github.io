24. NO: DATE: February 23, 2023 -A 50-item test was given to a group of 40 Grade Sixper 90 85% of them have a score of 30 and above ! How many pupils got a score of 30 and above?Percentage=Base ×Rate=40x0.85=34answer=34 pupils​


Answer:

If 85% of the 40 Grade Six pupils scored 30 or above on the 50-item test, we can find the number of pupils who scored 30 or above by multiplying 85% by 40:

85% of 40 = (85/100) * 40 = 34

Therefore, 34 pupils got a score of 30 and above on the test.

Step-by-step explanation:

The formula you mentioned, Percentage = Base × Rate, is used to find the percentage of a certain value or base, given a rate or percentage rate.

To use this formula to solve the problem, we can set the base as the total number of Grade Six pupils who took the test, which is 40, and the rate as the percentage of pupils who scored 30 and above, which is 85%. Substituting these values into the formula, we get:

Percentage = Base × Rate

Percentage = 40 × 0.85

Percentage = 34

Therefore, we arrive at the same answer as before, which is that 34 pupils got a score of 30 and above on the test.
