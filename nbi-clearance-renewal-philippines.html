A valid e-mail is important!

12. PHILIPPINE SENATES REJECTS US BASE RENEWAL


This headline is referring to the rejection of a proposal to renew the agreement allowing the United States military to use Philippine military bases. The agreement, known as the Visiting Forces Agreement (VFA), was set to expire in 2021 but the Philippine Senate voted in February 2020 to end it earlier. The VFA had been in effect since 1998 and provided a legal framework for US military personnel to visit and operate in the Philippines. The decision to end the VFA was a result of various factors, including political tensions between the US and the Philippines, concerns over Philippine sovereignty, and opposition from various Philippine groups who viewed the presence of US military personnel as a threat to national security. The rejection of the US base renewal was seen as a significant move by the Philippine government to assert its independence and pursue a more independent foreign policy.
