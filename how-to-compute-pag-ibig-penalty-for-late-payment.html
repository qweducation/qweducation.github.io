Answer:

A) Deposits - $460

B) Total monthly payments - $1920

C) Total cost of computer - $2380

Step by Step explainations:

a) The deposit, is 20% of the cash price. Given that the cash price is $2300, the deposit can be calculated as:

Deposit = 20% of $2300

Deposit = 0.20 * $2300

Deposit = $460

b) The total monthly payments are given as $160 each, and there are 12 equal monthly payments. Therefore, the total monthly payments can be calculated as:

Total Monthly Payments = Monthly Payment * Number of Payments

Total Monthly Payments = $160 * 12

Total Monthly Payments = $1920

c) The total cost of the computer on the payment plan is the sum of the deposit and the total monthly payments. Therefore, the total cost of the computer on the payment plan can be calculated as:

Total Cost of Computer = Deposit + Total Monthly Payments

Total Cost of Computer = $460 + $1920

Total Cost of Computer = $2380

15. Create your own situation that requires to make an amortization schedule. Choose any of the following conditions: Option A: with regular payment Option B: with irregular payment Answer the following questions using the situation you have made. (a) How many regular payments will be made? (b) Using option B, compute the final smaller amount. (c) Compute the interest on the nth payment. You will provide the value of n. (d) Compute the outstanding balance after t years/months. You will provide the value of t.


Option A: With Regular Payment

Situation:

You have taken out a loan of $50,000 at an annual interest rate of 5% to purchase a new car. You have agreed to make monthly payments of $900 for 5 years to pay off the loan.

Questions:

(a) How many regular payments will be made?

There will be 60 regular payments made (5 years * 12 months/year = 60 months).

(b) Using option B, compute the final smaller amount.

This question does not apply to this situation, as it is based on Option B which involves irregular payments.

(c) Compute the interest on the nth payment. You will provide the value of n.

To compute the interest on the nth payment, we can use the following formula:

Interest = Outstanding Balance * Interest Rate

For example, if n = 30 (the 30th payment), the outstanding balance would be $30,000 (the initial loan amount of $50,000 minus the 30 payments of $900 each), and the interest would be $1,500 (30,000 * 0.05 = 1,500).

(d) Compute the outstanding balance after t years/months. You will provide the value of t.

To compute the outstanding balance after t years/months, we can use the following formula:

Outstanding Balance = Initial Loan Amount - (Payment * Number of Payments)

For example, if t = 3 years (36 months), the outstanding balance would be $18,000 (50,000 - (900 * 36) = 18,000). This means that after making 36 monthly payments of $900 each, the outstanding balance on the loan would be $18,000.
