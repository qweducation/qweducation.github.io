13. what is net income and dividends​


Answer:

Net income = profits or losses earned a period of time. Retained earnings = Cumulative net income minus cumulative dividends paid to shareholders. Therefore, logic follows that the amount paid out in dividends is equal to net income minus the change in retained earnings for any period of time.

24. what are the common types of dividends?​


Answer:

Cash dividend.

Stock dividend.

Property dividend.

Scrip dividend.

Liquidating dividend.

Explanation:

Cash dividend- The most common type of dividend payment is a cash dividend. In this case, the company pays a dividend to all shareholders, and the money is deposited in their bank accounts according to their stock holdings. The dividend declaration process is usually predetermined.

Stock dividend- When a company issues additional shares to common shareholders without receiving any compensation, this is referred to as a stock dividend. It will be treated as a stock dividend if the company issues less than 25% of the previously issued stock. It is considered a stocksplit if the issuance of new shares exceeds 25% of the previous issue shares.

Property dividend- Any company can pay its shareholders a non-monetary dividend. The issued property dividend would be deducted from the asset's current market value before being recorded. Because the asset's market price is expected to be either above or below its book value, it will either make a profit or a loss, which will be recorded in the books. Because of this interpretation of the distributed asset, businesses may be forced to issue property dividends on purpose in order to manipulate their taxable income.

Scrip dividend - When any company doesnt have enough funds to pay dividend then it may choose to pay dividend in the form of promissory note to pay the shareholders at a later date. This essentially creates a note payable.

Liquidating dividend: - When the board of the company thinks of returning the original capital invested by the shareholders then it is known as the liquidating dividend. This may happen due to the fact the company intends to wrap up the business.
