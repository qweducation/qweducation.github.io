12. when and why are solar and lunar eclipse occurring?


Answer:

Traditionally, eclipses are divided into two major types: solar and lunar. Solar eclipses occur when the Moon passes between Earth and the Sun, leaving a moving region of shadow on Earth's surface. Lunar eclipses occur when Earth passes between the Sun and the Moon, casting a shadow on the Moon.

28. A solar eclipse occurs when the moon... *


Answer:

What is Solar Eclipse?

Solar Eclipse occur when the Moon comes in between the Earth and the Sun, causing a shadow of the Moon to fall on certain portions of the Earth. Form these locations, it appears as if the Sun has gone dark. Since the moon is very small in front of the Sun, it only blocks a part of it. Hence, a Solar Eclipse cannot be seen from every part of the Earth, but only from the locations where the shadow of the Moon falls.

3 major parts during a Solar Eclipse:

UMBRA - is the portion the the Moon's shadow where the Moon completely covers the sun. This is when the total Solar Eclipse occurs, where you cannot see the sun at all.

ANTUMBRA - is the area beyond the umbra. Here, the Moon is completely in front of the sun, but doesn't cover the entire Sun. The outline of the Sun can be seen around the shadow of the Moon. And that is when you see the Annular Solar Eclipse.

PENUMBRA - is the area of the shadow where only a portion of the Moon is in front of the Sun. This is when the Partial Solar Eclipse occurs, which let's you see only a part of the Sun.

30. What are the belief about solar and lunar eclipse in the Philippines ​


1. The Bakunawa: According to Filipino folklore, eclipses are caused by a sea serpent-like creature called the Bakunawa. The Bakunawa is said to devour the sun and moon, causing the eclipse. To ward off the Bakunawa, people would make loud noises and bang pots and pans to scare it away.

2. Harmful effects: Some people believe that solar and lunar eclipses can cause harm to pregnant women and their unborn children. In some areas, pregnant women are advised to stay indoors during an eclipse and avoid using sharp objects.

3. Rituals and offerings: Some Filipinos believe that eclipses are a time when spirits and ancestors are particularly active, and therefore perform rituals and offerings to appease them. For example, some people offer food and flowers to ancestors during an eclipse.

4. Bad omens: In some parts of the Philippines, eclipses are seen as a bad omen. It is believed that an eclipse can bring disaster, illness, or death. Some people avoid making important decisions or starting new projects during an eclipse.

Overall, the beliefs and superstitions about solar and lunar eclipses in the Philippines are deeply rooted in the country's culture and folklore. While some of these beliefs may seem superstitious or unfounded, they are still respected and observed by many Filipinos.
