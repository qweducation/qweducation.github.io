8. II. Answer the following: 1. Based on the data provided in a known website article entitled "Tuition Fee Guide: 2019 Cost of College Education in the Philippines", the average tuition fee in private colleges and universities is greater than P145,000 a year. Suppose that we want to perform a hypothesis test to find whether the average tuition fee is greater than P145.000. a. Determine the null and alternative hypotheses for the hypothesis test. b. Classify the hypothesis as two-tailed, left-tailed, or right-tailed. 2. A traffic enforcer believes that the number of cars passing through a certain intersection during rush hours in weekdays follows a normal distribution with an average of 800. A new highway is opened, and it is hypothesized that the number of cars passing through the intersection will decrease as a result. A sample of 15 weekdays is taken, and the mean number of cars passing through the intersection is 750 with a sample standard deviation of 42.​


Answer:

1.

a. The null hypothesis is that the average tuition fee in private colleges and universities is not greater than P145,000 per year. The alternative hypothesis is that the average tuition fee in private colleges and universities is greater than P145,000 per year.

H0: μ ≤ 145,000

Ha: μ > 145,000

b. This is a right-tailed hypothesis test, as the alternative hypothesis is testing for a greater value of the population parameter.

2. We can perform a hypothesis test to determine whether there is evidence that the new highway has resulted in a decrease in the average number of cars passing through the intersection during rush hours on weekdays.

a. The null hypothesis is that there is no difference in the average number of cars passing through the intersection during rush hours on weekdays before and after the new highway was opened. The alternative hypothesis is that the average number of cars passing through the intersection during rush hours on weekdays after the new highway was opened is less than the average number of cars passing through before.

H0: μ = 800

Ha: μ < 800

b. This is a left-tailed hypothesis test, as the alternative hypothesis is testing for a smaller value of the population parameter.
