19. section 18 (functions of health and safety representatives) of the OHS Act requires a health and safety representative to perform certain functions. state five of the general functions and provide five permitted functions a safety representative is entitled to do in respect of the workplace​


The OHS Act (Occupational Health and Safety Act) provides a list of general functions that a health and safety representative must perform in the workplace. These general functions include:

Inspecting the workplace regularly to identify potential hazards and unsafe conditions.Investigating accidents, near-misses, and incidents to determine their causes and recommend preventative measures.Participating in the development of health and safety policies and procedures, and promoting these policies and procedures to employees.Conducting or participating in training programs to promote health and safety awareness among employees.Liaising with management to ensure that health and safety concerns are addressed in a timely and effective manner.

In addition to these general functions, a safety representative is entitled to perform certain permitted functions in respect of the workplace. These permitted functions may include:

Identifying and evaluating hazards in the workplace, and recommending appropriate measures to control or eliminate those hazards.Requesting the employer to conduct investigations and risk assessments of workplace hazards.Requesting information from the employer about the hazards and risks associated with work activities.Accompanying inspectors during workplace inspections, and participating in the resolution of any issues identified during the inspection.Stopping work activities if there is a risk of serious injury or harm to employees, and ensuring that the activity does not resume until the hazard has been adequately addressed.
