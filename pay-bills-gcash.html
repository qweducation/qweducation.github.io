22. The snack stand has 59 total bills in the cash register for a total of $895. The snack stand only has twenty-dollar bills and five-dollar bills. How many of each bill does the snack stand have?


The cash register in the snack stand has 40 twenty-dollar bills and 19 five-dollar bills.

Step-by-step explanation:

Step 1. Obtain the following system equations from the given quantities in the problem.

x + y = 59

20x + 5y = 895

where:

x = no. of twenty-dollar bills

y = no. of five-dollar bill

Step 2. Equate for one variable in one of the two equations.

x + y = 59

x = 59 - y

Substitute the value of x to the original equation. Solve the equation with the corresponding operations.

20x + 5y = 895

20(59 - y) + 5y = 895

1180 - 20y + 5y = 895

1180 - 895 = 20y - 5y

285 = 15y

y = 19

Step 3. Solve for the missing variable by substituting the value of y to one of the original equations. Choose the equation which is easier to solve.

x + y = 59

x + 19 = 59

x = 59 - 19

x = 40

Therefore, there are 40 twenty-dollar bills and 19 five-dollar bills in the cash register of the snack stand.

25. A merchant is offered a 5% discount for immediate payment of a bill which is due in 90 days. What is the largest simple interest rate at which he could afford to borrow in order to pay cash


Simple interest is a simpliest and easiest method of calculating interest charge on a certain loan. Simple interest can be determined by multiplying the anual, monthly or daily interest rate by the principal amount by the number of years or time duration of loan.

In the given problem, in order to calculate the largest total amount of needed to pay is A = P(1 + rt),

where P is the Principal amount of money borrowed, r is the Interest Rate R% per period, and t is the Number of Time Periods.

In this case, the total interest is calculated below.

A= P(1+rt)

A= P(1+0.05(1.5)), assume the Interest rate is monthly.

Therefore, the largest interest is 1.075% of the principal amount.
