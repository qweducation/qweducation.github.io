22. Last 25 February 2023, we celebrated the 1986 EDSA People Power Revolution. Do you think, there is a significance in celebrating the event?Why or why not?​


Answer:

significance of the 1986 EDSA People Power Revolution in the Philippines, but ultimately, whether or not it is worth celebrating is a matter of personal and societal interpretation and values.

The 1986 EDSA People Power Revolution is considered a significant event in Philippine history. It was a non-violent revolution that overthrew the 20-year dictatorship of Ferdinand Marcos and restored democracy in the Philippines. The event demonstrated the power of the people to come together and effect change, and it inspired similar movements in other parts of the world.

Celebrating the anniversary of the EDSA People Power Revolution can serve as a reminder of the importance of democracy, human rights, and people's power. It can also be an opportunity to honor the sacrifices of those who fought for democracy and to recommit to upholding democratic values and principles.

On the other hand, some may argue that the celebration of the EDSA People Power Revolution is becoming a mere ritual without actual commitment to promoting democracy and social justice. There are also debates on the effectiveness of the post-EDSA governments in addressing poverty, corruption, and other social issues.

In summary, the significance of celebrating the EDSA People Power Revolution depends on one's perspective and the degree of commitment to upholding democratic values and principles in the present and future.
