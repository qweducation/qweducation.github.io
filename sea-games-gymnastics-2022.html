5. The early olympic games featured some gymnastic disciplines which could hardly be called "________", however.


The early olympic games featured some gymnastic disciplines which could hardly be called "artistic", however. rope climbing, tumbling and club swinging were some of the events that failed to survive the refining process

Detail of explanation:

Gymnastics is said to have started in ancient Greece, when it was utilized to train for athletic events, roughly 2500 years ago. There were competitions for gymnastics that involved tumbling, rope climbing, and other related activities in the Greek city of Athens.

The ancient Olympic Games were sponsored by the Athenians because they loved these competitions. The Greeks held that only when physical exercise was combined with mental work could there be symmetry between the mind and body. Gymnastics was a very useful part of the Romans' military preparation when they invaded Greece. Gymnastics is frequently described as the pinnacle of sport and art today, yet this view is not new. Aristotle, Homer, and Plato all espoused the benefits of gymnastic exercise for strength.

Early in the 1800s, the phrase "artistic gymnastics" was developed to set free-flowing styles apart from military-style maneuvers. Gymnastics competitions started to take off in schools, sports clubs, and other organizations all over Europe in the 1880s, despite the fact that many people thought of it as a novelty activity. Gymnastics was one of the first sports added when the Olympic movement was reborn in Athens in 1896.

However, there were several gymnastic events in the early Olympics that hardly qualified as artistic. Events like rope climbing, falling, and club swinging didn't make it through the refining process. Field events including the pole vault, broad jump, and shot put even occasionally made an appearance at the World Championships, which were first held in Antwerp in 1903, up until 1954. A single swimming event took place at the 1922 championships.

28. RRS about The correlation between online games addiction and sleep disorders of students at santalan high school sy 2022-2023​


Answer:

Introduction:

In today's digital age, a growing number of students are getting hooked on online gaming. This phenomenon, coupled with studies indicating the negative impact of excessive gaming on sleep patterns, has prompted an investigation into the correlation between online gaming addiction and sleep disorders among students at Santalan High School during the 2022-2023 school year.

Review of Related Literature:

Previous studies have shown that excessive gaming can lead to sleep deprivation, both in terms of quantity and quality. This is because gaming can lead to a release of hormones and excitement, making it difficult for gamers to wind down and sleep. In addition, gaming can interfere with the circadian rhythm, tricking the brain into thinking it is daytime when it is actually nighttime. As a result, gamers often report feeling tired during the day and awake at night.

According to a study conducted by the American Psychological Association, teenagers who play violent video games for long periods of time are more likely to experience sleep problems than those who play less or not at all. This is because violent games can lead to heightened arousal, nightmares, and increased feelings of anxiety and aggression.

In another study conducted by the Journal of Sleep Research, it was found that college students who played video games for more than four hours per day were more likely to experience sleep problems, including difficulty falling asleep and low sleep quality. The study concluded that online gaming addiction is a significant risk factor for sleep disorders.

Methodology:

For this investigation, a survey will be conducted among a random sample of students at Santalan High School to measure the extent of online gaming addiction and sleep disorders among the student population. The survey will be designed to gather information about the frequency and duration of online gaming, sleep patterns, and the presence of any sleep-related problems. The data gathered from the survey will be analyzed using statistical methods to determine if there is a significant correlation between online gaming addiction and sleep disorders.

Conclusion:

The findings of this investigation can provide insights into the relationship between online gaming addiction and sleep disorders among students. By identifying the risk factors associated with excessive gaming, school administrators and parents can take steps to educate students about responsible gaming habits and promote healthy sleep patterns. Through such efforts, it may be possible to reduce the negative impact of online gaming addiction on the health and well-being of students.
