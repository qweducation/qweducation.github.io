14. why Ping Lacson and Tito Sotto gonna be a leadership team?


Answer:

We are the alternative. We’re not pro-administration. We’re not the opposition,” he said.

“We’re neutral… It’s up to the people to pick who they want, who they should vote for,” he added, speaking partly in Filipino.

Why run in 2022?

According to Sotto, their decision to run for the two top posts in government comes from their “love of country.”

“It may sound corny to others. One of the reasons is the love of country,” he said. He noted that he and Lacson had 83 years in public service combined.

Asked about their programs, Sotto said they would push for budget reform to ensure that national funds would trickle down to localities.

“There should be another system that would let the people’s money reach down and not remain with the national development so that it would develop what is called mendicancy in government,” he said.

He further pointed to the ballooning debt of the country as one of the problems the next leaders will have to address.

To sum up his and Lacson’s programs, Sotto said it would be anchored on ability, honesty, and courage — “kakayahan, katapatan, at katapangan.”

“It’s all there. It’s wrapped up as one,” he said.

“We know the problems of the country — from the pandemic to corruption. All of those we have seen. Even the solutions we have seen in the Senate,” Sotto said. “But then we’re legislators. We’re not in the executive department. We recommend [solutions]. Some are implemented, some are not.”

21. Why should we vote for Vicente Tito Sotto? List his contribution


Vicente Tito Sotto is currently serving as a Senator of the Philippines, having been elected in 1992, 1998, 2010, 2016, and 2019. He has also served as the Senate President of the Philippines from 2018 to 2020.

Throughout his career as a public official, Sotto has authored and sponsored various bills and laws that aim to address issues and concerns of the Filipinos. Some of his notable contributions include:Republic Act No. 10627 or the "Anti-Bullying Act of 2013" - This law aims to prevent and address bullying in schools by imposing penalties to individuals who commit such acts.Republic Act No. 11166 or the "Philippine HIV and AIDS Policy Act" - This law seeks to improve the quality of life of individuals living with HIV/AIDS in the Philippines by providing access to prevention, treatment, care, and support services.Republic Act No. 11313 or the "Safe Spaces Act" - This law aims to protect individuals, especially women and children, from sexual harassment and other forms of gender-based violence in public places and online platforms.Republic Act No. 11210 or the "Expanded Maternity Leave Act" - This law provides working mothers with 105 days of paid maternity leave and an option for an additional 30 days without pay.Republic Act No. 10754 or the "An Act Expanding the Benefits and Privileges of Persons with Disability" - This law provides additional benefits and privileges to persons with disabilities, such as discounts on medical and dental services, transportation, and basic necessities.

These are just some of the contributions of Senator Vicente Tito Sotto to the government and society as a public official. Ultimately, it is up to the voters to evaluate the track record and platforms of each candidate and make an informed decision during elections.

Answer:

Senate President Vicente "Tito" Sotto III took a swipe at NBI for applying the law retroactively. Tito Sotto is Vico's uncle.

"NBI will be well advised to be cautious in their interpretation of the law I principally authored. Any so-called violation of RA 11469 can't be retroactive!" the Senate president said in a tweet.
