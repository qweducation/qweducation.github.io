Macrame is an ancient art form of knotting cords or strings together to create various decorative items such as wall hangings, plant hangers, and jewelry. It has been practiced for centuries, with its origins dating back to the 13th century Arab weavers. Macrame reached its peak in popularity during the 1970s, where it was heavily used in bohemian-style home decor. Over the years, macrame has evolved with new techniques, materials, and designs, making it a versatile art form that can be used in a variety of settings.
