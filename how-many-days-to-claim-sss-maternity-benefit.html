3. halimbawa ng maternity benefits​


Answer:

Maternity Benefits

daily cash allowance granted to a female member who is unable to work due to childbirth or miscarriage/emergency termination of pregnancy

Explanation:

A daily cash allowance granted to a female member who is unable to work due to childbirth or miscarriage/emergency termination of pregnancy

Qualifying Conditions

The member has paid at least three (3) months of contributions within the 12-month period immediately before the semester of her childbirth or miscarriage/emergency termination of pregnancy

If employed, she must have given notification of her pregnancy through her employer

She must directly notify the SSS if she is separated from employment, a self-employed or voluntary member including OFW member.

Amount of Benefit

The amount of the member's daily Maternity Benefit allowance is equivalent to the Average Daily Salary Credit (ADSC)

ADSC = Ave. MSC / 180

x 60 days for miscarriage/emergency termination of pregnancy

x 105 days for live childbirth (normal or caesarean section delivery)

x 120 days for solo parent under R.A. 8972 (Solo Parents' Welfare Act of 2000)

105 days + 15 days

The Maternity Benefit is granted to a qualified female worker in every instance of pregnancy, miscarriage or emergency termination of pregnancy regardless of frequency.

17. How many digit is an sss number in the philippines?

How many digits are in SSS number? - SSS number in the Philippines is ten-digit or has ten (10) digits.

***************************more information below***************************


The Philippine Social Security System or the Paseguruhan ng Kapanatagang Panlipunan is a social insurance program in the Philippines to all Filipino workers whether they are in private, professional, and / or informal sectors.

SSS is established by the Republic Act No.1161: Social Security Act of 1954’s virtue and was amended by Republic Act No. 8282 during 1997.

It is an insurance program that is state-run.

There are in fact two programs being administered by the Philippine’s SSS: The SS Program or Social Security Program and EC or Employees' Compensation Program. The former started during the year 1975 to provide double compensation to the labor force or workers who had illnesses or those who had sudden death and / or accidents during work-related activities. The latter provides the EC benefits that are granted only to the members with superiors and employers other than themselves.


The SSS members can apply for salary or calamity loans. These salary loans are being calculated based on the member's certain salary credit every month. Meanwhile, calamity loans are for the circumstances or instances when the Philippine government declared state of calamity in the area or town where the SSS member lives. These disasters are landslides, volcano eruptions, flooding, tsunami, earthquakes, El Niño, La Niña, etc.

***********************************

Here is the Social Security System (SSS) membership requirements:

1.       must be at least 15 years old

2.       working and non-working Filipinos are all welcome

3.       to claim lifetime monthly pension, the member of SSS must be be at least 60 years old and must have at least 120 monthly contributions

DECLARATION OF POLICY OF SSS (sometimes, being memorized per word by students or applicants who wants to work for SSS. Thus, this is the SECTION 2, RA 8282)

 

"It is the policy of the State to establish, develop, promote and perfect a sound and viable tax-exempt social security system suitable to the needs of the people throughout the Philippines which shall promote social justice and provide meaningful protection to members and their beneficiaries against the hazards of disability, sickness, maternity, old age, death and other contingencies resulting in loss of income or financial burden. Toward this end, the State shall endeavor to extend social security protection to workers and their beneficiaries."
