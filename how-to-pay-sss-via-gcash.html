4. how will you compare these postulates to SAS,ASA,SSS, and AAS​


Answer:

The postulates of congruent triangles and the congruence criteria of SAS, ASA, SSS, and AAS are all related to the concept of congruent triangles. The postulates are used to prove that two triangles are congruent, while the congruence criteria are used to show that two triangles are congruent based on the given information.The postulates of congruent triangles state that:

1.Side-Side-Side (SSS) Postulate: If the three sides of one triangle are congruent to the three sides of another triangle, then the two triangles are congruent.

2.Side-Angle-Side (SAS) Postulate: If two sides and the included angle of one triangle are congruent to two sides and the included angle of another triangle, then the two triangles are congruent.

3.Angle-Side-Angle (ASA) Postulate: If two angles and the included side of one triangle are congruent to two angles and the included side of another triangle, then the two triangles are congruent.

4.Angle-Angle-Side (AAS) Congruence: If two angles and a non-included side of one triangle are congruent to two angles and the corresponding non-included side of another triangle, then the two triangles are congruent

The difference between the postulates and the congruence criteria lies in the information given. The postulates assume that all three sides or angles of two triangles are congruent, while the congruence criteria provide specific information about the sides and angles that are congruent✏️ANSWER:

***************************more information below***************************


The Philippine Social Security System or the Paseguruhan ng Kapanatagang Panlipunan is a social insurance program in the Philippines to all Filipino workers whether they are in private, professional, and / or informal sectors.

SSS is established by the Republic Act No.1161: Social Security Act of 1954’s virtue and was amended by Republic Act No. 8282 during 1997.

It is an insurance program that is state-run.

There are in fact two programs being administered by the Philippine’s SSS: The SS Program or Social Security Program and EC or Employees' Compensation Program. The former started during the year 1975 to provide double compensation to the labor force or workers who had illnesses or those who had sudden death and / or accidents during work-related activities. The latter provides the EC benefits that are granted only to the members with superiors and employers other than themselves.


The SSS members can apply for salary or calamity loans. These salary loans are being calculated based on the member's certain salary credit every month. Meanwhile, calamity loans are for the circumstances or instances when the Philippine government declared state of calamity in the area or town where the SSS member lives. These disasters are landslides, volcano eruptions, flooding, tsunami, earthquakes, El Niño, La Niña, etc.

1.       must be at least 15 years old

2.       working and non-working Filipinos are all welcome

3.       to claim lifetime monthly pension, the member of SSS must be be at least 60 years old and must have at least 120 monthly contributions

23. Anna purchased a TV worth P60 000 with a 5% discount. She agreed to pay it via installment at the end of every month for two years. The payment scheme charges 5% interest compounded bimonthly. How much is his monthly payment?​


Answer:

compute Anna's monthly payment, you first need to determine the total amount she will pay for the TV, including the interest on the installments. To do this, you need to use the formula for compounded interest:

A = P * (1 + r/n)^(nt)

Where:

A is the total amount Anna will pay

P is the original price of the TV, which is P60,000

r is the interest rate of 5% per year

n is the number of times the interest is compounded per year (in this case, bimonthly, or 2 times per year)

t is the number of years Anna will pay the installments (2 years)

Plugging these values into the formula, you get:

A = 60000 * (1 + 0.05/2)^(2*2)

A = 60000 * (1.025)^4

A = 60000 * 1.10376

A = P66,225.60

To determine the monthly payment, you can divide the total amount Anna will pay (P66,225.60) by the number of months she will pay the installments (2 years * 12 months/year = 24 months). So, Anna's monthly payment will be:

P66,225.60 / 24 months = P2,759.40 per month

Therefore, Anna's monthly payment for the TV is P2,759.40.
