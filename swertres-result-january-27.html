28. 5-27. Joi's savings in January was P6,340 and her savings in March was P5,000. What was the percentage decrease in Joi's savings? Round your answer to the nearest 1%. Solution:​


Answer:

To find the percentage decrease in Joi's savings, we need to calculate the difference between her savings in January and March and divide that by her savings in January. We can then multiply the result by 100% to express the answer as a percentage.

Step-by-step explanation:

The difference between Joi's savings in January and March is P6,340 - P5,000 = P1,340.

The percentage decrease in Joi's savings is then (P1,340 / P6,340) x 100% = 20.95%, which we can round to 21%.

Therefore, the percentage decrease in Joi's savings is 21%.
