15. Enumerate female politicians that you know and their biggest contribution in our country? ​


Female Politicians in the Philippines and their Contributions

Miriam Defensor Santiago – Born on June 15, 1945,  Miriam Defensor was famous not just in the Philippines but also internationally, she was known for her brilliance in the legal world and her brave part in fighting corruption. She was an academic, author, lawyer, judge, and politician who served in the judicial, executive, and legislative branches of the Philippine government.  She was elected as one of the judges in the ICC or the International Criminal Court, the very first Filipino and Asian to be elected, too.

Corazon Aquino – Corazon Aquino was the first female president of the Philippines, she served during 1986-1992. She was famous for restoring democracy in the Philippines after the dictatorship of Ferdinand Marcos Sr. Corazon Aquino's position as president has led to spaces for women’s movements to grow and have progressive participation between women from different classes.

Risa Hontiveros – One of the current senators in the senate, she is known for advocating for various marginalized groups, public health, and the rights of women. Some of her notable contributions are the laws she helped made and passed. One of them is the RA 11210 or the Expanded Maternity Leave Law which granted working mothers 105 days of maternity leave that is paid. She also created the first legal framework for mental health which is the RA 11036 or the Mental Health Law.

Leni Robredo – The 14th vice president of the Philippines, her office got the highest audit rating (for years 2018-2020) from the Commission on Audit. She helped author and pass bills like the Tax Incentives Management and Transparency Act. Her anti-poverty program, created during her role as vice president and now turned into an NGO or Non-government organization, Angat Buhay has helped in solving the current pressing issues of the society like education, healthcare, and rural development.

Gloria Macapagal-Arroyo – She was the second female president of the Philippines who served during the year 2001 to 2009. She was a senator first before winning the presidential elections and during her time as a senator, she has helped author many laws which include the Export Development Act and the Anti-Sexual Harassment Law.

16. who is the richest politician in the Philippines?​


Answer:

Senator Cynthia Villar

Explanation:

Sen. Villar is the only billionaire senator in the Philippines with a total net worth of P1,983, 480, 135 this year and has no liabilities. She is the wife of former Senate President, Manny Villar. She is the founder and managing director of Villar Foundation.

Her political career started in 2001 when she ran and won a landslide victory as Representative of the Lone District of Las Piñas and served that post until 2010. In 2013, she ran for senator and got the 10th place.

Las Piñas is at the second spot of the richest provinces in the Philippines along with Makati, Muntinlupa, Paranaque, Pasay, Pateros and Taguig.
