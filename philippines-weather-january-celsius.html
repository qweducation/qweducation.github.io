23. The boiling water is placed inside the mug with the boiling point of 100 degree Celsius. Every hour the temperature of the water diminished by 25 degree Celsius because the weather condition on that place is snowy.(1 Point)What is the temperature of the water in degree Celsius after 3 hours?​


Answer:

(100 – 28)/6 = 12 degrees per minute.

The proper answer to this question is:

From Newton’s Law of Cooling

T(t) = Ts + (To - Ts )e^(-kt) where

T(t) = temperature of an object at a certain time

t = time

Ts = temperature of the surroundings

To = starting temperature of the object

k = a cooling constant, specific to the object

In this case, we know To = 100, Ts = 22 (assumed room temperature),

t = 6

so T(6) = 22 + (100 – 22)e^(-6k) = 28

then e^(-6k) = 6/78 = 0.0769

-6k = ln(0.0769) = -2.565, so k = -2.565/-6 = 0.4275

So the correct answer is that the cooling rate is:

T(t) = 22 + 78e^(-2.565)

25. Chairperson of the Philippine Executive Commission established on January 23, 1942


Answer:

The first Chairperson of the Philippine Executive Commission is Jorge Vargas

Explanation:

The Philippine Executive Commission was established during World War 2. During that time, the Philippines are under the Japanese Occupation and the country has no official government to do official functions and responsibilities. The Philippine Executive Commission, headed by Jorge Vargas was instituted and tasked to forefront the assumption of government functions here in the Philippines.

Jorge Vargas was a businessman, philanthropist and a civic leader. Before becoming the chairperson of the commission, he was the acting secretary of the Department of National Defense under the Quezon administration.

29. Why do we experience cold weather from december to january every year?​


Answer:

Average daily temperatures during December range from 22°C to 32°C in Palawan, Cebu, Bohol and the southern regions and from 21°C to 30°C in Luzon and the north. The mountainous region of Cordillera experiences slightly cooler temperatures, making December a good time of year for trekking around the Banaue Rice Terraces.    

With favorable weather conditions, it’s no surprise that December is a popular time of year to visit the Philippines. If you're looking for a Christmas getaway, look no further!
