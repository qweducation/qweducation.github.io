7. 6 example of proverbs


1. Don’t put all your eggs in one basket.

Don’t put all your effort into a single course of action, venture, investment, goal, or the like, because if it doesn’t work, you lose everything.

Example: Almost entire revenue of the Company comes from the Facebook platform. If Facebook tweaks its policies in future, the Company may sink. They shouldn’t put all their eggs in the same basket.

2. Don’t put the cart before the horse.

Do things in proper order. ‘Horse before the cart’ is the proper order, and not ‘cart before the horse’.

Example: Don’t put the cart before the horse by finalizing the house you want to buy before you arrange the funds for down payment.

3. Don’t throw the baby with the bathwater.

Don’t discard something valuable while getting rid of something worthless.

Example: We shouldn’t scrap the entire project for a subpart not planned well. Let’s not throw the baby with the bathwater.

4. Early bird catches the worm.

One who starts early on the work has higher chance of success.

Example: X: Why have you come so early for the season-ending sale? Y: So that I can choose from a wider selection and get a better piece. Early bird catches the worm, after all.

5. Easy come, easy go.

You say this when you get something easily and then lose it as easily.

Example: I found fifty dollars while on my morning walk, but I frittered it away foolishly by the afternoon – easy come, easy go.

6. Empty bags cannot stand upright.

A poor or hungry person cannot discharge his duties well.

Example: You cannot expect poor people to fight for climate change, because empty bags cannot stand upright. They need to first fulfill their basic needs.

7. Every cloud has a silver lining.

Every bad or negative situation can result in some benefit to you. (The presence of silver lining means that the sun is behind the cloud and will eventually emerge.)

Example: I know your business has suffered few setbacks this season. But remember, every cloud has a silver lining

CEBU's cultural heritage and artistic talents have always provided an exceptional contribution to the visual arts industry of the nation. With this in mind, Cebuano artists bring the pride with them when they compete internationally, further spreading the creativity of the people of Cebu.

SM City Cebu hosted a tribute for artists, hoping to create a platform to support the growth of Cebuano artists. From Feb. 27-29, an art exhibit entitled Art For Everyone – A Glimpse to Cebuano Art displayed the works of both the veteran visual artists and budding artists of this new generation who are starting to bloom in this industry of beauty and meaning. Various artists, art collectors, curators and art enthusiasts graced the exhibit displayed in The Northwing Atrium.

Featuring an exclusive selection of paintings and artworks curated by Celso Duazo Pepito, an acclaimed Cebuano artist known in both the local and the international scene of visual arts, the exhibit displayed all kinds of different artworks in different mediums with diverse techniques and signature styles showing the evolution of Cebu’s artistry, from the traditional to the modern and contemporary arts, striking conversations and providing a glimpse into the history and culture of Cebu.

With the evolution of Cebuano art, the exhibit is SM City Cebu’s contribution to help attain Cebu’s goal in promoting the importance of Cebuano artistry. The exhibit hoped to send a message to the viewers to continue appreciating Cebu art, which in turn, will be able to provide support and more opportunities for talented Cebuano artists


28. What is the definition of a proverb??? What are the example of proverbs????


A proverb is a simple and concrete saying, popularly known and repeated, that expresses a truth based on common sense or experience. They are often metaphorical. A proverb that describes a basic rule of conduct may also be known as a maxim. Proverbs fall into the category of formulaic language.
Examples:
Well begun is half done.
Honesty is the best policy.
