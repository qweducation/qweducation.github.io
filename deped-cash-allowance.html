17. Agree or Disagree? Explain briefly your stand or side on the issue of DepEd allowing distance learning amid extreme heat during summer months. ​


AGREE: ALLOWING DISTANCE LEARNING AMID EXTREME HEAT

The COVID-19 pandemic has made distance learning a new trend in the educational setting and many students and teachers have found it to be difficult. The Department of Education (DepEd) has developed several remote learning strategies in the Philippines, including online courses, modular training, and instruction by radio and television to continue education despite what happened.

Climate change penetrates more in the summertime. Students and teachers who study and teach inside the classroom may find it difficult to adjust to the summer's intense heat.

High humidity and temperatures can contribute to pain, dehydration, and other health problems that could hinder instruction and learning. Due to lack of ventilation and the structure of the classroom is not designed for this kind of problem.

The DepEd has taken several steps to reduce the effect of the high heat. These include scheduling lessons to take place during cooler times of the day, offering advice on proper ventilation and hydration, and encouraging both students and teachers to take breaks and rest when necessary. But it's up to the principal to take action since the DepEd releases a memorandum to change some matters.

In conclusion, the DepEd has put in place mechanisms to solve this problem, despite the fact that the intense heat of the summer might be difficult for distant learning.

Prioritizing student and teacher health and safety is crucial, as is making sure that education is maintained despite the pandemic.
