13. What is upper class? *Composed of people who hold the highest social status, usually are the wealthiest members of class society, and wield the greatest political power.


Upper class in modern societies is the social class composed of people who hold the highest social status, usually are the wealthiest members of class society, and wield the greatest political power.[1] According to this view, the upper class is generally distinguished by immense wealth which is passed on from generation to generation.[2] Prior to the 20th century, the emphasis was on aristocracy, which emphasized generations of inherited noble status, not just recent wealth.[3]

Because the upper classes of a society may no longer rule the society in which they are living, they are often referred to as the old upper classes, and they are often culturally distinct from the newly rich middle classes that tend to dominate public life in modern social democracies. According to the latter view held by the traditional upper classes, no amount of individual wealth or fame would make a person from an undistinguished background into a member of the upper class as one must be born into a family of that class and raised in a particular manner to understand and share upper class values, traditions, and cultural norms. The term is often used in conjunction with terms like upper-middle class, middle class, and working class as part of a model of social stratification.

19. If you were the wealthiest man in the world, what will you do with your riches?


Answer:

If I were the richest person in the world (which means I'd be worth about $54+ billion), I would hire worldwide experts to oversee funding in areas such as education, innovation, the environment, health care and human rights. I would allocate an annual budget to each expert to essentially run a non-profit (with employees) built on the idea of providing experimental, progressive solutions to every day problems that won't be correctly fixed by the government but could be produced, communicated and marketed to individuals in societies across the globe by a non-partisan believer in human prosperity. I realize this is a huge undertaking and is quite vague, but being the richest doesn't make me the smartest, so I will rely on the world's best to do some of the best things for the world.

Some items for more personal enjoyment:

I would buy the first, second and third row seats to top artists' concerts and give them away for free to the BIGGEST fans. I've always found that concert tickets are priced in such a way that only the rich can get the up close and personal experience, or they require that someone who really loves the artist(s) performing make a huge financial sacrifice to get that once-in-a-lifetime opportunity. I think the biggest fans deserve the best seats and therefore, through a really fun application process, I would give those fans a chance to get the seats they always wanted but could never have afforded. Rich people who may also be biggest fans would also have to apply for those seats. If they weren't selected, the best seat they could get would be the fourth row.

I would pay my immediate family and friends to do what they love. The only requirement would be that what they love actually benefit others in some way or create a good that others may want/need.

I would buy huge plots of land to simply protect them from being developed into anything but nature. I'd hire someone to find a better use for the land, like a massive community garden or simply a wildflower forest or whatever would fit best and benefit people without becoming concrete jungle.

I would also use my financial power to interview some of my idols. Having the money to buy time with some of the people I'm most fascinated with would be awesome. I'd love to pick their brains, learn more about them and sufficiently geek out.

Of course I'd travel a lot and want to live a luxurious life, but I don't think it's possible to know what to do with so much money and would try my best to make it money that counted rather than was just spent.
